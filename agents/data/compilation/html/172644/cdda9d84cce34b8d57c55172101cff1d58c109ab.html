Faculty resources at-a-glance

Faculty members at Wayne State University are vitally important to achieving the goals and mission of the university. With that in mind, it is expected that every faculty member will engage in teaching, service, and scholarship/professional development. This website will help provide resources and guidance in those areas, as well as suggestions on how to navigate the university to ensure each faculty member is successful.

Teaching resources

Teaching is a core activity of the university, and all faculty members are expected to achieve excellence in this role.  Teaching includes, but is not limited to, traditional classroom, laboratory, and studio instruction, online or hybrid instruction, and clinical training. 

  • Office for Teaching and Learning
  • Canvas Support
  • Online programs (professional development, teaching resources)
  • Writing a Statement of Teaching Philosophy for the Academic Job Search
  • Student Disability Services - a resource to ensure academic access and inclusion for students
  • Counseling & Psychological Services (CAPS) - providing free and confidential mental health assessment to currently registered students
  • Library Support for Faculty - resources to support your teaching, like media and course reserves
  • Student Support & Intervention (CARE referral, Campus Health Center, police, advising)
  • Office of Sexual Violence Prevention and Education
  • Anti-Bullying Statement - University Statement of Values to address bullying incidents
  • Bookstore / Book Adoption
  • Office of the Registrar resources - Including the Academic calendar and instructions for Midterm grades
  • Teach Anywhere - The Teach Anywhere Team is a collaboration between the OTL and C&IT with additional support from the Libraries. Their goal is to support faculty in making decisions and taking action to transition their courses to online or remote formats as smoothly as possible.
  • Assist Anywhere - The Assist Anywhere Team is a collaboration between academic staff and administrators across campus. Their shared goal is to support academic staff in making decisions and taking action to transition their duties to online or remote formats as smoothly as possible.
