Rethinking Assessment Strategies in the Age of AI

From CAT Base
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Originally presented for CAT+FD on 19 February 2025 by Ms. Janice Florent and Dr. Jason Todd


Traditional assessment methods often fall short in capturing students’ true abilities and fostering authentic learning experiences, and the introduction of AI (artificial intelligence) tools further complicates the task of accurately assessing genuine student learning.

As artificial intelligence continues to reshape the educational landscape, it's crucial to reimagine traditional assessment strategies. This workshop will delve into innovative approaches to assessment that are both engaging and rigorous in the age of AI. We will explore how instructors can design assessments that promote critical thinking, problem-solving, and creativity, while also mitigating the potential risks associated with AI-powered tools.

Workshop Objectives:

  • Understand the impact of AI on traditional assessment methods
  • Explore strategies to promote authentic learning and critical thinking
  • Learn how to leverage Brightspace's Assignments tool to simplify assignment submission, evaluation, and feedback
  • Learn how to utilize Brightspace's Rubrics tool to streamline assignment evaluation and feedback

This workshop, the 21st in our #LEX Advanced series, builds on the skills you learned in the #LearnEverywhereXULA course AND is a must-attend for any faculty member who wants to learn about designing alternative/authentic assessments that challenge students to apply their knowledge and skills in meaningful ways in this age of AI. Register today and start using alternative/authentic assessments to transform your teaching and learning!
