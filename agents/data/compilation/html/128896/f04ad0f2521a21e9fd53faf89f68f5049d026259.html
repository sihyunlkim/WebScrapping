Accreditation

 

Accreditation is a voluntary process recognizing educational programs and institutions for achieving and maintaining quality, performance, and integrity that meet meaningful standards. When a school decides to become accredited, it conducts an in-depth study of the quality of its education, administrative practices, curriculum, student affairs, faculty competence, and relationships with employers. Only after an on-site visit and commission review can the program and school be accredited.
