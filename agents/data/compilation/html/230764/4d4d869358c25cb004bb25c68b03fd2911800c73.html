Reimagining Education: Help Lead the Future of Teaching and Learning with AI at the University of Utah

October 16, 2025

The University of Utah One-U Responsible Artificial Intelligence Initiative (One-U RAI) at the Scientific Computing and Imaging (SCI) Institute is hiring a transformative cluster of experts focused on the future of education empowered by AI. Two new tenure-track faculty positions—centered on AI and emerging technologies in teaching and learning and workforce development and AI literacy—are part of an initiative to reimagine how people prepare for and succeed in an AI-driven world.

From designing responsible AI–powered learning tools to expanding digital literacy and credential opportunities, these roles offer a chance to connect pedagogy, technology, and societal impact. They mark the beginning of a strategic multi-year investment in advancing responsible AI for education. Selected faculty will collaborate across campus and beyond to drive innovation, establishing the university as a hub for the thoughtful integration of AI into teaching, learning, and workforce development.

“We’re not just preparing students for the future. We’re preparing educators, institutions, and communities to thrive in it,” said Chenglu Li, assistant professor in the College of Education, co-chair of the search committee, and a One-U RAI faculty fellow. “This cluster will build capacity for responsible, accessible, and forward-looking AI use across the educational ecosystem.”

What Makes These Roles Unique?

  • Interdisciplinary Reach: Bridge fields, including education, computer science, engineering, psychology, sciences, mathematics, business, architecture, design, humanities, social sciences, and more, to maximize the impact of your research and teaching. Collaborate across departments and with external partners to build scalable AI initiatives that reflect institutional and societal needs. The university’s collaborative ecosystem includes the SCI Institute, Martha Bradley Evans Center for Teaching Excellence, Utah Education Policy Center, and more.
  • Research Impact: Tackle real-world challenges like AI literacy, workforce upskilling, and faculty and curriculum development. Contribute to Utah’s economic and educational ecosystem through applied research and community engagement.
  • Pedagogical Innovation: Design and evaluate AI-powered learning tools, develop stackable certificates and micro-credentials, and lead faculty development programs that expand institutional AI capacity.
  • Ethical AI Leadership: Contribute to One-U RAI, which prioritizes transparency and community-centered design of AI systems in education.

“Responsible AI in education demands purposeful research design and a commitment to meaningful learning,” said Rob MacLeod, professor in the Department of Biomedical Engineering and deputy director of the SCI Institute. “We’re seeking faculty who can lead with insight and imagination, using pedagogical research to shape how AI supports educators and learners alike.”

  • Rank: Assistant or Associate Professor

Why the University of Utah?

Located in the bustling urban landscape of Salt Lake City, the University of Utah stands as the state's flagship institution and the region's hub of higher education. Also known as “the U,” it’s a Tier 1 research university, a member of the exclusive Association of American Universities, and home to the state's only academic medical center. Driven by a goal to become a top 10 public university with unsurpassed societal impact, the U is committed to making social, economic and cultural contributions that improve the quality of life throughout the state, the nation and the world.

Ranked as the best state in America by U.S. News & World Report, Utah has a robust economy, high-tech sector, vibrant urban area, and abundant recreation opportunities. In the foothills above campus are miles of pristine hiking and biking trails. Within a 30-minute drive are seven world-renowned ski resorts and within five hours you can visit any of Utah's five breathtaking national parks.
