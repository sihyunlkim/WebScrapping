Accessing ChatGPT Edu

  • Request access via University IT’s Service Catalog. 
    • Within 24 hours, you will receive an invitation email from OpenAI. 
    • Please check your spam/junk folder if you don’t see it. 
  • Accept the invitation and create your account using your uNID@umail.utah.edu email address:  
    (Example: u1234567@umail.utah.edu)  
  • Click log in and select “University of Utah ChatGPT Edu”  
  • Log in using University of Utah SSO:  
    • Enter your uNID and password  
    • Complete Duo two-factor authentication  
  • After successful authentication, you will be redirected back to ChatGPT and can begin using the service  
  • Once your account is set up, access ChatGPT Edu any time at https://chatgpt.com  

 

Important Note: 

  • Confirm you’re signed into ChatGPT Edu: 

Once logged in, look at the bottom left corner of the ChatGPT Edu interface. You should see “University of Utah ChatGPT Edu” listed under your profile, which confirms you are using the university-managed environment. 

Guidelines

  • For all university-related tasks, use your ChatGPT Edu instance rather than a personal ChatGPT account. This ensures you have access to the university’s higher data privacy standards and academic-focused optimizations—helping you get the most out of ChatGPT while keeping your work secure and aligned with university guidelines.
  • ChatGPT Edu can be used for any work that doesn’t contain sensitive or restricted data, as defined by university rules. Hospital and clinic personnel don’t yet have permission to use the tool—access for these environments is being evaluated separately to meet health-care data protection requirements.
  • Review university AI guidelines and policies for more information on how to use AI responsibly and effectively.

Training Resources

  • Chat GPT Edu 101 Training:
  • ChatGPT Edu 102 Training (Friday, February 20, 12 PM): is an intermediate, hands-on training session cohosted with OpenAI that explores practical, university-wide use cases for ChatGPT Edu. The session will cover advanced features, research and teaching workflows, data handling and privacy considerations, and best practices for effective and responsible use.
  • Getting Started with ChatGPT Edu (Fridays at 11:00 AM): Learn how to access ChatGPT Edu, navigate the interface, and write effective prompts through guided examples. The training session includes appropriate data use and practical applications such as creating slide decks, lesson plans, and analyzing data.
  • AI Office Hours (every other Friday at noon): Drop in to brainstorm use cases, discuss best practices, get guidance, find collaborators and share ideas for new tools. 
  • AI Tinker Lab (Wednesdays at 1 p.m.): A weekly drop‑in space for students to explore AI through hands-on experimentation, creativity, and community. 
  • Team members who completed the Train the Trainer  program are leading training sessions in their departments. Reach out locally to learn about schedules. 
  • View sample prompts for students and faculty.
