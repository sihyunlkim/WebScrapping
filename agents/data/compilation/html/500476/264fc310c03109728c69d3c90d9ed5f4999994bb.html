Additional Context

Arizona State University has no explicit AI policy for undergraduate admissions essays (no essay required for general admission) but developing comprehensive framework. Law school uniquely allows AI assistance for applications while emphasizing authenticity. Academic integrity policy prohibits unauthorized AI use with case-by-case handling until formal policy developed. Emphasizes faculty discretion and clear syllabus expectations. AI detection tools not recommended due to unreliability.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Arizona State University allow ChatGPT for essays?
Arizona State University has no explicit AI policy for admissions essays. While not explicitly prohibited, the lack of clear guidance means students should be cautious.
Do I need to disclose AI use to Arizona State University?
Yes, Arizona State University requires disclosure of any AI assistance. You must indicate what AI tools were used and how they helped.
How does Arizona State University check for AI?
Arizona State University may conduct manual reviews to check for AI use, looking for inconsistencies in voice or style.
Which Arizona State University programs have different AI policies?
Sandra Day O'Connor College of Law, Barrett Honors College have specific policies that differ from the general university policy.
