Office of the Registrar FAQs

When are the class schedules usually available?

The Class Schedules are usually available for the Fall and Summer Semesters in mid-March and Spring Semester in mid-October. They can be found in the Schedule of Classes link to the left or via our online Cavalier Center.

When may I register for classes?

Students are allowed to register by an Appointment Time Schedule according to total number of earned hours. Appointments can be found under the Registrar Appointments.

How do I change my major?

Complete the Change of Primary Major. After the Office of the Registrar processes the form, the student will receive an email confirmation.

Who is my advisor?

Advisors are listed in the Cavalier Center. Log into the Cavalier Center. Once logged in, you will click "Office of the Registrar" - "Academic Records" - "General Student Information."

How do I contact my advisor?

Faculty and staff may be reached by calling the switchboard at 330.499.7090 or by the Walsh University e-mail. Example: jdoe@walsh.edu.

How much are tuition and fees?

Tuition is based on the number of hours in which a student is enrolled, student level undergraduate, graduate), and residence status. Fees are assessed according to the enrolled number of hours. Please consult your Class Schedule or speak with a Financial Aid Advisor located in the Student Service Center in Farrell Hall for additional information.

What is the Withdrawal Policy?

Registration for classes creates a contract for payment of tuition, fees, and charges.

A student choosing to terminate this contract with Walsh University must officially withdraw during the first 5 business days of any 8 week session or are obligated to pay all session charges in full. A student who wishes to withdraw from Walsh University, thereby discontinuing enrollment, will need to complete the University Withdrawal form online. University withdrawal is not official until this form is submitted by the student and processed by the Office of the Registrar. Nonattendance to class or notification to a professor does not constitute an official withdrawal.

Withdrawal from a class or from the University on or before the fifth business day of a session will cancel all financial obligations to the University for that session.

Sessions:

  • Fall I and Fall II
  • Spring I and Spring II
  • Summer I and Summer II 

Five (5) business days or earlier: 100% of tuition **

Six (6) business days or later: No Refund 

** Due to the block tuition billing method, undergraduate students should contact the Student Service Center to determine how financial aid may be effected by withdrawing from a class, session or the university. Based on how the block tuition is divided between the two semester sessions will impact the amount of the tuition refund. No refunds will be issued for completed classes.

How do I order an official transcript?

Walsh University has officially partnered with the National Student Clearinghouse, and through this website, you can order your official transcript.

If any of your coursework was completed prior to Fall 2000, you will need to contact the Office of the Registrar directly. This system will not retrieve any academic records prior to Fall 2000. Please see "Option 2: Manual Transcript Request" below.

Transcript Processing

  • Transcript orders are typically processed within 1-2 business days.
  • Currently, enrolled students should ensure all final semester grades are posted before ordering. Please review the Grades and Transcript Processing Dates Calendars and Deadlines for information on important dates to keep in mind.
  • Walsh University will process requests as they are received unless a "transcript hold" is designated.
  • If you have a University hold on your account, the order will not be processed.
  • Please refer to the Walsh University Academic Calendarfor holidays and closures. During these times, transcript orders may not be processed.

Option 1: Order Your Transcript Online (Recommended)

The online transcript ordering option allows you to order transcripts 24/7 regardless of your location. Transcripts ordered through the National Student Clearinghouse are $15 regardless of being sent electronically or by mail.

Option 2: Manual Transcript Request (Paper Form)

If you attended prior to the year 2000 or do not have a credit card to order a transcript online, you may fill out the paper request form by following the steps below.

Stop into the Student Service Center (FH 101) and complete a Transcript Request Form (PDF).Please note we do not have an option to send transcripts electronically when ordered via this method.Transcripts ordered in person cost $15 and can be paid with cash, check, or card. Checks can be made out to Walsh University.

Or

Download and complete a Transcript Request Form (PDF) and mail it in with cash or check payment for processing (see mailing information on the form). Please note we do not have an option to send transcripts electronically when ordered via this method.

Please include the following information with your written request:

  • Address
  • Date-of-Birth
  • Social Security number
  • Destination of the transcript
  • Date of request
  • Include $15.00 per official copy ordered

Ordering your transcript via mail can cause a delay of several days and a week or more during peak volume times. The quickest method for requesting and receiving your transcript is using the online method mentioned above. If you are experiencing problems when using the online option, please contact our office for assistance before making a request via mail.

*Students with balances will need to call the Student Service Center for further information.
