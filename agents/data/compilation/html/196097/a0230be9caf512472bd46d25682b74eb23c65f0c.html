Resources

  • With funding from the SUNY Empire Innovation Program, the State of New York and private donations, the AI Innovation Institute focuses on four grand challenges (health care, infrastructure, education, and finance) and five foundational research areas (automated and scalable knowledge acquisition, predictive intelligence, explainable AI, trustworthy AI, and ethical AI).  Building on the strength of College of Engineering and Applied Sciences, the AI Innovation Institute currently comprises ten core faculty drawn from Computer Science, Biomedical Informatics, Applied Mathematics, and Electrical Engineering.  Another two dozen faculty from around the University with interests in AI and applications serve as affiliate faculty.

  • Stony Brook University hosts AI tools for use by students, faculty, and staff. 

    Tools include:

    • Microsoft Copilot
    • Google Gemini
    • Google NotebookLM
    • Turnitin
    • Zoom AI Companion

    These tools are approved for use with Stony Brook University data, but they are not to be used with HIPAA data. Use of other AI tools has potential to improperly disclose University data to train models and may appear in chats with people who should not have access to University data.

  • Check out CELT's resources to find out more information about AI in learning and teaching and help you make the best decisions about the use of AI in your courses and to learn more about these new tools and technologies. If you would like an individual consultation, email celt@stonybrook.edu.
  • Check  out Artificial Intelligence (AI) Writing and Generative Resources through the University Libraries.
