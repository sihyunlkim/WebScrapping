Penn Generative AI Tools & Resources

Generative AI will revolutionize the teaching, research, and business of Penn.

ISC manages a range of Generative AI tools for campus with negotiated enterprise contracts including improved security, privacy, and cost. 

The following table provides information on available tools, costs, and data protections.  For all of these tools, users must be logged into a University account for appropriate protections.  Personal accounts on these services are not protected.  

For product inquiries, please contact your IT Support Provider.
 

ToolsCapabilitiesCost per userPenn Data Risk Classification
Microsoft Copilot Chat*Full-range generative AI chat client, running on ChatGPT, that provides web search enhanced with generative AI to create novel content (text, images).
FAQs can be found here.		No additional charge over existing Microsoft 365 agreements*. Copilot Chat is self-service and available at this site. Low, Moderate, and most High Risk Data, except SSNs and credit card data. Additional Guidance.
 
 
Microsoft 365 Copilot*Full-Range; business tools integrated Integrates generative AI running on ChatGPT into the Microsoft Office applications—Excel, OneNote, Outlook, PowerPoint, Teams, and Word. It also includes a Work search that allows queries across all of those applications.Available for faculty and staff at $31 per month; please contact your IT Support Provider. Low, Moderate, and most High Risk Data, except SSNs, and credit card data. Additional Guidance.
 
 

Gemini for Google Workspace

Full-Range; business tools integrated Integrates generative AI into the Google Workspace. The tool is designed to streamline routine tasks and enhance productivity. Available for some faculty and staff at $20 or $30 per month only at the following schools: ASC, SEAS, and Wharton; please contact your IT Support Provider. Low and Moderate Risk Data. Do not input sensitive data including HIPAA data, personal health information (PHI), financial account information, SSNs, and credit card data. Additional Guidance.
Google NotebookLMAI Research Assistant A research and note-taking assistant that focuses responses solely on your uploaded content, greatly reducing the risk of errors  (‘hallucinations’).  It can output text and audio. Available for some faculty and staff only at the following schools: ASC, SEAS, and Wharton; please contact your IT Support Provider. Low and Moderate Risk Data. Do not input sensitive data including HIPAA data, personal health information (PHI), financial account information, SSNs, and credit card data. Additional Guidance.

Grammarly

 

Writing-centric tool that provides writing assistance, expanding beyond grammar and spell-check with options including AI-assisted outlining, citation, and revision. Beginning January 2026, ISC will offer Grammarly for Education licenses as a paid service to Penn affiliates at $87/year with an annual commitment; please contact your IT Support Provider. Low, Moderate, and most High Risk Data, except SSNs and credit card data.

 
ChatGPT EduFull-range generative AI chat client (text, images, audio).
FAQs can be found here.		Available for faculty and staff at $13 per month; please contact your IT Support Provider. Students may have access when required in course or research experience, and paid by the School, department, or instructor.Low and Moderate Risk Data. Do not input sensitive data including HIPAA data, personal health information (PHI), financial account information, SSNs, and credit card data. Additional Guidance.
 

Updated June 2025.  *Some Microsoft offerings are managed by individual Schools.

There are also numerous tools ISC administers that have AI plug-ins such as Box and Zoom AI Companion. For more information about those plugins, please refer to the the base page for that service.

Many other AI tools are available to the public but are less secure. Please follow Penn’s Guidance for Use of Generative AI.
