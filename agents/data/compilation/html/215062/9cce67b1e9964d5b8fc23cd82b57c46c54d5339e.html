Teaching in the Age of AI: Faculty Share Their Strategies

  • Home
  • News & Features
  • CETLI News
  • Teaching in the Age of AI: Faculty Share Their Strategies

25Aug25

CETLI News, News & Features
  • Erin Bartnett
  • 0 Comment

August 25, 2025

Generative AI is rapidly changing the teaching and learning landscape. To help you consider how you might approach Generative AI in your courses this semester, CETLI has connected with faculty to learn about how they are approaching the role of Generative AI in their classrooms through syllabus language and assignment design.

For one-on-one support for all of your teaching goals, CETLI staff are available to consult with youat any point in the semester.

Faculty Insights

  • Faculty share examples of their AI Policies Setting clear guidelines for AI use on individual courses or assignments enables students to focus on the learning objectives you want them to achieve.
  • AI Skeptics Approach Assignment Design in the Age of AI Cam Grey of Classics and Joshua Mosley of Design share how their discussions with colleagues helped them develop assignments to explore ethical relationships to AI use in the classroom.
  • Using Generative AI Tools to Accelerate Learning Masao Sako and Bhuvnesh Jain of Physics share how they have used Generative AI in both independent and group work to accelerate learning in STEM courses.
  • Helping Students Develop AI Literacy - Amanda Bettencourt of Nursing, Simon Richter of FIGS, and Ann Kuttner of Art History have experimented with assignments that help students develop AI literacy by interrogating the quality of the output they receive from various generative AI tools.

Additional Resources

For more resources on assignment design, Penn-approved AI tools, and more, visit CETLIs pages on Generative AI & Your Teaching.

To attend one of CETLI's events centered on timely teaching topics, including generative AI, visit CETLI's events page.
