Teaching & Learning AI Digest November 2024


Vol. 2, Issue 4 | November 2024




Welcome to November’s issue of the Teaching and Learning AI Digest. Below are exciting stories about the evolution of AI in our classrooms, labs, campus community and around the world, all curated by the team at Purdue University’s Innovation Hub.

 

Purdue’s powerful new LLM “Purdue GenAI Studio” available campus-wide

In collaboration with the Institute for Physical AI (IPAI), Purdue University’s Rosen Center for Advanced Computing (RCAC) now offers a powerful new feature in its artificial intelligence software services—Purdue GenAI Studio. This large language model (LLM) service makes open-source LLM models like LLaMA accessible to anyone at Purdue.
Learn More
 

“The Art of ChatGPT Interactions” course free for staff and students

Want to use ChatGPT but need some expert help? Sage Campus offers a free course for Purdue staff and students to build skills and confidence in using generative AI tools like ChatGPT. Under the guidance of AI expert Leo Lo, you’ll master the art of prompt engineering, deepen your understanding of how ChatGPT works, and learn how a systematic approach can help you get better outcomes.
Learn More

Students design “a game about AI using AI”


The Purdue graduate students recently launched “Transforming AI for Learning” (TRAIL), an alternate reality game that uses AI tools to educate players through an interactive mystery-solving game.
