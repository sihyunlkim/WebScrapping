Computer science department to host ChatGPT workshop

Submitted by Computer Science Office

The computer science department in the Carl R. Ice College of Engineering will host a workshop on practical uses for ChatGPT from 9 a.m. to noon on Friday, July 19, in Room 1107 of Fiedler Hall.

Titled "The Practical Applications of ChatGPT Workshop," the event aims to demystify artificial intelligence through an interactive seminar that walks through day-to-day, practical uses for ChatGPT, including creating training programs and marketing materials, planning events, writing emails and full business plans, or connecting with customers more effectively. While integrating these applications can seem daunting, it's just a matter of understanding how to use this virtual tool to maximize its value to organizations.

Troy Tabor, director of the Center for Entrepreneurship at Wichita State University, will give the presentation.

Space is limited for this free workshop. Register to attend today.
