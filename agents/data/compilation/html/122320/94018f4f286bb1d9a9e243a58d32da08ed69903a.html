Using AI Ethically: A Student Guide

Artificial intelligence (AI), computer tools that can analyze information, recognize patterns, and generate responses based on large collections of data, can be a valuable tool for learning, brainstorming, and problem-solving.

This guide outlines how to use AI responsibly while maintaining academic integrity and ensuring your work reflects your own learning.

1. Follow Assignment-Specific AI Guidelines

Your instructor may set AI policies for specific assignments or apply a general guideline across the entire course.

  • Different assignments may allow different levels of AI use.
  • Check your syllabus or assignment instructions for specific rules.
  • If you are unsure whether AI use is permitted, ask your instructor before using it.

2. Use AI as a Support, Not a Substitute

  • AI can help you generate ideas, explore topics, or organize information.
  • You are responsible for evaluating its output and making all final decisions.
  • Always think critically! AI can make mistakes, provide incomplete information, or misrepresent facts.

3. Engage Critically and Carefully

  • Review AI-generated content for accuracy, bias, and reliability.
  • Consider ethical implications of AI suggestions or solutions.
  • Avoid entering personal, confidential, or sensitive information into AI tools.

4. Be Transparent About AI Use

  • If AI contributes to an assignment, clearly document and explain its role.
  • Provide proper citations according to course or disciplinary standards (e.g., APA, MLA).
  • Remember: you are fully responsible for the quality, originality, and accuracy of your work.
  • Violations may be subject to the academic integrity policy

5. Maintain Academic Integrity

  • Do not submit AI-generated work as if it were entirely your own.
  • Avoid using AI to bypass learning objectives, such as solving problems you are expected to work through manually.
  • Treat AI as a tool to enhance your understanding, not to replace your learning.

6. Best Practices for Ethical AI Use

  • Use AI for brainstorming, drafting, summarizing, or clarifying ideas, not for writing your entire assignment.
  • Keep a record of your process, including notes, drafts, and sources.
  • Reflect on how AI influenced your work and include this in your submission if required.

Remember

AI is a powerful tool, but your learning and integrity matter most. Using AI ethically means enhancing your skills without compromising your responsibility for your work.
