Introduction

Welcome to the Generative AI Resources library guide for faculty! This guide provides connections to resources and information on utilizing AI at SCU.

This guide is meant to provide suggestions and additional clarifications around topics about AI in academia and on campus. The content in this guide is not intended to replace the guidance from Academic Technology or the Faculty Collaborative for Teaching Innovation. 

No Campus AI Policy

Currently at Santa Clara University, there is no campus policy on how artificial intelligence should/should not be utilized. The campus is allowing faculty members the autonomy to decide how AI tools can be utilized in their classrooms. 

AI Resources at SCU

  • Academic Integrity of AI (AI^2)
    The mission behind AI^2 is to explore the application of artificial intelligence in student learning and research. We gather and distribute ideas, examples, and other resources related to this emerging topic.
  • AI Collaborate
    Aims to empower students to make a significant impact with artificial intelligence by offering comprehensive training in AI skills at all levels—introductory, intermediate, and advanced.
  • Responsible AI
    Mission is to foster interdisciplinary research, promote ethical AI education, and cultivate partnerships that ensure AI advances are safe, equitable, transparent, and beneficial to the broader good of society.
  • SCU Generative AI Guidelines
    These guidelines for using Generative AI are based on SCU’s current technology policies and standards and are updated periodically.
