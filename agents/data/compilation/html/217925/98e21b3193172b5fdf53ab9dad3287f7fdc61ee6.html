Board of Trustees

Dr. William H. Jones

President

Dr. Bill Jones currently serves as the eighth president of Columbia International University, having previously held the position as its sixth president. During his first term, CIU experienced remarkable growth and transformation. Under his leadership, CIU launched its first fully online degree program, established the Kepha (Chinese Initiative) program, and secured full accreditation for the Prison Initiative. Dr. Jones was also instrumental in founding CIU’s intercollegiate athletics program, building the Pine View apartments, and founding the School of Business.

A milestone in the history of CIU’s K–12 school, Ben Lippen, was achieved during Dr. Jones’ leadership with the appointment of the first Ben Lippen parent as the board chair, a historic move after 80 years. Additionally, his strategic leadership resulted in a significant increase in CIU’s endowment, growing from $12 million to $43 million.

Beyond his work at CIU, Dr. Jones is passionate about spreading God’s love and forgiveness. In 1987, he co-founded Crossover Global, a mission organization that has planted over 4,000 churches among unreached populations worldwide. He also has a strong ministry presence with marketplace leaders, including state and federal government officials. His dedication to service was recognized in 2019 when he received the Order of the Palmetto, South Carolina’s highest honor, and the Key to the City of Columbia from Mayor Steve Benjamin in 2017.

Dr. Jones holds a Bachelor of Science degree from the Georgia Institute of Technology and a Certificate in Theology from Western Conservative Baptist Seminary. He earned dual Doctor of Ministry degrees from Columbia Biblical Seminary & Graduate School of Missions and Gordon-Conwell Seminary.

He and his wife, Debby, who holds a master’s in Christian Education from CIU, have four married children. Together, they continue to serve in ministry and contribute to the vision of CIU.

Dr. James Lanpher

Executive Vice President and Provost

james.lanpher@ciu.edu | (803) 807-5027

Biography

Dr. James Lanpher is a leader in biblical higher education. He has served in a variety of capacities with the Association for Biblical Higher Education (ABHE), taught overseas at the Alliance Biblical Seminary (now the Alliance Graduate School) in Manila, The Philippines, and has also taught at the University of Notre Dame and Crown College. He is also former Vice President for Academic Affairs at Trinity College of Florida.

Dr. Lanpher graduated cum laude from the Georgetown University School of Foreign Service in Washington, D.C. with a Bachelor of Science in Foreign Service degree majoring in international economics and obtaining language proficiency in Chinese. He received his Master of Divinity from Alliance Theological Seminary in Nyack, New York, and his Doctor of Philosophy degree in Christianity and Judaism in Antiquity from the University of Notre Dame in South Bend, Indiana. His dissertation, “The Miraculous in Mark: Its Eschatological Background and Christological Function,” focused on the role of miracles in Mark’s portrayal and understanding of Jesus.

Dr. Michael Johnson

Senior Vice President

michael.johnson@ciu.edu | (803) 807-5039

Biography

Michael Johnson joined the Columbia International University faculty in 2022. From 2016–2022 he served as Alaska’s Commissioner of Education. Before becoming Alaska’s Commissioner, Dr. Johnson served in the Copper River School District as superintendent, school principal, district curriculum and staff development director, elementary teacher, and special education program assistant.

Michael is daily privileged in marriage to Beth. Together they are blessed with two wonderful children — Noah, and Katie. As a family, they enjoy traveling, music, reading, visiting with extended family and friends, games, and chillaxing together.

Diane Mull

Senior Vice President of Advancement, Alumni, University Communications and Gift Receipting/Research

diane.mull@ciu.edu | (803) 807-5502

Biography

Diane Mull has served on CIU’s Advancement team for over a decade, as well as at CIU and other institutions as the assistant director of Alumni Relations, director of the Annual Fund, executive director of Advancement and director of Philanthropy. She is also a recipient of the Council for Advancement and Support of Education award.

She received her degree in Communications and Business Administration from Messiah University where she assisted in creating the University’s first Senior Gift advancement initiative as well as the first Phonathon and Parents Fund.    

For over 20 years Diane has led Bible studies with international scholars studying in the United States, with community and church women’s ministries, and in Africa with women in leadership. 

At CIU, she served as the Wives Fellowship president while enrolled with her husband Andy in CIU’s graduate school. Diane and Andy have two daughters and sons in law, Laura and Mark, and Liz and Ed and 6 grandchildren.

Dr. David Sayers

Chief Financial Officer

david.sayers@ciu.edu

Biography

Dr. David Sayers has served over 14 years as a “CFO for hire” assisting mostly nonprofit organizations with CFO expertise. He also served for nearly a decade at five colleges as Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Chief Development Officer (CDO), and Chief Operating Officer (COO). In addition, he has held positions as a university foundation president and a department chair and professor in university Business Departments.

For 10 years, Dr. Sayers worked in the banking industry in the area of real estate and business lending. He also developed and operated commercial and construction lending programs where he gained experience in every facet of the bank from teller to branch manager to assistant controller to mortgage lender.

Dr. Sayers earned a Ph.D. in Organizational Leadership from Columbia International University, a Master of Accountancy from Gardner-Webb University, an MBA from East Tennessee State University, and a Bachelor of Business Administration from Radford University. He is a graduate of the Collegiate Business Management Institute of the Southern Association of College and University Business Officers (SACUBO), as well as the certificate training program of the Association of Business Administrators of Christian Colleges (ABACC).

He has a Bank Leadership Certificate from Stonier Graduate School of Banking, and holds several banking, accounting and finance-related certificates from programs designed specifically for roles as CFO or in commercial banking.

Dr. Jennifer Booth

Vice President for Academic & Enrollment Services

jen.booth@ciu.edu | (803) 807-5028

Biography
Dr. Jen Booth serves as the Vice President for Enrollment & Academic Services at CIU. Jen aims to build relationships with other departments and academic areas to lead in technical innovation, enhanced student services, and increased efficiency. Jen is passionate about using her gifts and abilities to serve students. Her personal goal is to streamline administrative complexities for students and professors enabling them to focus on educational training.
 
Having been raised in a Christian family, Jen came to personal faith in Christ at a young age. It was in her teenage years that she felt called to surrender her life to Christ and eventually to full-time service in ministry.
 
Jen came to CIU after serving at Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary (SEBTS) in Wake Forest, NC. During her years as assistant director of student resources, Jen oversaw financial aid, employment services, and international student services.
 
Jen’s own educational background is in Christian higher education, having earned a Bachelor of Arts degree from Trinity College of Florida in New Port Richey, a Master of Divinity degree from Southeastern Baptist Theological Seminary, and a Doctor of Education degree from Trevecca Nazarene University.
 
Jen is married to her college sweetheart, Dave, and together they have three children—Grayson, Zachary, and Emory.

Tirrell Howell

Vice President of Corporate Communications & IT

tirrell.howell@ciu.edu | (803) 807-5747

Biography

Tirrell Howell has over two decades of experience as a leader in higher education and local state
government. During the COVID-19 pandemic, he served as the deputy chief information officer
at the South Carolina Department of Health and Environmental Control. In this role, he worked
with other leaders to direct the technical response efforts, ensuring the safety of South
Carolinians.

Tirrell is the founder and president of a nonprofit organization, the Ron Howell Foundation,
focused on preparing the next generation of technology leaders. He has also presented at the
Association for Biblical Higher Education (ABHE) and other EdTech conferences, sharing his
expertise in effective IT governance.

At CIU, Tirrell oversees the Information Technology and Campus Security departments, and he
chairs several strategic committees. He holds an MBA in Project Management from Amberton
University and a Bachelor of Business Administration with a focus on Computer Information
Systems from Winthrop University. His professional credentials include certifications in the
Governance of Enterprise Information Technology (CGEIT, ISACA), COBIT 2019 (ISACA),
and Project+ (CompTIA).

Tirrell is also an ordained minister and has served as a pastor, ministry leader, and musician for
various ministries throughout South Carolina.

Dr. Ben Porter

Vice President of Christian Education

ben.porter@benlippen.com | (803) 807-4219

Biography

Dr. Ben Porter has devoted 23 years to education, with the past 15 years spent at Ben Lippen School in various leadership roles, including House Parent, Athletic Director, Lower School Principal, and Assistant Head of School. He holds a Ph.D. in Educational Leadership and a Master’s in Curriculum and Instruction from Columbia International University. A 2001 graduate of The Citadel, Dr. Porter is passionate about prioritizing the student experience at Ben Lippen. He and his wife, Margaret, have been married for 22 years and have two sons, Drake (11th grade) and Stone (9th grade), who have been part of the Ben Lippen community since they were in Pre-K4.
