Overview

It’s important to be aware of the many ethical concerns associated with generative AI. These include issues of equity of access, intellectual property, privacy, sustainability and climate.  

The Markkula Center for Applied Ethics at Santa Clara University provides a comprehensive set of resources on AI Ethics Literacy. Other general resources include: 

 

  • Code Academy: Ethics of Chatbots
  • Educator considerations for ChatGPT
    OpenAI's documentation for educators covers many risks, academic integrity issues, and other ethical considerations
  • Ethics of Artificial Intelligence
    Course guide for Philosophy 3328
  • Google Scholar Search: Chatbots and Ethics

Equity and access

The main popular, standalone chatbots are free and, ChatGPT has a paid premium tier (ChatGPT Plus). If these tools become increasingly fee-based, or restrict premium features behind paywalls, there is potential for inequity of access. Most of the more specific AI-powered research tools have a both free and paid models, and some are exclusively for paying subscribers.

  • Google Scholar Search: Chatbots and Equity
  • Will AI Chatbots Raise Digital Equity Concerns for Students?

Bias

Chatbots base their responses on patterns in their training data and may unintentionally respond in ways that mimic portions of that data. The precise content of Large Language Model training data is not transparent, but it is derived from past open internet sources and includes incorrect and biased information.

There’s a concentration of power in a few companies that can afford to develop and train Large Language Models. LLM training also has a large carbon footprint, and relies on human data annotators and feedback.

  • ACLU: Will ChatGPT revolutionize surveillance?
    ACLU commentary on several ethical issues related to generative AI and chatbots.
  • Google Scholar Search: Chatbots and Bias
  • ChatGPT and large language model bias

Privacy risks

Currently, the popular, standalone chatbots require you to create an account and log in to use them. The companies that make them store and have access to your chatbot conversations. The conversations are used to improve the product and could be used to train future models.

Never share confidential, sensitive, or proprietary information in a chatbot conversation.

  • Anthropic approach to user safety
    By default, Claude conversation data is deleted after a specified amount of time, depending on the interface. Claude conversations are not used to train future models unless a user opts-in.
  • Google Gemini Apps Privacy Hub
    The Google Gemini FAQ clearly explains several privacy-related questions, such as what personal data it collects and uses, and Google's current position on advertising in chatbot conversations.
  • Microsoft Privacy Statement
    Microsoft considered Bing Chat to be part of its larger Bing search and Microsoft Edge web browser products. It's use of account holder data inherits from Microsoft policies for these and is mentioned in its Privacy Statement that explains its uses of personal data.
  • OpenAI Terms & Policies
    OpenAI's website describes their practices regarding account holders' personal information and data, and the terms and conditions of using their products.
  • Perplexity Privacy Policy
    Describes Perplexity's privacy and data retention policies, as well as information transmission to third-party services such as OpenAI.
