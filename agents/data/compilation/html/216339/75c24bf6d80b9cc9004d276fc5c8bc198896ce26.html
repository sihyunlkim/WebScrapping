Make an appointment

  • Make an appointment with a subject librarian or specialist
    Subject librarians and specialists at the library can help you learn more about generative AI tools and how they can assist you in your research, working with data, and producing scholarship. See the library's list of subject librarians and specialists.
  • Make an appointment with the Center for the Advancement of Teaching
    Staff at the Center for the Advancement of Teaching offer pedagogical and educational technology consultations to help Temple instructors revise courses and assignments. They can also help with using generative AI as a teaching tool.
  • Make an appointment with the Writing Center
    Temple students can make an appointment with a writing tutor for writing help in English or Spanish. Temple instructors can consult with Student Success Center staff for help with teaching writing in the context of generative AI.

Attend or request a workshop

  • Request a library instruction session for your course
    Request a library instruction session for your course about generative AI and digital literacy, AI-related copyright and privacy issues, or AI tools for research.
  • Temple Libraries workshops
    The Libraries offer an extensive schedule of free, public workshops on research tools and techniques every semester.
  • Temple Center for Advancement of Teaching workshops
    The Center for Advancement of Teaching offers many workshops for instructors every semester, including ones on approaches to generative AI in teaching.

Complete or teach with library tutorials

Develop your research skills with our self-paced online tutorials. See our full menu of tutorials at library.temple.edu/tutorials.

  • How to SIFT through Misinformation
    This tutorial will help you to: define types of information disorder, identify fact-checking tools used to determine the trustworthiness of recent news content, and apply the SIFT method to help verify information online.
  • Intro to Generative AI and Chatbots
    This tutorial will help you to: describe how chatbots work, identify strengths and weaknesses of current chatbots, consider how chatbots may help or hinder learning, locate resources for additional help.

Instructors, contact the Libraries' instructional designer at asktulibary@temple.edu for help using library tutorials in your course.

  • Guide for instructors: Embed the Libraries' online tutorials in a course
    Step-by-step instructions and instructional video on how to embed the Libraries' interactive tutorials in your Canvas course. Find our tutorials in Canvas Commons by searching "library" and limiting to Temple University. Contact the Libraries' instructional designer for help embedding tutorials and connecting them to the Canvas gradebook.

    The Center for the Advancement of Teaching's report on Turnitin's AI writing detection tool found that it could have
    value in certain limited situations, and should be used with an abundance of caution. It is often inaccurate and does not allow the ability to verify the results. Turnitin's tool appeared to be better than other tools currently available.
