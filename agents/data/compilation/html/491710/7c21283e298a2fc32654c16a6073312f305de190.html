Yeshiva Gedolah of Cliffwood was founded in 2004 under the leadership of Rabbi Shimon Alster, Rabbi of Bais Medrash Torah U’Tefilla in Flatbush, NY and former Rebbi and Mashpia in Mesivta of Long Beach.

Over the years, Rabbi Alster’s success in interacting with students and imparting his enduring influence within them resulted in countless requests for him to establish his own yeshiva. With the advancement of Torah study and escalation of the yeshiva world in twenty-first century America, even the plethora of yeshivos continuously established in the Tri-State Area had become insufficient to satisfy the growing need of such institutions. Thus, the proposal soon turned to practicality.

Yeshiva Gedolah of Cliffwood was established by the Rosh Yeshiva, Rabbi Shimon Alster, with the support of a Board of Directors enlisted from among his close students and congregants. From its inception, the Yeshiva’s objective was to advance each student to his greatest potential. The Yeshiva’s method of education centers on the close interaction of faculty members and students, maintaining the Rosh Yeshiva’s previous path.

Specifically situated in the quiet Jersey town of Cliffwood, Yeshiva Gedolah of Cliffwood is far from the distractions of the city – in an idyllic location that facilitates students’ undisturbed immersion in their studies. Beginning with only twenty students in September of 2004, the Yeshiva’s student body rapidly grew to 35 by that year’s winter session. Success breeds success, and the name of the institution spread as students left the yeshiva, while they kept ongoing connections with its faculty and their peers.

The mission of Yeshiva Gedolah of Cliffwood is to:

  • Educate students in the systematic study and analysis of the Talmud and classical Talmudic commentaries;
  • Teach language and reasoning skills, legal research, logic and modes of rational argument;
  • Emphasize the moral application of Talmudic principles through the study of Mussar and ethical works;
  • Convey an integrated Torah perspective for meaningful and productive interaction within contemporary society;
  • Provide a background in Halacha with an emphasis in practical application.
