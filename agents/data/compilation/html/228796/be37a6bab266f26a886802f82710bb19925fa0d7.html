Online Master of Arts in Education With a Concentration in Artificial Intelligence in Education

Program Highlights

REQUEST INFORMATION APPLY NOW

30 credit hours |  In-state tuition: $490/credit hour | Out-of-state tuition: $575/credit hour

  •  Available in an easily accessible 100% online format from UTEP’s College of Education
  • Skills and knowledge necessary to become powerful advocates for the responsible and ethical use of Artificial Intelligence in K-16 teaching and learning
  • Affordable, with a focus on data literacy practices and leadership
  • Includes student-driven projects based on areas of interest
  • Boasts supportive, diverse faculty with expertise in STEM education

NOTE: YOU MUST HAVE A BACHELOR'S DEGREE TO APPLY.

PROGRAM ADMISSIONS REQUIREMENTS

Become a powerful advocate for the ethical usage of Artificial Intelligence

Local school practitioners, as well as those across the nation in education-related fields, are increasingly tasked with educating while accounting for rapid advances in technology and artificial intelligence, despite having had limited training in this area during their teacher preparation. The Master’s in Artificial Intelligence in Education helps address this gap. It provides a set of courses that integrates interdisciplinary scholarship to merge the application of Artificial Intelligence with human-centered, culturally sustaining approaches to pedagogy. The program focuses on developing system approaches to instructional design and assessment, as well as the utilization of advanced data literacy, to produce responsive and ethical educational experiences that ensures the success of all students. 

This degree is perfect for:

  • Curriculum developers
  • General education teachers with an interest in applying artificial intelligence
  • Training and development specialists
  • Instructional coordinators 

Students in this program will

  • gain the skills and know to identify and address the unique challenges of applying generative artificial intelligence in diverse K-16 educational contexts.
  • focus on system approaches to pedagogy and utilization of advanced data literacy for instructional design and assessment.
  • help improve outcomes for students across the lifespan from early childhood to adulthood through the responsible use of cutting-edge technologies.
  • acquire skills in the following areas:
    • Implementing curricula, lessons and assessments with generative artificial intelligence
    • Leadership and educational advocacy for ethical usage of artificial intelligence in education
    • Implementing research and professional development/training

 Types of jobs students hold after graduation include:

  • Curriculum developer
  • Instructional coordinator
  • Post-secondary administrator
  • Training and development specialist
  • Educational policy analyst

Course Overview

Coursework is unique and includes

  • Practical assignments with applications to schools and community educational settings
  • A mix of readings, videos, and multi-media resources
  • Engagement with classmates from diverse backgrounds, who speak a variety of languages, and who have experience engaging with students/families from diverse backgrounds

 To earn a Master’s in Artificial Intelligence in Education, you must successfully complete of 30 hours of required coursework with a grade of “C” or better.

Core Courses (12 hours)

STEM 5300: Foundations of Gen AI

Focus on the history of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI).Explore how generative AI differs from traditional AI and its potential to transform education. The course emphasizes the importance of responsible AI practices by exploring the ethical and legal frameworks that ensure fairness, transparency, and accountability in AI development and deployment, with the ultimate goal of prioritizing human well-being in education.

STEM 5304: Instructional Design in the Age of Gen AI

Study the major concepts of instructional design theory, and the role that artificial intelligence (AI) may play in learning and in the design of instructional experiences. The course emphasizes competence development through the evaluation and/or redesign of existing learning environments and the creation of new learning environments. With a focus on practice-base application, examine theories, models, and activities associated with the design of experiences, feedback cycles, and environments where Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI) can play a role.

STEM 5308: Adaptive Learning and Assessment with Gen AI

Explore the convergence of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI), adaptive learning, and assessment and the complexities of assessment in the Gen AI-driven classroom. Examine how Gen AI can enhance adaptive learning platforms, the potential impact of Gen AI on student learning, and ethical considerations of AI-driven personalized learning. Emphasis is on assessment strategies that evaluate critical thinking, analysis, problem-solving and creativity in K-16 classrooms.

STEM 5312: Research Methods on Gen AI in Education

Study applications/interventions used in a learning environment and how to apply systematic research to evaluate and improve it. Explore the inquiry process of Design-Based Research, including developing a focus question, selecting methods that establish relevance/rigor, conducting a literature review, collecting and analyzing data, and research dissemination. A particular focus will be on data that is generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) applications and how to apply this data as part of research on AI applications.

Electives (12 Credit Hours Required) : Take any four (4) courses

STEM 5316: Ethics, Culture, and Society in the Age of Gen AI

Focus on the study of the ethics of Artificial Intelligence (AI). It explores a framework for AI ethics, values and norms, principles of beneficence and non-maleficence for AI, accountability, transparency, privacy, security, and current challenges for AI ethics. Conduct critical analysis of AI policy documents and explore challenges in promoting the strategic use of AI for public good.

STEM 5320: Systems Thinking for Educators

Examine system thinking as a powerful framework for nurturing learning.Through hands-on lesson planning using Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI), learn to integrate systems thinking concepts into instructional practices designed, think critically about complex issues and be an informed and responsible citizen who recognizes the interconnectedness of the world around you.

STEM 5324: AI in STEM education

Study different theories about the use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI)  to teach STEM subjects. Focus on the appropriate roles for Gen AI in teaching key concepts within mathematics and science, as well as interdisciplinary STEM domains. Emphasis is on exploring the application of AI to inquiry-based teaching and learning.

STEM 5328: Data Science for Educational Professionals

Develop data literacy by exploring the foundations of data science. Examine the basic skills for representing data, manipulating data, and analyzing data to further understandings of what data might represent. Explore the basics of machine learning and algorithms, including the use of basic statistical concepts that supports interpretation of data that is generated by Artificial Intelligence (AI) applications for learning.

STEM 5332: Collaborative Learning With Gen AI

Explore the convergence of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI) and collaborative learning. Study how Gen AI may enhance collaborative learning platforms, the potential impact of AI on student group learning experiences, and ethical considerations of AI-driven small to large group, student-centered learning. Explore group assessment strategies that evaluate critical thinking, analysis, problem-solving, and creativity, as well as innovative collaborative methods to gauge conceptual understanding across subjects.

EDT 5375: Technology, Assistive Tools and Issues of Access

Learn about assistive technology tools and techniques for the classroom teacher, as well as legal issues regarding technology access and equity. Study principles of educational technology including terminology, historical development, social and ethical implications, proficiency in the application of technology tools, and the integration of technology in school curricula and instructional design.

Culminating Experience (6 hours required): Choose either Capstone OR Thesis pathway

STEM 5340: Capstone 1 for AI in ED

This course serves as part one of the culminating experience for the MA in ED- AI in Education program. Earning a grade of Pass (80% or Better) in this capstone course fulfills a degree requirement for graduation. This course provides the opportunity for students to demonstrate knowledge gained from their course of study and their academic and professional skill development in the field of AI in Education through the initiation of a Capstone Project.

STEM 5344: Capstone 2 for AI in ED

This course serves as a culminating experience for the MA in ED- AI in Education program. Earning a grade of Pass (80% or Better) in this capstone course fulfills the degree requirement for graduation. This course provides the opportunity for students to demonstrate knowledge gained from their course of study and their academic and professional skill development in the field of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Education through the completion of a Capstone Project.

OR

TED 5398: Thesis 1

This course serves as a culminating experience for the MA in ED- AI in Education program. This course provides the opportunity for students to demonstrate knowledge gained from their course of study and their academic and professional skill development in the field of AI in Education through the initiation of a Thesis paper

TED 5399: Thesis 2

This course serves as a culminating experience for the MA in ED- AI in Education program. This course provides the opportunity for students to demonstrate knowledge gained from their course of study and their academic and professional skill development in the field of AI in Education through the defense and publication of a Thesis paper.
