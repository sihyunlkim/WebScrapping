Educate with purpose. Lead with heart.

At LaGrange College, preparing to become a teacher is about more than mastering lesson plans or earning certification — it’s about discovering the kind of leader you are called to be. Through close mentorship with faculty, meaningful classroom experiences, and deep connections within local schools and communities, you’ll grow into an educator who inspires confidence, curiosity and belonging in every student you serve.

From your first education courses to hands-on field experiences, you’ll learn by doing — developing the skills, compassion and adaptability today’s classrooms demand. Whether you feel drawn to elementary, middle grades, or secondary education, LaGrange equips you to make an immediate impact while building a lifelong career grounded in purpose.



What Programs of Study are offered at LaGrange College?
 The LaGrange Education Department offers an innovative collection of courses calculated to graduate well-prepared teachers in the following degree programs that lead to initial certification:

  • Elementary Education (B.A.) 
  • Middle Grades Education (B.A. to M.A.T.) 
  • Secondary Education (B.A. to M.A.T.)

Additionally, the LaGrange Education Department offers the following degree programs that do not lead to initial certification: 

  • Education Studies (B.A.)
  • Teaching Minor

Professor Randolph "Dolph" S. Hardigree toggle open

Randolph “Dolph” S. Hardigree is a Lecturer of Education and is excited to join the Panther Education Department as an instructor in undergraduate and graduate degree programs. Hardigree holds an Ed.S. in Curriculum & Instruction as well as Tier I Leadership certification from LaGrange College. Additionally, he holds a B.S. in Operations Management and an M.Ed. in Elementary Education from Auburn University. 

Prior to joining the LaGrange College faculty, Hardigree spent 30 years as an educator in the Troup County School System, including 12 elementary years at Hollis Hand Elementary School and 18 secondary years in Life Sciences at LaGrange High School. Hardigree also helped establish the International Baccalaureate Diploma Program at LHS, serving as IB Coordinator and teaching the IB Theory of Knowledge course for the last three years. 

In addition to his local career, Hardigree enjoys working as a coach, workshop facilitator, and study group leader for Project Zero at the Harvard Graduate School of Education. 

Hardigree is a proud native of Birmingham, Alabama. His wife, Stacey, is a native of LaGrange and has also spent over 30 years serving the local community as a music educator.  

Dr. Don Livingston toggle open

Dr. Livingston “Dr. D.” received his B.S. from Drexel University in Early Childhood Education, his M.Ed. from West Chester University in Elementary Education and his doctorate in Curriculum Studies from Georgia Southern University. Dr. D. joined the LaGrange College community in 2001.

Dr. D. has served as the President of the Georgia Association of Independent College of Teacher Education (GAICTE) twice and once as President of the Georgia Association of Colleges of Teacher Education (GACTE). He has also served as the President of the Georgia Educational Research Association and was co-editor of the association’s journal, The Georgia Educational Researcher. In 2017, Dr. D. was awarded the Cindi Chance Award for lifetime achievement to teacher preparation in Georgia.

His research interests include expanding curriculum theory, teaching global engagement. In addition to teaching a wide variety of courses in both the undergraduate and graduate programs, Don along with his wife, Dr. Sharon Livingston, sponsor study away courses in Thailand, Costa Rica, Greece, and for the 2020 January Semester, a study away course in New Zealand.  

Dr. D. has served as the President of the Georgia Association of Teacher Educators, President of the Georgia Association of Independent College of Teacher Education (GAICTE) twice and once as President of the Georgia Association of Colleges of Teacher Education (GACTE).

Study Abroad Opportunities

Thailand toggle open

This 20-day experience takes you to the international city of Bangkok before traveling into the northern regions of Thailand (Chiang Mai, Chiang Dao) and Chiang Rai). Discover how art and play are universal languages when interacting with the children of Thailand. Learn about a completely different culture on the other side of the world. Activities include cultural orientation and development of art project ideas prior to departure; one week of service to hill tribe children by teaching through the arts; evenings and weekends are open for visiting temples, shopping, and exploration; zip-lining and a visit to an elephant sanctuary are planned activities.

Greece toggle open

Walk in the footsteps of Socrates, Plato and Aristotle in Athens to learn how their ideas on education are still relevant today. Then travel to Peloponnese to learn about the first Olympic Games and grasp the complexities of ancient Greek societies through excursions to historical archaeological sites.

New Zealand toggle open

Why do some cultural groups seem to do better in societies, while others do not? Although Maoris and Polynesians are culturally similar, Maoris, a conquered indigenous people, do less well in New Zealand society than Polynesian immigrants who voluntarily settled there. What might we learn about our U.S. society through a 20-day immersion experience with Maoris, Polynesian immigrants, and the New Zealanders of British and Irish ancestry? Our course illuminates key terms such as dominant culture, involuntary minorities, and voluntary immigrants as concepts to learn how power operates through cultural identities; and what impact an oppositional identity has on academic performance, relative social status, and economic prosperity. 

Costa Rica toggle open

Experience a full cultural and educational immersion by spending 16 days working with and for the wonderful people in the beautiful country of Costa Rica.  This Servant Leadership themed course will provide opportunities to serve while elevating Spanish language skills and working alongside the United Methodist Church and missions organizations with possible church/school programs and construction.  On weekends we will enjoy days off to travel to volcanoes, hike in the mountains and relax on the beautiful beaches. All majors are welcome.  Costa Rica is a country of great beauty and need...the perfect place to spend a January  serving and learning in a way you’ll never forget.
