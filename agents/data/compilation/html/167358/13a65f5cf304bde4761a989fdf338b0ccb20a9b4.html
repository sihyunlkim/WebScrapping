How to securely use AI at Northeastern University 

  • Categories: Artificial Intelligence, Security
  • Categories: AI, Cybersecurity, GenAI

When a student pastes their financial aid appeal into ChatGPT, or a researcher copies unpublished data into an artificial intelligence tool, they’re potentially exposing sensitive information beyond university protections. As AI becomes essential across campus, a few key practices can help use these tools effectively while protecting sensitive data. 

Sensitive data in AI tools 

One of the biggest concerns in AI use for academics, research, or work, is  pasting or uploading sensitive information directly into non-enterprise AI platforms. 

This is especially important when dealing with personal information, health records, financial data, or research subject information. 

Based on Northeastern’s Data Classification Guidelines, sensitive data includes: 

  • Personally identifiable information (PII): Social Security numbers, dates of birth, addresses
  • Student records: Grades, financial aid details, disciplinary actions
  • Health information: Protected under HIPAA
  • Research data: Unpublished findings, proprietary datasets
  • Financial information: Bank details, payment records
  • Government-regulated data: Controlled Unclassified Information (CUI)

As you read these privacy principles and consider how to incorporate them in your work, remember that personal information is not just data that directly identifies an individual (such as name, address, phone number or email address).  

It also includes any information that can be used indirectly to identify individuals, such as the NUID, SSN, health insurance number, device ID, online identifier, genetic information, and much more. 

How Northeastern’s approved tools protect data 

Northeastern provides enterprise AI tools that prioritize security and privacy. Approved tools like Claude and Microsoft Copilot include critical protections: 

  • Data isolation: Information isn’t used to train AI models or shared across accounts 
  • Compliance frameworks: Enterprise tools meet rigorous security standards  
  • Data storage: Your data stays within university-approved systems 

Do’s and don’ts for secure AI use 

Do: 

  • Use approved AI tools (Claude, Microsoft Copilot) through university credentials 
  • Remove or de-identify sensitive information before using AI assistance 
  • Review Northeastern’s Data Classification Guidelines to determine data sensitivity levels 
  • Consult with the Northeastern AI Review Committee and research compliance with the IRB before using AI in research 
  • Report suspected data breaches to Northeastern ITS immediately 

Don’t: 

  • Paste passwords, API keys, or credentials into any AI tool 
  • Upload personally identifiable information, sensitive information, or regulated data to unapproved platforms 
  • Share unpublished research data or confidential university information with public AI services 
  • Use personal accounts or free AI versions for university work involving sensitive information 

Approved vs. unapproved AI tools 

Northeastern offers multiple AI tools to the global campus community. Examples of approved tools include: 

Claude (Anthropic): Available through Northeastern’s enterprise agreement with strong privacy protections when accessed via university credentials. 

Copilot (Microsoft): Integrated with Microsoft 365, offering AI assistance within productivity tools with enterprise security enabled. 

Other platforms may be used for specific use cases. Unapproved tools lack the data protection agreements and security controls required for university information, and run the risk of breaching the university’s AI policy. 

Making smart AI choices 

Before submitting any prompt, consider: Does this information belong in a non-approved AI tool? Could sharing this data violate regulations or university policy? Is there an approved platform available? 

By using Northeastern’s approved AI platforms and following these guidelines, the university community can leverage AI’s benefits while protecting sensitive information and maintaining data security.
