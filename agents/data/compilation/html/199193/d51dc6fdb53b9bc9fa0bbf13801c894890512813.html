AI in the classroom

Whether you're an instructor looking to learn more about Artificial Intelligence (AI) in the classroom, or a student wondering if you can use ChatGPT to complete an assignment, the Libraries is here to help. 

Have more questions about AI in the classroom? Email us at library_askus@ncsu.edu

Want to request a library instruction session for your class? Request a Library Instruction Session

Quick definitions

  • Artificial Intelligence (AI) - a broad field, which refers to the use of technologies to build machines and computers that have the ability to mimic cognitive functions associated with human intelligence, such as being able to see, understand, and respond to spoken or written language, analyze data, make recommendations, and more. (Google Cloud)
  • Generative AI - a subset of artifical intelligence that learns from data to produce new, unique outputs (MIT). Examples of generative AI include chatbots such as ChatGPT, and image generators such as DALL-E.
  • Large Language Models (LLM) - a category of generative AI model trained on immense amounts of data, making them capable of understanding and generating natural language (IBM). OpenAI's GPT-4, which powers their ChatGPT chatbot, is an example of a Large Language Model.
  • Machine learning - a subset of artificial intelligence that enables systems to learn and improve from experience. (Google Cloud)

View more definitions of AI terms

Request an instruction session

Instructors can request a library instruction session for their class from AI tools for research to integrating an experiential learning activity using generative AI. After we receive your request, we'll be in touch with you shortly by email to discuss your course needs. Contact us at library_instruction@ncsu.edu.

FAQs about AI in the classroom

  • "What is NC State University's AI policy?" As of February 2025, there is no official university-wide policy regarding AI use. However, several university units have created guides to help students and instructors navigate the use of AI in the classroom, including the Office of Faculty Excellence's Navigating the Landscape of Higher Education in the Age of Artificial Intelligence, the Guide for Students on the Ethical Use of Generative AI Tools, and the Graduate Handbook's Artificial Intelligence Policy for Dissertations and Theses. There is also a regularly updated page on UNC System Institutions' AI Documentation, which provides links to documentation about AI from each of the UNC System institutions.
  • How can I tell when AI was used to write something? Plagiarism checkers are unreliable and fallible - do not use these as sole evidence of AI use or academic dishonesty. DELTA provides a thorough guide to AI-evaluation tools and other strategies for detecting the use of AI.
  • What am I allowed to use AI for? Different colleges, departments, and even faculty have different policies. As a student, your best bet is to check with your professor or student work supervisor directly.
  • "How can I address AI in my syllabus?" DELTA has created a guide to AI syllabus statements.
  • "How can I develop assignments with generative AI in mind?" DELTA has created a guide which includes assignment ideas and strategies.
