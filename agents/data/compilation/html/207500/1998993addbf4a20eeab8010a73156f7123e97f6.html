Writing and Citing: Campus and Online Resources

Action Tutoring

The Student Learning Center, a department of University College, offers free tutoring sessions through UC Action. Walk-in, small group appointments, faculty directed sessions, and uniform exam study nights are offered to help students take action towards their own academic success.

Action Tutoring

Assignment Calculator

This can help students create a timeline for completing a variety of writing assignments. This was created in partnership with the Writing Center and OU Libraries.

Assignment Calculator

OU Libraries

Visit the libraries webpage for information about resources available to you as a student at the University of Oklahoma! You'll be able to find information about the databases and other resources available to help you with your research needs.

OU Libraries

OU Writing Center

Visiting the Writing Center can be helpful when writing papers, and may be required by some professors. Writing Center staff will read your papers and help with content and citing sources. The Writing Center offers synchronous and asynchronous meetings with students.

OU Writing Center

The OWL at Purdue

Covers all aspects of writing: thesis statements, citations, grammar, etc. This may be a helpful resource when attempting to avoid plagiarism!

The OWL at Purdue

Knight Cite

Developed by a Calvin College student, this is an online citation generator service provided by the Hekman Library of Calvin University. This service simplifies the often tedious task of compiling an accurate bibliography in the appropriate style by formatting the given data on a source into a reliable citation.

Knight Cite

WebCite

Caches web pages (similar to the Internet Archive's Wayback Machine) ensuring they can be accessed later, even if the original changed or was removed.

WebCite

SGA General Counsel

The SGA's Office of General Counsel has many duties to best serve the students of the University of Oklahoma including: advising students who are charged with academic misconduct or student code violations, as well as students who wish to appeal a grade.

If you are charged with academic misconduct and wish to have an advisor, you can receive assistance from a member of general counsel, all of whom are law students.
