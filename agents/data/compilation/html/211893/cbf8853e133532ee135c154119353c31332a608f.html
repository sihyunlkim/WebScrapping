Alternatives to AI

  • I’m a Professor. A.I. Has Changed My Classroom, but Not for the Worse by Carlo Rotella
    New York Times article from 11/25/25. His three pillars of an AI-resistant classroom:
    1. Use pen-and-paper in-class exams and quizzes, 2. Emphasize writing process over product, and 3. Prioritize classroom activities.
  • If It Was Good Enough for Socrates, It’s Good Enough for Sophomores
    A New York Times Opinion essay by Molly Worthen, on the appeal of oral exams.
  • Alarmed by A.I. Chatbots, Universities Start Revamping How They Teach
    New York Times news article about how higher ed institutes are responding to the increased use of AI bots in college courses.
  • Students Hate Them. Universities Need Them. The Only Real Solution to the A.I. Cheating Crisis by Clay Shirky
    An essay proposing the return of blue book in-class essay exams.
  • Against AI
    Assignment ideas, syllabus language, and video explainers against using large language model AI in the classroom.
  • How Aristotle is Fixing Deep Learning's Flaws
    An article from The Gradient about a project to merge Aristotelian logic with AI deep learning.

ChatGPT

  • This 22-year-old is trying to save us from ChatGPT before it changes writing forever
    Article from NPR''s Planet Money about the person who is created an app to detect usage of ChatGPT.
  • Why I'm not Scared of ChatGPT
    An opinion piece from the Chronicle of Higher Education that argues that educators should look at the increased use of AI bots as an opportunity, not a threat. (If accessing this article from an off-campus network, you will need to sign-in to your Curtis Chronicle subscription.)
  • Student and Faculty Perceptions of Artificial Intelligence in Student Writing
    A report from a study done at the Psychology Department of Kalamazoo College, March 2023.
