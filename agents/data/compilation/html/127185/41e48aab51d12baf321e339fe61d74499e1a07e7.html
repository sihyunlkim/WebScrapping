AI Guidelines for FLC marketing and communications

This document is a companion to FLC’s institutional AI Guidelines. It provides practical, role-specific guidance for staff, faculty, and student workers using AI tools to support content creation, design, branding, and digital storytelling on behalf of FLC.

Scope of use

This guidance applies to AI use in and for:

  • Web content, email campaigns, and social media
  • Print and digital publications
  • Photo and video production
  • Advertising and recruitment materials
  • Internal communications and presentations

Where AI can support your work

Written content

AI can be a powerful assistant in content creation, but it requires human review to ensure accuracy and alignment with FLC's brand.

✅ Do ❌ Do not
Draft emails, captions, social posts, and headlines Use AI to generate final, unedited copy
Summarize or reduce word counts  
Rewrite for tone, clarity, or audience fit  
Apply AP Style suggestions (with review)  
Use AI to synthesize ideas or identify optimized terms  
Use tools for AI plagiarism checks  

 

Visual content

AI can assist in optimizing visuals, but should never misrepresent people, places, or institutional branding at FLC.

✅ Do ❌ Do not
Enlarge or de-noise low-quality images Generate photorealistic people or AI avatars
Extend nondescript backgrounds (e.g., skies, walls) Alter someone’s appearance (e.g., hair color, facial features)
Retouch minor blemishes or lighting (no identity changes) Place students in locations they weren’t photographed in
Remove competitor branding/logos from clothing Combine people from separate shoots into one image
Cut backgrounds for graphic layouts Modify or generate FLC logos, Skyler, or spirit marks
Use symbolic or abstract AI illustrations Add people or structures to an image that were not originally there
Include AI content in internal/student-facing materials with proper logos Use AI to generate or replicate the voice of FLC individuals
  Depict FLC facilities or settings that do not exist

*Note that AI-generated content is not protected by copyright

Brainstorming and research

AI can help stimulate new ideas and identify content themes, but human insight must guide and validate it.

✅ Do ❌ Do not
Jumpstart visual or written concept development Treat AI results as complete research—human verification is required
Suggest SEO keywords and metadata Use AI to decide brand positioning or audience messaging unilaterally
Identify patterns in existing content  
Help with outlines, naming, or campaign theme ideas  

 

Workflow integration tips

Phase How to use AI
Planning Identify parts of your project where AI can reduce manual effort or unlock ideas
Drafting Use AI to speed up rough drafts—but always layer in brand and tone
Review Always include a human edit for voice, consistency, and accuracy
Approval Tag AI use in your project notes when submitting content
Reflection Note which prompts or outputs worked well and share with colleagues

 

Best practices for prompt design

  • Include FLC tone, voice, or campaign context when prompting
  • Share examples of past content to get on-brand results
  • Refer to FLC’s mission or audience profiles to shape responses
  • Keep a shared list of tested, effective prompts (and share with colleagues!)

AI attribution standards when AI contributes to the final deliverables

AI is now a regular part of many content creators’ workflow, much like any other tool in our tech stack. While we don’t require formal attribution when AI contributes to a project, we prioritize transparency in our usage.

Tool access and support

  • Use only approved tools listed by the AI Resource Group
  • MarComm maintains a list of recommended tools for creative and editorial work
  • Training is available through Reed Library, the MarComm team, CTL, or the FLC AI Institute
  • Contact your supervisor or the AI Resource Group before using new tools not yet evaluated

Editing: AI tools can answer questions about AP style, but should be checked, as not all tools are current. Reach out to MarComm for more information on available AI tools to help with writing that adheres to the institutional brand guidelines.
