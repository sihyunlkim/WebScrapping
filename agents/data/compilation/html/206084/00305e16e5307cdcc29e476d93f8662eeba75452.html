About AI Tools for Academic Work

Below is a list of select AI tools useful for academic work. Some tools require a fee to use advanced features.
Remember to review AI generated content for accuracy, bias, and originality.
Use AI ethically. It is not a substitute for peer-reviewed sources or expert feedback.

AI for Research Projects

  • Caktus
    Use for:
    - Essay and assignment drafting (with a need for human review)
    - Math problem solving and code generation
    - Citation generation and formatting
    - Language learning tools
    - Resume and email writing support
  • ChatGPT (Chat Generative Pre-training Transformer)
    Use for:
    - Essay planning, drafting, and editing support
    - Summarization of academic texts and research papers
    - Code generation and debugging (especially in Python and other common languages)
    - Language translation and grammar correction
    - Study aid for test preparation and concept review
  • claude
    Use for:
    - Long-context understanding for analyzing lengthy academic texts
    - Summarization of research papers and articles
    - Assistance with writing, editing, and refining academic prose
    - Support for coding, data analysis, and technical problem-solving
  • Copilot (formerly Bing AI Chat)
    Use for:
    - Real-time web search integration for up-to-date academic sources
    - Summarization of articles, papers, and web content
    - Drafting and editing support within Microsoft Word and Outlook
    - Data analysis and visualization in Excel
    - Multimodal capabilities (text, image, and document understanding)
    - Citation suggestions and source linking
  • Elicit
    Use for:
    - Semantic search for academic papers (beyond keyword matching)
    - Automated extraction of study details (e.g., sample size, methodology, outcomes)
    - Literature matrix creation for comparing multiple studies
    - Question answering based on academic sources
    - Integration with Semantic Scholar for access to open-access research
  • ExplainPaper
    Use:
    - Simplifies technical and domain-specific language in research papers
    - Click-to-explain interface for targeted clarification
    - Supports PDF uploads and links to arXiv papers
    - Useful for reading comprehension, literature reviews, and academic writing
  • Google Gemini (formerly Bard)
    Conversational AI tool to brainstorm ideas, spark creativity, and accelerate productivity. Use for:
    - Research writing assistance and summarization of academic texts
    - Real-time collaboration within Google Docs and Sheets
    - Translation and language support for multilingual research
    - Voice command and multimodal input capabilities
    - Integration with Google Scholar and other educational tools
  • Lex
    AI-powered word processor and editor. Features:
    - In-line AI suggestions and edits for clarity, tone, and structure
    - Holistic feedback on entire essays or research papers
    - Ability to distinguish between user- and AI-generated content
    - Customizable prompts and collaborative writing tools
    - Integration with Claude AI for more natural, human-like feedback
  • MedEdMENTOR
    AI for medical education research. Features:
    - AI-guided theory selection based on research questions or phenomena
    - Database of over 250 summarized educational theories
    - Literature search tools tailored to HPE scholarship
    - Actionable lessons and glossaries for understanding key concepts
    - Personalized mentoring and writing support for theory-driven research
  • Consensus GPT (Formerly ResearchGPT)
    Used for:
    - Searching a database of 200M+ papers directly within the ChatGPT interface.
    - Summarization and comparison of findings across multiple studies
    - Drafting of academic content with accurate citations
    - Filtering by study design, journal quality, and publication type
  • Research Rabbit
    Used for:
    - Visual citation maps showing relationships between papers, authors, and topics
    - AI-driven paper recommendations based on your research interests
    - Timeline views to track the evolution of research fields
    - Collaboration tools for sharing collections and insights
    - Integration with citation managers like Zotero and EndNote
  • scite
    Features:
    - Smart citation analysis (supporting, contrasting, mentioning)
    - Citation context directly from full-text articles
    - Research dashboards for tracking topics or authors
    - Integration with reference managers like Zotero and EndNote
    - Browser extension for citation insights while browsing
  • Semantic Scholar
    Features:
    - Semantic search for more accurate and relevant results
    - Citation graphs and influence scores to assess paper impact
    - Paper summaries and key phrase highlights
    - Author pages with publication history and co-author networks
    - Filters for open access, publication type, and field of study
