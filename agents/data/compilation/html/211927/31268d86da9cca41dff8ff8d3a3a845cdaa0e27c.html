Artificial Intelligence (AI) and Research - Learn More

10/07/2024
Andrea Rodgers
No Subjects

The Artificial Intelligence (AI) guide is a brand new resource created by DCCC Library Services to help you navigate the basics of AI and its use as a research tool. The new guide contains information on:

  • What Is Artificial Intelligence?
  • AI Uses and Limitations
  • How To Prompt AI
  • Evaluating AI Tools and Output
  • Creating AI Citations
  • Resources and Additional Reading

We hope the DCCC community finds this guide informative and helpful.

If you have specific questions about AI and research, just ask!

Library Services is happy to present to courses on best practices in using AI for research help. Sign up for a session, or make a one-on-one appointment with a DCCC librarian to learn more about AI.

From Reconstruction to Civil Rights: Discovering Stories in ProQuests Black Freedom Struggle Database

The Black Freedom Struggle digital archive provides over 3,000 primary source documents dedicated to the African-American fight against racial injustice in the United States.Its twofold goal is to enable a deeper understanding of the influential people who have pursued this quest for freedom while likewise providing information about the harsh, often violent, opposition they faced. From slavery and the abolitionist movement to Black Lives Matter and contemporary police brutality, this database focuses on six key subjects (eras) to support students and scholars better understand this critical piece of American history:Slavery and the Abolitionist Movement (1790-1860);Civil War and Reconstruction (1861-1877);The Jim Crow Era to the Great Depression (1878-1932);The New Deal and World War II (1933-1945);The Civil Rights and Black Power Movements (1946-1975);The Contemporary Era (1976-2000s).Primary sources include newspaper and periodical articles, census information, official government documents, laws, and even FBI reports for an eye-opening reflection on the history and legacy of racial injustice in America.To access, visit learningcommons.dccc.edu/blackfreedomstruggle, or Library Services' A-Z Database List....

Witnessing History: Discovering MLK through the EBONY Archives

Explore hundreds of articles on Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., featured across more than 800 issues available.The EBONY Archives offers a powerful window into the life and legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr., documenting his leadership through contemporary articles, interviews, and photography. It provides students and researchers with essential primary sources that capture how Dr. Kings work and impact were represented in the Black press, adding depth and cultural context to civil rights studies.--Text copy provided by POWER LibraryEBONY Archives is provided in part by Library Services and Technology Act (LSTA) funds from the U.S. Institute of Museum and Library Services and through the Commonwealth of Pennsylvanias Library Access funds administered by the Pennsylvania Department of Education, Office of Commonwealth Libraries. To access, visit learningcommons.dccc.edu/ebony-magazine-archive, or Library Services' A-Z Database List....
