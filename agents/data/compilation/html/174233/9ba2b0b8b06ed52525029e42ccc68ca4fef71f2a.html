What information do I need to report?

If you have ever been suspended, expelled, or placed on probation from any high school or college due to academic dishonesty, or because of an alleged offense that harmed or had the potential to harm others, you must answer 'Yes' to this question and provide a description of the incident(s) and the outcome(s).

If you have been found legally responsible for a sexual offense, or if you have sexual offense charges pending against you at this time, you must answer 'Yes' to this question and provide a description of the incident(s) and outcome(s) or anticipated outcome(s).

Why do I need to report this information?

The University of Minnesota Duluth is committed to maintaining a scholarly, educational environment characterized by safety, respect, and integrity. As part of this commitment, the University requires undergraduate applicants to supply information about their conduct.

How are responses to these questions considered?

We believe that selecting applicants for admission to the University of Minnesota Duluth requires a very careful, individual, overall assessment of each applicant's application materials. Each application is reviewed comprehensively, based on primary and secondary review factors, to determine whether an applicant is admissible. After the admissions committee has reviewed the application materials and determined that an applicant is academically admissible, only then does the admissions committee consider any responses regarding student conduct or sexual offense charges and/or convictions. More information about how those responses factor into an admission decision is in the next question.

How do these responses factor into an admission decision?

The following factors are considered in our review of any 'Yes' answer to the conduct questions. These factors are tied to the University of Minnesota Duluth's commitment to maintaining a scholarly, educational environment characterized by safety, respect, and integrity.

  • Pattern or repeated behavior
  • Recency of conduct
  • Evidence of responsibility
  • Severity of incident or crime (serious/minor harm)
  • Nature of incident or crime (person/property; intentional/negligent/reckless; violent/nonviolent; sexual offense)
  • Age at time of offense (juvenile/adult)
  • Punishment served (fully served, probation, etc.)
  • Conditions on probation or parole (order for protection, no contact with minors, etc.)
  • Evidence of rehabilitation (work, treatment, school, community service)
  • Particular incompatibility with University program where seeking enrollment

When do you review the answers that I provided to these questions?

Answers provided to these questions are considered only if an applicant is first determined to be academically admissible; then, only after an applicant is determined to be otherwise academically admissible, will academic dishonesty issues, reported conviction, or pending charges be reviewed. If an applicant is not academically admissible, the applicant is denied admission, and the conduct information is not considered.

The committee will review the description of the circumstances. If the description is sufficient and the circumstances around the conduct are clear, an admission decision will be made. If the description is not clear or additional information is needed for the committee to complete its review, a follow-up questionnaire is sent to the applicant. The completed and signed questionnaire is required before a complete review of the application can be made. A representative from the Office of Admissions may contact an applicant's former institution or a background check may be performed to verify information provided by the applicant, or to obtain further information.

The applicant's application for admission will be comprehensively reviewed by the admissions committee to determine if the applicant is academically admissible to the University of Minnesota Duluth.

What if I have charges pending against me that haven't been resolved?

We may defer a decision until the charges have been resolved. Please contact our office to discuss your individual situation.

Will student conduct incidents, charges or convictions prevent admission to the University of Minnesota Duluth?

Applicants reporting academic dishonesty, prior sexual offense charges and/or convictions, or pending sexual offense charges, are never automatically barred from admission to the University. The case details are reviewed by a committee, based on the factors listed above. (The committee is composed of representatives from the following University departments and offices: the Office of Student Conduct and Conflict Resolution, Housing and Residence Life, the UMD Police Department, the Office of Diversity and Inclusion, Academic Services, and the Office of Admissions.)

In rare cases when the circumstances raise concern about academic integrity or the overall safety of the campus community, it may be determined that admission cannot be granted.

Reported offenses and incidents that require further review are referred to the committee, which meets on an as-needed basis to review these cases. This committee carefully reviews all the application materials provided by the applicant, and will make a recommendation based on review of the factors listed above. A determination is then made by University officials as to whether admission is granted. 

If I am denied admission because of conduct reasons, may I appeal the decision?

Due process is provided to any applicant denied admission. An applicant may appeal the deny decision. An appeal of a decision is a request for a different decision, on the basis of new information that was not initially included in an applicant's application.

Appeals should include:

  1. New information that was not previously shared at the point of application AND
  2. Compelling information that an additional review is warranted.

A separate appeals committee will review the application, supporting materials and appeal information to determine whether an appeal should be approved and admission granted. The applicant will be notified in writing of the decision. [The appeals committee may include members from the Office of Student Life, the UMD Police Department, the UMD college to which the applicant is seeking admission, and the Office of Admissions.]

If you have questions about our process or would like assistance in completing your application responses, please contact our office.
