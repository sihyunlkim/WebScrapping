Research

Sparking innovations at a world-class research university

Thousands of faculty members across Indiana University Bloomington are conducting life-changing research every day. We’re exploring new frontiers, from health, to business, to technology. And we're leading the way in the arts and humanities with unapologetic excitement for transformative cultural experiences.

Together, IU faculty and students are facing the world’s biggest challenges head on—enhancing lives, building Indiana's economy, and strengthening communities across our great state and the world.

Research and creative activity changing the world

Assisting scientists who study the Earth’s sun, stars

IU Bloomington is one of only three regional coordinating sites for Citizen CATE, a continent-spanning project to record the April 2024 eclipse during totality.

Learn more

Demystifying human-animal disease connection

IU researchers consider how to minimize the emergence of zoonotic diseases, or new infectious diseases where pathogens are circulated between humans and domestic and wild animals.

Learn more

Preparing U.S. military to mitigate cyber attacks

A $506K grant from the Department of Defense enables IU researchers to produce new course content and provide guidance to other universities, stakeholders to keep our nation safe.

Learn more

Centers and institutes on the forefront of discovery

Luddy Artificial Intelligence Center

This center brings together IU experts who investigate challenges and opportunities of AI, from technical, societal, scientific, and applied perspectives.

Environmental Resilience Institute

The ERI is fostering Indiana’s ability to withstand the wide-ranging impacts of climate change, including changes that affect the state’s economy and health.

Irsay Institute

"The Irsay" is dedicated to health and healthcare support, research, and education with a focus on mental health services, stigma, and workforce development.

Center for Research on Race and Ethnicity in Society

This interdisciplinary center brings together faculty and students across IU to study how bias and discrimination affect ourselves and our society.

The Kinsey Institute

For more than 75 years, the Kinsey Institute has been breaking down barriers and researching critical issues on sexuality, gender, reproduction, and relationships.

The Observatory on Social Media

The Observatory on Social Media brings together several schools and centers to analyze and counter misinformation on social media.
