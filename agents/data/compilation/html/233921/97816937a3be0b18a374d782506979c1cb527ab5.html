Guidance for Using ChatGPT Edu

Responsible Use of Generative AI

The technologies and circumstances surrounding AI continue evolving. Our efforts to provide interim guidance may be superseded by formal governance or legislation at any time.

Please see Virginia Tech’s AI Principles at Responsible and Ethical AI Principles for broad guidance on using AI. At present, ChatGPT Edu has only been reviewed and approved for up to moderate risk data. For examples of low and moderate risk data, please see the table in section 2.1 Data Risk Classification table on Virginia Tech’s Risk Classification Standard.

Saving Content

Since this is a pilot with no promise of continued access and because your data (such as chats, projects, and Generative Pre-trained Transformers or GPTs)—cannot be migrated out of the ChatGPT Edu workspace at the end of the pilot, we recommend you save a copy of any information you want to keep long-term in an appropriate location, such as a document, outside of ChatGPT.

Accessing ChatGPT Edu

If you are invited to join ChatGPT Edu, you obtain access by:

  1. Completing the ChatGPT Edu Training on PDN Canvas.
  2. Receiving an email invitation to ChatGPT Edu within 1-2 business days.
  3. Creating or migrating an existing @vt.edu ChatGPT account from your invitation email.

Before proceeding, please carefully review the following terms of participation in this pilot program:

  • You must use your primary email address (i.e., [Your PID]@vt.edu) to sign up for and access ChatGPT Edu. This is the address we will use to invite you to ChatGPT Edu. Virginia Tech email aliases cannot be used to log into ChatGPT Edu at this time.
  • If you have a ChatGPT Free or ChatGPT Plus account associated with your primary email, then upon invitation to the pilot, you must choose either to migrate your existing chats and GPTs or to export your existing data. If you migrate your data to ChatGPT Edu, then your chat history and GPTs will be owned by and remain in the ChatGPT Edu workspace. You will not be able to migrate your chat history or GPTs back to a Free or Plus account in the future. The other option is to export your chat history, but that history cannot be reimported into a ChatGPT account.
  • If your primary email address is associated with a ChatGPT Business plan, then you will use the single sign-on (SSO) option to log into ChatGPT Edu and the other login methods (such as password or social login) to access your Business workspace.
  • If you have a ChatGPT account using a Virginia Tech email alias, that account should not be affected by your participation in this pilot.
  • If you have a free or paid account, whether with your primary email or alias email, that uses the Developer Platform (https://platform.openai.com), then your access to the Developer Platform should not be affected.
  • Your conversations in ChatGPT are generally private, though there are cases where authorized university personnel may access conversations in accordance with VT policies. We recommend not inputting sensitive or personal information into ChatGPT Edu. Please keep this in mind if you are prompted to migrate or export data from an existing account. For details, please see the question on our FAQ “Who can see my conversations with ChatGPT Edu?”

Creating Your Account

The first time setting up your account, you must use a web browser. Do not use the ChatGPT mobile app for initial setup.

The steps below assume that you have completed the ChatGPT Edu Canvas training and received an invitation email to ChatGPT Edu.

To create your ChatGPT Edu account:

  1. Go to the email invite in your inbox and click Join workspace to launch the website.
  2. Click the Log in button.
  3. Type your primary email ([PID]@vt.edu) and click Continue.
  4. You may be redirected to the VT Login page or you may be presented with a list of login options. If you see a list of login options, then select chatgpt-edu-pilot SSO from the list.
  5. Log in with your PID and PID passphrase and complete your 2 factor authentication.
  6. You are now added to the ChatGPT Edu workspace. See the step below that applies to whether you had a ChatGPT account at your primary email address and what kind of account it is. 
    1. No existing ChatGPT account: follow the prompts to create a new account.
    2. Existing primary email @vt.edu account with a Free or Plus plan: you must choose between two options:
      1. Transfer existing chat history and GPTs to your account when migrating into the ChatGPT Edu workspace. If you choose this option, your chat history and GPTs will be owned by the ChatGPT Edu workspace. Please note that you will not be able to migrate your existing chat history or GPTs back to a Free or Plus account nor to another workspace.
      2. Export and delete existing chat history. This option will only export your chat history. There is no way to import the exported chat history into other ChatGPT accounts or workspaces.
    3. Existing primary email @vt.edu account with a Business plan: you will have a new ChatGPT Edu workspace added alongside your existing workspace. No chats or GPTs will be migrated.

Logging into ChatGPT

  1. In your browser, go to https://chatgpt.com.
  2. Click the Log in button.
  3. Type your primary email ([PID]@vt.edu) and click Continue.
  4. You may be redirected to the VT Login page or you may be presented with a list of login options. If the latter happens, then select chatgpt-edu-pilot SSO from the list.
  5. Log in with your PID and PID passphrase and complete your two-factor authentication.

Credit-Based Usage

In January 2026, OpenAI implemented a new business model that consumes credits when advanced large language models (GPT-5.1 Thinking or GPT-5.1 Pro) and features (Deep Research, image generation, or voice) are used. Credits are not needed to use the base GPT-5.1 model and features such as search, file upload, and canvas. 

  • Note: GPT-5.1 selects the response model and, if credits are available, may select the GPT-5.1 Thinking model, which would consume credits.

To manage costs, we will be providing most users with a limited, weekly replenishing allowance for credit-based features. 

Two levels of access are available to pilot participants:

  • Base access: Access to basic features that do not require credits.
  • Advanced access: Access to basic features plus advanced features, with a limited credit allowance provided.

Access levels are designated based on applications to the ChatGPT Edu pilot extension.

Base access: Credit-using features such as deep research, voice mode, image generation, Thinking & Pro models appear “grayed out” in the interface as they are not available for use. There are no limits to utilizing non-credit-based features, such as search, file upload, canvas, and others.

Advanced access: If you have advanced access and reach your credit limit, you will see messages and changes in the interface, such as those shown in the screenshots below. 

If you run out of advanced model access, you will see: “0 message remaining.” Please ignore the direction to “Upgrade to Plus to keep the conversation going.”

When a feature is no longer available, it will appear washed out in the interface and, if you hover over it with your cursor, will inform you of when it will be usable again. As a reminder, we will not be able to increase credits, so you will need to wait until your credits replenish.

You may also go to submit a prompt and be informed of the credit limit.

Training & Resources

We provide general training resources on ChatGPT Edu but cannot offer role-specific training. As part of the pilot, we want you to try ChatGPT responsibly in your role and provide feedback to help us understand its effectiveness.

Training

Current training resources include asynchronous mandatory training on Generative AI and responsible AI usage along with optional training on how to use ChatGPT from OpenAI. 

Access the Canvas training site for:

  • (Optional) Generative AI and responsible AI usage: This asynchronous training module introduces you to generative AI and goes over responsible use and AI principles. The objectives:
    • Define GenAI
    • Identify key challenges and limitations of using GenAI
    • Acknowledge responsible AI principles
  • (Optional) ChatGPT 101 training by OpenAI: With this training, you can review how ChatGPT can help you in your work tasks and familiarize you with the interface and prompting.
  • (Optional) ChatGPT 102 training by OpenAI: The purpose of this video is to apply ChatGPT to reduce workload as well as how to build a generative pre-trained transformer (GPT) (a fine-tuned version of ChatGPT).

You can sign up for consultations to get answers to your questions and obtain hands-on support. To schedule a consultation, please go to TLOS Consultations and navigate to "ChatGPT Edu Pilot." You will select a time duration (15, 30, or 60-minute time blocks), consultant, and preferred date and time. You'll then be prompted to enter your name and email address and a brief synopsis of what you'd like to discuss.

If you review the training materials but still have difficulty using ChatGPT Edu, please go to the 4Help Portal, sign in, select Get Help, and let us know that you need help learning to use ChatGPT Edu.

Troubleshooting

I don’t have access to Virginia Tech ChatGPT Edu.

If you have been accepted into the ChatGPT Edu Pilot Extension but have not received an email invite to join within 1-2 business days, please go to the 4Help Portal, sign in, and select Get Help to let us know.

I can’t log into ChatGPT Edu.
  • If you have trouble accessing your account, please go to the 4Help Portal, sign in, and select Get Help to let us know you completed the training but still have not been invited to ChatGPT Edu.
  • If you've forgotten your PID, go to Finding My Forgotten VT Username (PID).
  • If you've forgotten your PID passphrase, go to Changing or Resetting My Password.
I can’t download ChatGPT onto my desktop.

ChatGPT can be downloaded onto your device if it meets the system requirements. 

  • Windows 10 version 17763.0 or higher.
  • macOS 14+ with Apple Silicon (M1 or better).
    • Check your Macbook specs to ensure you meet the requirements. Intel-based macOS users cannot download the app.

If you do not have administrator access on your device and your device meets the minimum requirements for the app, then please discuss with your departmental IT about whether they can install the application for you.

I can’t download ChatGPT onto my phone.

Having issues downloading ChatGPT on your mobile devices? Common issues include:

  • Compatibility of your phone. To check your phone's compatibility, see:
    • What are the ChatGPT Android app minimum requirements?
    • ChatGPT iOS app minimum requirements
  • Low storage space. To check your storage:
    • Android: Go to Settings > Storage.
    • iPhone: Go to Settings > General > iPhone Storage.

FAQs

Does OpenAI use my conversations and data in ChatGPT Edu for training?

No. If you have multiple ChatGPT subscriptions, check that you are using ChatGPT Edu for your work and not a different ChatGPT account/plan that you may have.

Does OpenAI own the content or data I use with ChatGPT Edu?

No. The inputs and outputs with ChatGPT Edu are not owned by OpenAI. Be sure you are using ChatGPT Edu and not a different account/plan that you may have.

Can I still join the ChatGPT Edu pilot?

The application deadline has passed and all selected participants have been contacted.

What kind of data can I use with ChatGPT Edu?

You can only use low and moderate-risk data. You cannot use high-risk data. To help you identify high-risk data, see the Data Risk Classification table on page 2 of the Virginia Tech Risk Classification Standard. See the “Secure and protect data and privacy” of the Responsible AI Principles - ChatGPT Edu document for more specific guidance, some examples and additional information.

Will I be able to export my chat history or GPT before the pilot ends?

There is no option to migrate your chat history or GPTs back to a Free or Plus account in the future. There is also no option to export all your chat history. If this were possible, there is no way to import that chat history into a different ChatGPT account.

To save key chats and deliverables, we recommend routinely copying + pasting information you want to keep from ChatGPT to a blank document in a secure location at intervals throughout the duration of the pilot.

Tip: You can preserve formatting when copy + pasting from ChatGPT through the following options:

  1. Google Docs: After you enable markdown and install the Google Docs Offline browser extension, you can use the "Paste from Markdown" option.
  2. Microsoft Word:
    1. Windows: Right-click and choose "Keep Source Formatting."
    2. MacOS: Right-click and choose “Paste Special”, then “Formatted Text (RTF).”
What are the limits on my ChatGPT Edu conversations?

The “context window” (the maximum amount of text input you enter and output response from the model in a single conversation) varies depending on the model you choose. See OpenAI's models and select a specific model to see its context window. Keep in mind, the ratio is 1 token = ¾ word, so 100,000 tokens is roughly equal to 75,000 words.

There are also rate limits in place to manage the frequency of interactions in a given period of time. See OpenAI’s Rate Limits page for more information.

For users with advanced access, invoking credit-based features (e.g. Thinking or Pro models, Deep Research) may deplete your allocation of credits for that cycle. Should that happen, you will not be able to use any credit-based features until the next cycle, when credits are renewed. 

What’s the difference between base and advanced access?
  • Base access: Access to basic features that do not require credits. This includes features such as the flagship and instant models, file upload, search and canvas.
  • Advanced access: Access to basic features plus advanced features, with a limited credit allowance provided. Advanced features include Thinking and Pro models, Deep Research, voice mode, and image generation.
I have advanced access. How will I know when I’m approaching my credit limit?

Unfortunately, there is not a way for users to see how many credits are allocated to them or how many credits are remaining within the cycle. If you are finding yourself consistently running up against credit limits, here are some tips to help you stretch your credits a little farther:

  • When possible, you may wish to opt for instant or legacy models over Thinking or Pro models, as the latter consume far more credits. Be aware that using the flagship model (GPT 5.2) may automatically switch to an advanced model for complex queries. If you do need to use a Thinking model, opt for "Standard" thinking time rather than "Extended" to conserve credits.
  • Utilize credit-based features strategically and sparingly. Keep in mind that credit-based features include advanced (Thinking, Pro) models, deep research, voice mode, and image generation.
    • Example: If preparation is needed to refine a Deep Research prompt before actually running it, you may wish to tackle the preparation step manually, through no- or low-credit using features in ChatGPT Edu, or through another AI platform like Copilot first. Once the Deep Research prompt has been refined, you can then run it in ChatGPT Edu's Deep Research mode. 
  • OpenAI is currently offering 5 free Deep Research queries and 3 free image generations per day for all ChatGPT Edu users. Taking advantage of this promotion may help carry your credits a little farther.
What model should I use with ChatGPT?

ChatGPT offers quite a few different models to choose from. To learn the differences and know which is the best for your purposes, please consult OpenAI's list of Models. Please note that flagship and Instant models are available to everyone with base access while Thinking and Pro models are only available to participants with advanced access.

What sharing and GPT access features can I use or not use in Virginia Tech’s ChatGPT Edu instance?

These features and permissions may change.

 You can:

  • Share your conversations with other individual pilot participants.
  • Share generative pre-trained transformers (GPTs) you create with other pilot participants, either as individuals or groups.

You cannot:

  • Download 3rd-party GPTs.
  • Share GPTs or conversations with non-pilot participants.
What should I do if I’m receiving a ChatGPT error message?

These are general troubleshooting steps:

  • Check the service status of ChatGPT to see if the service is down or encountering high error rates.
  • Check that you are logged into ChatGPT Edu account (you should see a VT logo in the top right corner) and not another account you have with ChatGPT (e.g., a free version).
  • If you’re having trouble logging in, try logging into ChatGPT Edu from a private/incognito browsing window or from a different web browser. If this works, you may need to clear your web browser’s cache. It may also be the case you have a 3rd-party browser extension that may be blocking or affecting your connection.
  • Update your web browser or the mobile/desktop app. You may need to contact your departmental IT to update your browser or app if you do not have administrator access on your device.
  • If you are using a private relay service (e.g., iCloud’s private relay) or a VPN service, try accessing ChatGPT Edu without using the VPN or relay service.

If you continue to receive a ChatGPT error message, please go to the 4Help portal, sign in, and click Get Help to report the issue. Please include what troubleshooting steps you’ve taken and a screenshot of the error, if possible.

Can I use the ChatGPT desktop app or mobile app?

Please see Downloading ChatGPT.

What’s the difference between ChatGPT Edu and ChatGPT Enterprise?

ChatGPT Edu and ChatGPT are similar, but not the same. ChatGPT Edu has contractual terms  more appropriate to higher education institutions.

What’s the difference between ChatGPT Edu and ChatGPT Free/Plus/Business?

The main differences are that ChatGPT Edu:

  • Has been approved for low and moderate risk data through Virginia Tech’s procurement process.
  • Keeps chats and GPTs in one workspace.
  • Puts all pilot participants into one workspace, which facilitates sharing between workspace members and allows for central management. For example, administrators of ChatGPT Edu can create groups for teams or departments that want to easily share GPTs with one another.
How can I share my GPTs with my department or team?

ChatGPT Edu administrators can create groups to facilitate sharing GPTs among departments or teams. If you would like a group created, please contact our Virginia Tech support team by going to the 4Help Portal, signing in, and selecting Get Help. In the ticket you submit, please specify the name for the group and list the PIDs and names of the people who should be group members.

How do GPTs work in the credit-based system?

Credits are tied to the user invoking the GPT, not the GPT creator. If a user switches to or is routed to a Thinking model, that usage would count against the user’s own credits. If the user prefers not to consume credits, we recommend staying on Instant.

For users without access to advanced models, the system will fall back to 5.1 Thinking Mini, which does not consume credits.

Can I connect my GPT to other tools with an API?

No, API access through GPTs is not available at this time, primarily because API actions present a risk of sending data to unvetted third parties.

Can I purchase more credits?

Unfortunately no, additional credits cannot be purchased at this time.

Who can see my conversations with ChatGPT Edu?

Access to conversations in ChatGPT Edu is guided by university policies and federal regulations to ensure privacy and proper use. Access is managed as follows:

  1. Your conversations are generally private: Your ChatGPT Edu conversations are not publicly visible or used by OpenAI to train its models. However, as with other university-provided tools, some access by authorized personnel may occur under specific circumstances.
  2. Authorized access: According to Virginia Tech’s policies on electronic information (e.g. Policy 7035), authorized university personnel may access electronic communications when it is necessary for legitimate business purposes such as ensuring compliance with university policies, resolving security concerns, or responding to legal obligations. ChatGPT admins cannot see the contents of your ChatGPT Edu messages at this time. There are companies that offer a Compliance API that enables viewing ChatGPT Edu conversations, but there are no plans to purchase a Compliance API tool. 
  3. User responsibility: It is advisable not to input sensitive or personal information into ChatGPT Edu, as privacy and security depend on the nature of the data being used. You should also follow Acceptable Use of Information Systems at Virginia Tech.

Regarding OpenAI’s ability to view your conversations: “Authorized OpenAI employees will only ever access your conversations for the purposes of resolving incidents, recovering end user conversations with your explicit permission, or where required by applicable law.” (from the ChatGPT Enterprise and ChatGPT Edu FAQ response to:  “Who can view conversations and chat history in ChatGPT Enterprise and ChatGPT Edu?”).

In addition to the above, Virginia Tech’s ChatGPT Edu workspace administrators also have access to a user analytics dashboard of aggregated usage data across the workspace.

2/27/2025 Update: OpenAI began sending weekly reports on user and GPT activity to the pilot’s evaluation team. The user data includes but is not limited to, each participant’s name, email, when they were last active, and the number of messages they have sent across ChatGPT, custom GPTs, and Projects. The GPT data includes, but is not limited to, the GPT’s name and description, who created it, how many people it is shared with, and the number of messages to it. These weekly user reports do not include the contents of messages from your interactions with ChatGPT.

3/31/2025 Update: OpenAI launched a new user analytics dashboard for ChatGPT Edu admins which shows aggregated data on unique active users, total messages sent/received along with the models and features used, as well as GPT activity including GPT names, their creator name, total messages to the GPT, and how many people are using that GPT. It also shows per-user activity. This dashboard does not include the contents of messages from your interactions with ChatGPT.

I deleted a conversation or a GPT I made, can you restore it?

Unfortunately, once a conversation or GPT is deleted, it cannot be restored.

Can students use ChatGPT Edu?

No, ChatGPT Edu was not reviewed for use with student data. However, GA/GTA/GRAs are eligible for the ChatGPT Edu pilot if they applied to the pilot and consented to sharing select personal data with OpenAI and using ChatGPT Edu in their capacity as a GA/GTA/GRA.

OpenAI rolled out a new ChatGPT feature, when will it be available?

ChatGPT is an evolving tool. Some features may be automatically enabled as the pilot progresses while others may be optional to enable. If there are security concerns around optional features, then those features may not be enabled.

Why might I have my ChatGPT Edu license revoked?

Your ChatGPT Edu license may be revoked if:

  • You do not respond to feedback surveys or participate in feedback activities (e.g., interviews) required for the pilot.
  • You do not log in for 60 days.
  • We become aware of misuse or irresponsible use of ChatGPT.
