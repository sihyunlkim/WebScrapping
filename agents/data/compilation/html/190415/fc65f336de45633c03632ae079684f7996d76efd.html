GenAI in Teaching and Learning

Cornell’s response to generative AI (‘GenAI’) in teaching and learning is built around seven core principles:

  • the integrity of the faculty-student relation.
  • a commitment to experimentation, evidence and learning from experience.
  • the centrality of faculty judgement and expertise in the classroom.
  • responsiveness to real student needs and uses.
  • recognition of both AI ‘goods’ and ‘harms’. 
  • respect for institutional and disciplinary heterogeneity.
  • the extension and renewal of Cornell’s core mission and values.

Established in spring 2024 with all-college representation including faculty, staff and students, the Cornell GenAI Education Working Group, a part of the university-wide AI Advisory Council, is the central place in which new ideas, policies and practices around GenAI in the classroom are being worked out at Cornell.  Key initiatives to date include: 

  • a spring 2025 all-campus survey on GenAI use and perspectives with more than 700 faculty and nearly 2000 student responses (for full quantitative results for faculty see: GenAI2025_Faculty_FINAL; for student results see: GenAI2025_Student_FINAL). Qualitative results are being analyzed and will be shared back in summary form in later fall 2025).
  • through our Center for Teaching Innovation, campus-wide programs in Critical AI Literacy (under development with the CU Library); faculty consultations; and faculty, department and college workshops and events oriented to both ‘leaning in’ and ‘leaning out’ of GenAI (‘leaning in’: thoughtful and creative adoption of GenAI tools where these serve course-level and wider learning goals; ‘leaning out’: effective avoidance or steering around GenAI (for example, through modifications to assessment, assignment and course design) where these undermine or replace rather than enhance student learning;
  • ongoing work with Cornell Information Technologies, vendors and educational researchers to support experimental and pilot tool adoptions, evidence-based standards of review, and more effective research around the learning impacts and efficacies of GenAI tools;
  • our Teaching Innovation Case Series, built from our annual Creative Teaching Awards designed to recognize and share new and emerging teaching practices across Cornell colleges, including (to date) ‘Creative Responses to Generative AI’ (2024) and ‘Innovative Assessment Practices’ (2025).  
  • guidance on syllabus statements and a new icon-based system for expressing course-level GenAI policies to clarify course and instructor expectations;
  • ‘AI+AI’: efforts to strengthen and update Cornell’s academic integrity procedures to better reflect the presence of GenAI, including better models for attributing student use of GenAI in assignments or course settings; efforts to streamline, strengthen, and reform the university’s academic integrity system; and the development of evidentiary standards and processes appropriate to the adjudication of GenAI-related violations of academic integrity.

Convened by the OVPAI, members of the Education Working Group work closely with the Center for Teaching Innovation; Cornell Information Technologies; Cornell’s AI Research Initiative; department, college and university leadership; and faculty, staff and students from across the university to continue to evolve and update Cornell’s response to Generative AI in teaching and learning.  Check out the links above, and back with this page regularly for updates. 

Questions, ideas, or want to get involved?  Contact the OVPAI at: vpai@cornell.edu.
