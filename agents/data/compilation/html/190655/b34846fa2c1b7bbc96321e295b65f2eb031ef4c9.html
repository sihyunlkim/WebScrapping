City Tech AI Symposium: Building the Future of AI — Together

Join us for a day of innovation, collaboration, and AI discussions. Engage with industry leaders, learn from faculty experts and students. Participate in interactive AI activities and speak to industry representatives. This is your opportunity to contribute to AI discussions at the College, connect, and foster collaborative relationships.

Location: Academic Complex

Date: Thursday, May 8, 2025

Time: 9:30 am - 3:00 pm

Register Now

PROGRAM

9:30 AM
Welcome & Opening Event

Welcome address by Executive Administration (President/Provost). Introduction to the AI Symposium and its objectives, and a brief overview of City Tech's commitment to AI innovation. Includes "AI Time Capsule" activity.

10:00 AM
Keynote: Navigating the AI Revolution [Mark Roman - Info Tech Group]

Presentation by Info-Tech on the evolving AI landscape in higher education. Topics include emerging trends, ethical considerations, and innovation opportunities in teaching, learning, and research.

10:45 AM
AI at City Tech: College Initiatives [David Smith - City Tech]

An overview of AI-related projects, sessions, survey results, and goals from across City Tech. A look into successes, challenges, and the evolving AI roadmap within the college.

11:00 AM
Roundtable: The Evolving Classroom [Moderator: Rita Uddin - City Tech]

Panel discussion on the role of AI in education: can it enhance collaboration, engagement, and personalization, or does it pose a disruptive force to academic norms? Featuring students, faculty, and technologists.

  • Panelists:
    • Twaambo Kabosha, City Tech SGA President 2024-2025
    • Kyshia Anderson, City Tech student - Business & Technology of Fashion - Business and Fashion
    • Benito Mendoza, Associate Professor - Computer Engineering Technology
    • Jose Diaz, Director - Academic Technologies and Online Learning (AtoL)
12:00 PM
Break

Light refreshments served.

12:30 PM
Session 1: ML/AI Tools for Research [Viviana Acquaviva - City Tech]

Viviana Acquaviva (City Tech) presents practical use cases and tools for leveraging machine learning in academic research.

1:15 PM
Session 2: Ethical Consideration in AI Development and Deployment — An Introduction to Ethical Challenges in Legal Practice Involving AI [Marissa Moran - City Tech]

Marissa Moran (City Tech) discusses the legal and ethical challenges posed by the growing use of AI in law, emphasizing the importance for legal professionals to understand these emerging risks as AI becomes more widely adopted in legal practice.

2:00 PM
Session 3: AI and Computer Science [Christelle Scharff - Pace University]

Christelle Scharff (Pace University) explores how AI is transforming the field of computer science and how students can engage with emerging technologies.

2:45 PM
Session 4: AI-Powered Personalized Learning [Miguel Amigot - ibl.ai]

Miguel Amigot (ibl.ai) demonstrates how universities are implementing AI-native teaching and learning, focused on faculty support and student success.

Keynote Speaker

Mark Roman, Info-Tech Group

Mark Roman is the Managing Partner for Education and works with members throughout the higher education and K-12 sectors around the world. As a global Managing Partner, he is responsible for the delivery of Info-Tech services to members and the alignment of research with the education sector's needs.

Mark has served on several boards in the education sector and has also run a successful IT strategy consulting firm specializing in education clients.

Mark has an MBA from Queen's University, a B.Math (Computer Science) from the University of Waterloo, and he is a Project Management Professional. He has three children, five computers, and seven guitars, and in his remaining time, he is fascinated by military history.
