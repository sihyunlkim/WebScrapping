User University Policies

The following university policies govern access to data and the use of institutional systems.

Accessing University Systems: IT Security Standards

Information security standards provide a set of best practices to safeguard Masons and your data, assets, and resources. These security standards are based on the National Institute of Standards and Technologys Special Publication (NIST SP) 800-53 moderate baseline controls, scoped and tailored to the context of higher education institutions. NIST SP 800-53 is a widely accepted industry standard, most often used by U.S. federal and state agencies. The IT Security Standard describes controls as they apply to systems and processes based upon a high/medium/low classification. For details, see IT Security Standards.

Code of Conduct

Authorization to use university computing services and applications is based on an individual’s affiliation with George Mason, that individual’s role and responsibilities, and the designated category of the system. George Mason employees and contractors accessing George Mason’s internal networks are required to follow IT Security Standards. and university policies.

University Policies

All authorized users of George Masons computing resources are required to comply with University Policy 1301 Responsible Use of Computing, and the following policies, where applicable:

  • University Policy 1114 Data Stewardship
  • University Policy 1118 Compliance with the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act (HIPAA)
  • University Policy 1122 FERPA Compliance
  • University Policy 1119 Classified Information and Personnel Security Clearances
  • University Policy 1306 Banner and Related Administrative Systems Security
  • University Policy Number 1311 Information Technology Security Program
  • University Policy 1316 Controlled Unclassified Information

Note: George Mason-managed computers will lock after 30 minutes of inactivity.

Banned Software

Purchase a George Mason-managed Endpoint Device

ITS has identified pre-configured endpoint device models from select manufacturers for purchase and has tested these pre-configured endpoints with George Masons standard software image.

Note: As most positions are funded for one endpoint device, ITS recommends laptops for employees who telework or work remotely.

If your department has inquiries about these standards and wishes to purchase a model not listed on this page, or needs assistance with purchasing a computer, ITS will work with you to try to meet your needs. For assistance, please submit a Desktop Consultation request.

ITS-approved devices are approved for purchase with George Mason funds. If you need further computer purchasing guidance, seeFiscal Learning and Engagements Computer Purchasing Guide. For assistance, request a Desktop Consultation

Computers must be purchased from one of the following approved vendors

  • Dell machines:Dell, Patriot Tech
  • Microsoft Surface:CDW-G, Patriot Tech
  • Apple:Apple e-commerce, CDW-G, Patriot Tech
