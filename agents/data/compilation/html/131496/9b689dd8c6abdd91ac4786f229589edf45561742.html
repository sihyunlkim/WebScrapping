CNDLS AI Lab

  • CNDLS AI Lab
  • Overview
  • Apply
  • Resources

Overview

The CNDLS AI Lab is a new initiative designed to help departments and programs develop literacy and fluency with AI, engage with AI in teaching and research, and reflect on the impact of AI on their curriculum with care and creativity.

Apply here

Artificial intelligence is transforming how we teach, learn, and research. For universities, this is a turning point: AI is reshaping writing, research, assessment, and professional practice across disciplines.

At Georgetown, we believe in engaging with AI critically, creatively, and ethically. The CNDLS AI Lab is not prescriptive; it is a reflective space where departments can explore how AI affects their field, their students, and their curriculum, all while staying grounded in Georgetown’s mission and our values.

What we offer

Through the Lab, departments will partner with CNDLS and choose their own path through tailored workshops across three core areas:

  • AI Literacy & Fluency

    Build a shared foundation. Understand how AI tools are impacting your discipline, research, and teaching—and explore their limitations, risks, and ethical implications.

  • AI & Pedagogy

    Rethink assignments and assessments. Explore how students are already using AI, and design for authentic, rigorous learning that strengthens—not replaces—human skills.

  • AI & Curriculum

    Look ahead. Map the impact AI is having on your program, your curriculum, and your students’ learning.

How it works

The work is intended to foster deep reflection on the changing nature of education and research, and how that manifests in your context. Engagements are tailored to your department’s unique context.

Each process will look different, per the needs of each group. Overall, the Lab will likely follow a flexible, two-phase process:

Phase 1: Needs Assessment — An exploratory 90–120 minute session to help us connect on your goals, challenges, and opportunities, and identify a departmental liaison.

Phase 2: Tailored Engagement — A custom-designed pathway that supports orienting your program or department in literacy, pedagogy, and curriculum through workshops, design sessions, or ongoing faculty learning communities.

CNDLS will support participants through a variety of resources including the Applied AI in Higher Education Microcredential, our AI Teaching Guide, and other relevant resources.

The work is intended to foster deep, communal reflection on the changing nature of education and research, and how that manifests in each participating group’s context. Participation is voluntary and opt-in. Engagements are tailored to your department’s unique context.

If your school, department, or program is interested, please get in touch to begin the dialogue.
