Teaching in the AI Era: Policies, Syllabi and Student Learning

July 23, 2024

The Center for Teaching and Learning will host Teaching in the AI Era: Policies, Syllabi, and Student Learning from 11:45 a.m. to 12:45 p.m., Wednesday, Aug. 14 on Microsoft Teams. This Wednesday Webinar will share innovative teaching practices that address generative artificial intelligence (AI) tools such as Microsoft Copilot and ChatGPT.

Facilitated by Dr. Carol Cirulli Lanham, professor of instruction in the School of Economic, Political and Policy Science, and Dr. Sarah Moore, associate professor of instruction in the Naveen Jindal School of Management, the course is designed for faculty, graduate teaching assistants and others interested in teaching. The session will examine the development of effective syllabi policies about generative AI as well as how to refine assignment descriptions to promote academic honesty. The goal is to review and share strategies to enhance student learning, promote ethical behavior and increase instructional success.

Participating in the session fulfills part of the requirements for the Graduate Teaching Certificate and Postdoctoral Teaching Certificate programs.

Register by 3 p.m., Tuesday, Aug. 13.
