Orange Connect for Faculty

What is Orange Connect?

Orange Connect is an early alert tool that helps faculty express their concerns for students so that the students, their pathway coach, and/or advisor are all aware of those concerns. This helps alleviate some of the confusion associated with reiterating concerns to several individuals and assists advisors and pathway coaches in helping students. A student should only have to tell their story once. 

Orange Connect empowers our college community to work more efficiently, sustainably, and holistically by simplifying how all SUNY Orange students navigate their network of support. Our efforts as faculty and staff will be connected as we are able to see how each student is being supported, and ensure we are able to keep them on their pathway to success.

FAQ

How do I set up office hours in Orange Connect? 

There are three options for setting up your office hours in Orange Connect:
1. Scheduled appointments – This will allow students to schedule their own appointments during the
designated office hours.
2. Walk-ins – This will show your office hours but students will not be able to schedule their own
appointments.
3. Scheduled and walk-in appointments. Students will be able to schedule appointments at those
designated times and also you can take walk-ins who drop by.

What happens after a flag is raised?

Once a flag is raised by a faculty member, both the student and an individual from the appropriate support area (i.e. advising, pathway coaches) are notified. The advisor or pathway coach performs outreach in an attempt to start a conversation with the student about the concerns raised by the faculty member. The information provided by faculty via comments alongside the flags, can help facilitate a more productive conversation with the student.

Flags are resolved in two instances. Outreach is successful and a conversation and plan is made to help the student get back on track for success in the course. Outreach is not successful even though the support area has tried several methods to contact the student. Either way the faculty member is notified.

When do progress reports come out? 

  • the 1st round of OC progress reporting (4th week) 
  • you will receive an email prompt to log in and provide feedback to students as needed for all of your course sections
  • please note, this is a replacement for the "U-grades" and feedback previously submitted via Banner
  • not all student will require a flag or intervention but this is also an opportunity to reflect positive reinforcement "kudos"
  • follow the instructions - log in to Orange Connect to access the survey grid, simply check the box next to the particular student's name for whom you would like to report progress. You will have options to add comments.
  • click Submit. If you don't have any progress to report, it is important that you click Submit, so we know you reviewed the survey. You will receive a confirmation email.
  • items you raise will be addressed by the appropriate individuals from our institution.
  • please note, this is NOT the only opportunity you have to provide feedback or refer a student for support
  • if you did not have enough information to provide feedback prior to the close day you can still enter feedback through Tracking in OC

 Do I need to write a comment for every flag I raise?

Writing comments with flags is highly encouraged so that the instructor, pathway coach and advisor are all on the same page. Comments allow for the pathway coach or advisor to have a more personal conversation with the student about their struggles in your class. Without comments, advisors and coaches do not have the whole story, which can lead to confusion. Students do not see these comments.

Do I have to wait for a progress report to raise a flag?

Flags can be raised at any time during the semester. It is best to raise flags as soon as a concern is identified by the instructor. The progress reports are meant to remind faculty to provide feedback to the students (including kudos) at targeted weeks in the semester.

Why should I give a kudos?

Kudos allow you to recognize a student for their great work or improvements in your course. This may help students stay engaged and motivated in the course.

Doesn’t Orange Connect make my relationship with my students less personal?

Orange Connect early alerts, flags, kudos, etc. are not meant to take the place of one-on-one conversations we regularly have with students; instead, it allows faculty to build on the relationships we have already formed with them. By alerting students, it opens up more opportunities to engage in a conversation between the student, instructor, pathway coach, and advisor. Orange Connect can direct students to additional supports to help enhance their success.
