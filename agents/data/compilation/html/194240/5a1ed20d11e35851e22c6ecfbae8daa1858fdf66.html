Website Guidelines

 

1. General Guidelines

  • Institutional Advancement is primarily responsible for managing and maintaining the SUNY Orange Website, in conjunction with Information and Technology Services (ITS).

 

2. Web Page Manager Guidelines

2.1 Templates

  • All SUNY Orange web pages follow specific templates that follow the website's overall design. If you need certain features on your website, please contact the Office of Communications. 

2.2 Navigation

  • If you need a new web page created, please contact the Office of Communications.
  • If you need pages/links in the left-hand navigation menu updating, please contact the Office of Communications.
  • For the main top "mega-menu", the items listed there have been determined through user-surveys, and website traffic analytics. If you believe another link should be added there, please contact the Office of Communications.

2.3 Preferred development tools

  • All Web Page Managers must use our CMS (OUCampus) to edit the website.

2.4 Logos & headings.

  • Logos are not permitted unless cleared by the Office of Communications.
  • Please only use Heading 3 and Heading 4 on your webpages. DO NOT use Heading 1 or 2.
  • If you need to change the large orange title at the top of the page, please contact the Office of Communications.

2.5 Fonts

  • For most regular text, please use the "Paragraph" style, and Bold, Italics were necessary. Please do NOT use the Underline as this looks like a link to a website visitor.
  • Please do not use other font colors, families, or sizes, unless directed by the Office of Communications.
  • If you need a larger font for emphasis, or some additional graphical feature (such as a shield/accordion/table) please contact the Office of Communications.

2.6 Colors

  • The website should automatically take care of colors for text, headings, tables, links etc. Please do not use additional colors for fonts or tables, unless directed by the Office of Communications.

2.7 Images

  • Images must
    • Be representative of SUNY Orange;
    • Be produced with correct production methods (e.g. Image resolution must be 72 dpi. All images must be either in JP(E)G or GIF formats depending on the type of the image);
    • Be proportionally correct;
    • Be a small file size so as to download quickly (this can be done in the CMS if needed).
    • Contain correct URL paths (src attribute) so that the images appear correctly on the web page;
    • Conform to the copyright guidelines;
  • If you need photos of SUNY Orange people or places, contact the Department of Institutional Advancement (x4402).
  • Flyers, posters and other textual information should not be used - post an image, but have the text information next to, or below, the image. This will then meet accessibility standards.
  • If you are taking your own photos for use on your website, please obtain a release from any persons shown in the photo, from our Media Guide page.

2.8 Links and navigation

  • All web pages except for the home page should explicitly indicate the user's location. This can be easily accomplished by using the so-called "breadcrumbs" (e.g. Unit Home Page &gt;&gt; Programs & Degrees) .
  • When linking within SUNY Orange's website, only relative links are allowed
  • Links from a College page to any non-College site should indicate the user is leaving the College's website. This can be done simply being evident in the text, or by adding "(External Link)" next to, or below the link.

2.9 Multimedia/Video

  • For animations, video and audio please contact the Office of Communication (ideally before the animation, video, or audio has been completed!)

2.10 Information redundancy

  • Duplication of information should be avoided. If the information to be included on a web page already exists on another web page, a link to the latter should be provided. Ultimately the information should be on the website of the owner of that information.

2.11 Commercial advertising

  • Commercial advertising is not permitted on pages published on SUNY Orange Web servers. This includes banner advertisements as well as graphic images that promote a commercial service and some text links. No graphic or text may imply SUNY Orange's endorsement of commercial products or services. A disclaimer should be displayed if non-endorsement is not evident from the context.

2.12 Copyright

  • Copyright laws apply to electronic publishing as well as to print publishing. Publishers must have permission from the copyright owners to copy and display text, graphics, or photographs on their pages. In the alternative, publishers must have a reasonable basis for believing their use of copyrighted materials of others constitutes fair use or that the materials are in the public domain. Please see our Copyright Guidelines for more information.

 

 

3. Personal Business & SUNY Orange web resources

  • SUNY Orange resources may be used to create Web pages about an individual or an individual’s interests but may not be used to create Web pages for personal business, personal gain, or partisan political purposes, except as permitted by other College policies or by law.

4. Additional notes

4.1 Uploading web pages to the web server

  • All web pages must be approved by the Assistant Director of Communications for New Media before it goes live.
  • If a web page violates our style guidelines, or our copyright guidelines, or any othe best practices, the web page may be declined for publication. If this happens you will receive a note in OUCampus providing the reason it was declined.
  • Unit web pages must be updated on a regular basis. In case a web page with outdated content is encountered by the Webmaster, the unit will be notified. If updated content is not provided the Office of Communications, at its discretion, will update or remove the content.

 

4.2 Technical Support

  • Contact the Office of Communications with any issues you have with editing the website.
