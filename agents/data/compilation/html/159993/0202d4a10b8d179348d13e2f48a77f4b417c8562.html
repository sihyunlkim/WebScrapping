Dr. Fronczak, a Polish-American physician born in Buffalo in 1874, was a pioneer in his field. He earned his M.D. in 1897, becoming the first Polish-American to do so. A dedicated public health advocate, he served as Buffalo's Health Commissioner and played a key role in WWI Polish war relief efforts. He represented the U.S. at numerous international medical conferences and, post-WWII, worked with the UN to aid Poland. Fronczak's legacy as a humanitarian and a champion of Polish-American relations endures.
