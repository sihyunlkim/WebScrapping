Faculty Committees

Faculty Advisory Board

All Department Chairs and Center Directors of the Medical School, as well as the Dean of the Graduate School, Dean of the School of Health Professions, and Dean of the O’Donnell School of Public Health, serve as the official advisory body to the provost regarding academic affairs.

Faculty Development Committee of the School of Health Professions

The Faculty Development Committee of the School of Health Professions consists of Department Chairs, Academic Program Directors, and the Assistant/Associate Dean(s).

Faculty Assembly of the UT Southwestern School of Health Professions

This body consists of all members of the faculty of the School of Health Professions. Full assembly meetings are held each Spring and Fall.

Faculty Advisory Council

The Faculty Advisory Council (FAC) is a UT System committee comprised of faculty members representing component institutions. The purpose of the committee is to facilitate the flow of ideas and information between and among the Board of Regents, the System administration, and the component institutions. There are usually four meetings per academic year in Austin. UT Southwestern has one representative from the Medical School and one representative from the School of Health Professions, plus an alternate.

Get to Know Your Colleagues

UT Southwestern is known for highly productive and rewarding collaborations. For the purpose of attracting trainees and collaborations, new tenure-accruing Assistant Professors are introduced to the UT Southwestern community at the annual New Faculty Research Forum. A useful tool for learning the wealth of expertise among your colleagues is the Faculty Finder, through which you can search for colleagues by name or by research area.

You are strongly encouraged to post your profile in the Faculty Information System upon arrival at UT Southwestern and to keep it updated either on your own or by assigning access to an assistant. Information in your profile will be searchable by research interests and will help attract trainees to your research group.

Missions

  • To improve health care in our community, Texas, our nation, and the world through innovation and education.
  • To educate the next generation of leaders in patient care, biomedical science, and disease prevention.
  • To conduct high-impact, internationally recognized research.
  • To deliver patient care that brings UT Southwestern's scientific advances to the bedside—focusing on quality, safety and service.

Organization of the Medical Center

Part of The University of Texas System, UT Southwestern is governed by the UT Board of Regents. President Daniel K. Podolsky leads the medical center, which includes three degree-granting institutions. The three schools train more than 4,400 medical, graduate, and health professions students, residents, and postdoctoral scholars each year. Faculty are appointed to the School of Health Professions or the Medical School; faculty are also non-divisional members of the Graduate School, but to mentor graduate students, faculty members must join a graduate school program.

  • UT Southwestern Graduate School of Biomedical Sciences
  • UT Southwestern School of Health Professions

Six-Year Plan

UT Southwestern’s Six-Year Plan is a rolling strategic plan updated every two years by committees appointed by the President. The committees, comprised mostly of faculty, help the institution prioritize its needs and suggest where to use resources. Six-Year Plans are available in the Health Sciences Digital Library and Learning Center.
