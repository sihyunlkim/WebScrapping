Tips for using AI tools

  • Ask clear and specific questions.
  • Use relevant keywords for more accurate responses.
  • Break down complex requests into simpler parts.
  • Provide feedback to enhance AI tool responses.
  • Familiarize yourself with the AI tool's specific features and commands.
  • Customize the AI tool according to its supported features.
  • Practice with the AI tool regularly to understand its capabilities and limitations.
  • Avoid including private personal information.

From: Efficient Prompt Engineering for Librarians, Yinlin Chen, Week 1 - Introduction to Generative AI and AI chatbots lecture slides, August 2024

LLM AI Tools

Provided for UMaine students, faculty, and staff

  • Copilot
    An AI productivity tool that is embedded across Microsoft 365 applications. UMaine students, faculty, and staff can access Copilot from your UMaine Launchpad from the M365 Copilot / Microsoft 365 button. When you use Copilot through your UMaine credentials, enterprise data protection ensures that your data is private and secure and not used to train models.
  • Gemini
    Gemini is a multimodal LLM that can generate text, images, audio, and video. It is conversational and strongly integrated across Google apps. UMaine students, faculty, and staff can access Gemini from your UMaine Google Apps. When you use Gemini through your UMaine credentials, your data is private and secure and not used to train models.
  • Perplexity
    Perplexity is a conversational AI search engine that provides real-time answers with cited sources. It has a variety of AI models to choose from. It works on a freemium model, with Pro subscribers having access to more features.
  • NotebookLM
    This is a Google research and writing assistant that lets you interact with documents you create or upload. It only works with the information you provide. UMaine students, faculty, and staff can access NotebookLM from your UMaine Google Apps. When you use NotebookLM through your UMaine credentials, your data is private and secure and not used to train models.

Available on the web - free, freemium, or for purchase.

  • ChatGPT
    A conversational AI tool that can be used for text, code, and general knowledge tasks. It operates on a freemium model. Free-tier users have a limited number of messages they can send using GPT-4o within a specified time frame. Once this limit is reached, users are switched to GPT-4o Mini, a lighter version of GPT-4o.
  • Claude
    A conversational AI tool that is great for text based tasks like creative writing and summarization. It has a free version as well as multiple price points. Higher tiers give access to more features.
