Choosing the Right AI Tool

Kris Baldwin, Virtual Education Specialist
October 2025

Connect with Virtual Education

Information Technology Services (ITS) recently announced the addition of Google Gemini and Google NotebookLM to our options for enterprise level AI assistants. Here’s a quick look at the two versions of Copilot that are available, plus an introduction to the two new resources from Google. For more information, visit the ITS AI at Iowa State site or Virtual Education | AI Tools and How To Use Them on MyExtension. 

Copilot Chat is a conversational assistant powered by large language models available to all ISU staff with a NetID at no cost. It’s great for brainstorming, summarizing, and answering general questions. It’s the preferred enterprise tool at ISU due to its FERPA and HIPAA-compliant data protection and secure integration with institutional systems. 

Microsoft 365 Copilot is deeply integrated into Outlook, Word, Teams, and other Microsoft apps. It works with your ISU emails, meetings, and documents to provide personalized support—like drafting emails or summarizing meetings. It’s also data compliant, but costs $30/month. 

Google Gemini is now also approved for use at ISU with no cost associated. It integrates with Google Workspace rather than Microsoft, but is useful for general productivity tasks. It offers a flexible alternative for creative and collaborative work. 

Google’s NotebookLM offers a unique approach to generative AI. Unlike other tools that search the web or institutional data, NotebookLM works exclusively with the materials you provide—summarizing, organizing, and generating content based on your uploaded documents. It’s especially useful for creating audio or video overviews, timelines, mind maps, or other materials, but has a wide variety of uses when you want to dig into specific information. 

Each tool has its strengths—choose the one that best fits your task and data sensitivity needs. For secure, work-related tasks, Copilot remains the top choice – and it also assisted with the writing of this article. Nothing wrong with a bit of shameless self-promotion, Copilot! 

Some of this information is specific to Iowa State University Extension and Outreach. If you have questions about using AI in a non-ISU setting, please contact virtualed@iastate.edu.
