Student Code of Conduct​

University of Hawai`i – West O`ahu exists so that the people it serves have learning and enrichment opportunities to improve their quality of life and their standard of living. UH West O`ahu supports a positive educational environment that will benefit student success. To ensure this vision, UH West O`ahu has established guidelines for the redress of grievances by individuals accused of prohibited conduct in such proceedings. In addition, UH West O`ahu has established a Student Code of Conduct to ensure the protection of student rights and the health and safety of the UH West O`ahu community, as well as to support the efficient operation of UH West O`ahu programs.

Key Points:

  • There are 23 prohibited conduct or behaviors.
  • Some examples include but not limited to plagiarism, hazing, misrepresentation of facts, furnishing false information, and physical abuse.
  • Any one may report an incident that involves a student’s conduct or behavior.
  • Witness, Stand Up, Speak Out and Report What Matters! Each members of the UH West O`ahu community shares the responsibility to speak out, take action, and report concerns specially hazing when something isn’t right. 
  • The Student Conduct Officer (SCO) works with both parties (the Complainant and the Respondent).
  • Both parties has rights and responsibilities under the grievance process.
  • The goal of the conduct process is to educate.
  • UH West O`ahu established a grievance process and procedure.
