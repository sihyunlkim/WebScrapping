Q&A

IMPORTANT: The Q&A's on this guide are illustrative of the types of conversations you can have in ChatGPT, and were copied over from chats that librarians had with ChatGPT beginning in March 2023. 

Question: What is ChatGPT? 

ChatGPT Response: "ChatGPT is an AI tool that responds to your questions in plain language. Think of it like a smart assistant that can brainstorm, summarize, explain, or draft text — but you still need to check its work."

Question: Explain how to Get Started with ChatGPT in 3 Easy Steps.

ChatGPT Response: 

  • Go to chat.openai.com (create a free account if you don’t have one).
  • Type a question as if you were asking a tutor or colleague. You can:
    • Copy-paste text (up to a few pages) directly into ChatGPT and ask:
      “Summarize this article in 3 bullet points.”
    • Share a link (sometimes works, sometimes not — best to copy-paste the text).
    • Upload a file (Word, PDF, or text) if you are using the paid version (ChatGPT Plus).
    • Always be specific: “Summarize the article for a business student at an introductory level” is better than just “Summarize this.”
  • Review & Refine (This is the key step!)
    • ChatGPT works best when you treat it like a back-and-forth conversation. After the first answer, you can ask it to adjust, clarify, or give examples. Try prompts like:
      • Make it shorter or longer
        • “Can you summarize this in 3 sentences instead of 3 paragraphs?”
      • Change the tone
        • “Explain this like I’m new to business.”
        • “Rephrase this in professional language I could use in a presentation.”
      • Give examples or comparisons
        • “Can you give me two real-world examples of a company using SWOT analysis?”
        • “Explain this idea with an analogy.”
      • Help with structure
        • “Turn these notes into an outline I can build a paper around.”
        • “Organize this information into bullet points.”
      • Check understanding
        • “Ask me 3 quiz questions to test if I understood the concept of opportunity cost.”
        • “Can you explain this in simpler words?”
      • Brainstorm alternatives
        • “Give me 5 different ways to start my introduction.”
        • “Suggest possible titles for my case study.”
Question: What is important for students to remember when using ChatGPT?

ChatGPT Response: 

  • Fact-check everything
    • ChatGPT doesn’t “know” things the way a textbook or database does — it predicts text. That means answers can sound confident but still be wrong, outdated, or missing details.
    • Always cross-check numbers, dates, and names against reliable sources (your textbook, library databases, government reports, or professional publications).
    • Tip: Use it for drafting or exploring ideas, not as the final authority.
  • Use your own words
    • Copy-pasting AI text directly into an assignment can lead to plagiarism or academic integrity issues.
    • Instead, treat ChatGPT as a starting point — take its output, compare it with your notes and readings, and then rewrite it in your own voice.
    • This practice helps you learn the material, while also making sure your work reflects your understanding.
  • Be transparent
    • Some instructors may ask you to disclose if you used AI. Others may forbid it altogether.
    • If you’re unsure, check the syllabus or ask your professor how they want you to handle AI use.
    • When allowed, you can note something like: “I used ChatGPT to brainstorm ideas for my outline but wrote the draft myself.”
  • Protect your privacy
    • Don’t paste in personal details (student ID, phone number, home address, etc.).
    • Avoid putting in sensitive or unpublished work (like a draft research paper you’ll later submit for grading).
    • Remember: you control what you share — keep it limited to what’s needed for the task.
  • Keep learning in focus
    • AI is a support tool, not a shortcut. If you use it to avoid doing the thinking yourself, you miss out on the practice you’ll need for exams and real-world problem-solving.
    • Ask yourself: Is this helping me learn, or just helping me finish faster?
  • Experiment with purpose
    • Don’t be afraid to “play” with ChatGPT — ask the same question in different ways, try different levels of detail, and see how the responses change.
    • This will help you get comfortable with prompting, and also make it clear that the first answer is rarely the best one.
