Learn to Utilize AI and Stay Productive

Faculty at KSU need to be prepared to use Generative AI appropriately for productivity and with their students in class. Familiarity with AI will help faculty to stay at the forefront of technological advancements, better prepare students for the evolving job market, and contribute to interdisciplinary collaboration and research. It also positions faculty to address the ethical and societal implications of AI, fostering a more comprehensive and forward-thinking educational experience.

  • AI Guidance for Faculty

    Faculty wanting to use Artificial Intelligence to improve productivity in grading, writing, and research should be cautious entering student data or intellectual property into these systems. To ensure data security and FERPA compliance, faculty should sign into Microsoft's Copilot using their KSU Microsoft credentials (NetID). To be sure you are using the protected enterprise version of this chat look for the following statement above the chat box in green: Your personal and company data are protected in this chat.

    To log in, visit Copilot. AI is advancing very quickly, and this guidance is likely to change with future advances. If you have questions, submit a DLI ServiceNow ticket for assistance.

These professional development resources will help you to:

  • Develop a foundational knowledge of generative AI tools
  • Understand the impact of ethics on the use of Gen AI in higher education and craft policies for your own classroom. 
  • Engineer prompts and submit them to a Gen AI tool for productivity purposes. 
  • Use AI to support course design and facilitation to enhance your students’ learning experience.
  • Use Gen AI to facilitate your students’ engagement and support.

Professional Development

Begin your learning journey with a microlearning video, infographic, self-directed module, job aid, or other resource that requires less than an hour of your time. An asterisk (*) at the end of a title indicates eligibility for a micro-credential upon successful completion of the professional development item.

Resources

  • What is Generative AI?

    Explore our Generative AI Reference Guide to learn both the key benefits and limitations of using Gen AI in higher ed.

  • Generative AI Explained

    The Generative AI Microlearning [VIDEO] will assist you in understanding common terminology and popular AI models through real-world applications and examples, while also reviewing current limitations and ethical considerations, especially related to output model bias.

  • Introduction to Prompt Engineering

    Explore our Introduction to Prompt Engineering Infographic to learn the what and the why of prompt engineering.

  • Prompt Engineering RTF Framework

    Use the Prompt Engineering RTF Framework Infographic to create effective ChatGPT prompts.

  • Prompt Engineering

    Watch AI Prompt Engineering Microlearning [VIDEO] to better understand how to create effective prompts and submit them to a GenAI tool for productivity purposes.

  • Faculty Considerations for AI Use

    Explore additional considerations when allowing or requiring AI use in any KSU course.

  • AI Use Syllabus Statements

    Worried about what to tell your students about AI Use in your course? Explore these sample syllabus statements you can use.

Self-Directed Modules

  • Generative AI Foundations*

    Explore current technologies in Generative AI Foundations. Examine their practical applications and consider the ethics of integrating AI into educational practices. 

  • Using Large Language Models for Prompt Engineering*

    Learn how to effectively interact with large language models (LLMs) like Microsoft Copilot.

  • Using Copilot for Effective Course Design*

    Learn to confidently and ethically use Copilot for a variety of course design and content generation tasks.

  • Innovative Ways to Use Copilot for Student Engagement*

    Create interactive course content (self-study guides, flash cards, games, simulations, an discussion forms) with Copilot for Student Engagement for practicing concepts and increasing cognitive engagement in your students.

  • Copilot for Assessment Redesign: Academic Strategies Self-Paced Course*

    Redesign your existing course assessments to effectively incorporate Copilot whilel maintaining academic integrity and promoting deeper learning.

  • Rethinking Assessments for Academic Integrity Using Generative AI

    Rethinking Assessments examines strategies for redesigning assessments to be AI-resistant and for incorporating generative AI to promote critical thinking and original work.

Webinars & Workshops

  • GenAI for Course Design & Student Engagement (GACD)*

    Learn skills to confidently and ethically use generative AI tools  (specifically, Microsoft Copilot) to assist in course design, student engagement and workflow.
