Guidelines for the Responsible Use of Artificial Intelligence (AI) in Graduate Research and Writing

These guidelines provide best practices for using AI tools such as ChatGPT, Microsoft Copilot, and Google Gemini in graduate-level research and writing. They are intended to support ethical, transparent, and academically sound use of AI technologies. These are advisory recommendations and may be updated as tools and standards change.

Adopted 9/4/2025

Appropriate Uses of AI

Graduate students may use AI tools for the following purposes:

  • Brainstorming and developing ideas
  • Editing grammar and style
  • Improving clarity and communication, especially for non-native English speakers

 

Limitations and Risks

Students should be aware of the following limitations:

  • AI may produce inaccurate, biased, or misleading content
  • Confidentiality is not guaranteed; input may be stored or reused
  • Uploading unpublished manuscripts or sensitive data is not permitted
  • AI-generated content may violate plagiarism or copyright policies

 

Guidelines for Research Use

  • Verify all AI-generated content using credible, peer-reviewed sources
  • Document/record prompts and outputs if AI is used in idea development or analysis
  • Do not use AI to generate any portions of academic writing unless explicitly permitted
  • Avoid sharing confidential or proprietary information with AI platforms

 

Guidelines for Writing Use

  • Grammar and style checks are generally acceptable and do not require disclosure
  • Generating substantive content with AI may be considered plagiarism
  • Review publisher and journal policies before submitting work
  • Disclose AI use when required, typically in the Methods section, Acknowledgments, or cover letter

 

 Ethical and Professional Standards

  • AI tools cannot be listed as authors
  • Seek approval from your advisor and all co-authors before using AI in collaborative work
  • Verify all AI-generated data or claims
  • Do not upload sensitive subject data to AI platforms
  • Understand the terms of service and data practices of any AI tool you use

 

Journal and Agency Policies

  • Reviewers must not use AI to analyze confidential submissions
  • Agencies such as the NIH prohibit AI use in peer review
  • Most academic journals require disclosure of AI use and prohibit AI-generated figures or text unless clearly described in the research method

 

Examples of Publisher Policies

  • Elsevier Generative AI Policy
  • Taylor & Francis AI Policy
  • Springer AI Editorial Policy

 

Summary of Expectations

  • Use AI tools thoughtfully and ethically
  • Disclose AI use when required
  • Do not upload confidential or unpublished work
  • Maintain full responsibility for the content you submit
  • Misuse or undisclosed AI use may be considered academic misconduct
