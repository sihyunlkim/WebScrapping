Interfolio @ UConn

What is Interfolio?

Interfolio hosts a suite of faculty information system modules geared towards higher education institutions. UConn's purchase of the following modules are part of the university's effort to streamline faculty data and processes related to evaluations and other workflow solutions.

Access interfolio

Faculty Activity Reporting Module 

Interfolio on-demand support can be accessed by calling 1 (833) 844-2118 Monday through Friday, 9:00am-6:00pm Eastern Time. Please also refer to the Interfolio Support Website and the below drop down menu for technical assistance.

For UConn specific questions, contact interfolio@uconn.edu.

Faculty Activity Reporting launched in October 2024 for faculty to being reviewing and updating their profiles and activities. This includes reviewing data migrated from HuskyDM, accepting activities identifed by Interfolio Data Services (IDS), linking with ORCID or uploading Google Scholar data, and adding any manual activities as necessary. 

Now, the Provost's Office and BPIR are working together to develop templates for annual reporting, PTR/PR, and more. Below is the status of each template in progress:

AVAILABLE NOW:

  • Standard CV Template (these can be customized for individuals or units)
  • Annual Activity Report

IN PROGRESS:

  • PTR Form
  • CIRE Form
  • Customized forms for individual units

If you are interested in customizing a report for your unit, please contact interfolio@uconn.edu.

Data from Interfolio Data Services (IDS) Expand

UConn utilizes Interfolio Data Service (IDS), technology that populates the Interfolio Faculty Activity Reporting (FAR) module, in bulk. The Data Service comes connected to an expanding group of sources that include over 250 million activities, published and tracked in over 60,000 journals, government systems, and other authoritative outlets.

The Data Service then allows scholars to validate the academic data matched with them. Only after a scholar has validated their data will it be saved to their profile in Interfolio. See the below instructions on reviewing matched activities.

Interfolio — Use Interfolio Data Service

Templates & Vitas Expand

For a general overview of templates and vitas, read this article: Interfolio — Overview of Vitas and Templates in Vita Builder

Templates

Template - A template is used to set up a vita, including the sections and styling of the vita. These can be created at multiple levels including by unit or by individual faculty member.

Biosketches - similar to vitas, however, some sections are required to be included in a biosketch and cannot be changed, as this could make the biosketch out of compliance with the granting agency's guidelines. Within a section, faculty members can select which items to display in the selected biosketch. In addition, date ranges for biosketches are typically locked to comply with the granting agency's guidelines.

Personal Templates: 

Faculty can create their own templates to generate CVs based on their activities and profile. Follow the instructions here to build your own template: Interfolio — Create Personal Vita Templates

Institutional Templates:

At the institutional level, there will be templates available to generate forms for PTR/PR actions as well as a general merit template. These will be available in Spring 2025. Additional templates may be created as we continue to use the system.

Unit Specific Templates:

Administrative users within specific units (i.e. in a dean's office or department head's office) have the privileges to create unit level templates for use in processes like merit, annual evaluations, etc.)

For help with building personal or unit specific templates, contact interfolio@uconn.edu.

Vitas

Vita - The vita is the output of the template. This is where faculty will go to add custom text and generate/save/export their vita.

To create a vita in the vita builder, follow the steps here: Interfolio — Create and Export a Vita with New Vita Builder

Delegates and User Emulation Expand

For Faculty:

Faculty can now select other Faculty Activity Reporting (FAR, Faculty180) users to be a proxy (delegate) user on their account, allowing proxy users to access the CV information and reports for the faculty accounts they are assigned to. Interfolio — Configure Faculty Account Access

If you are looking for a user that doesn't appear in the system, contact interfolio@uconn.edu.

For Administrative Users:

Administrative users can emulate faculty and staff to access information or view FAR from their perspective. Interfolio — Use Emulate User Tool

ORCID Connection Expand

In order to use this integration, a faculty member must generate a user ORCID ID by establishing an account/record in the ORCID system. Once an ORCID account is established, the faculty member gives FAR permission, via OAuth, to access the ORCID account directly, then the ORCID import and export functionality becomes available. Instructions on how to link an ORCID account can be found here: Interfolio — Integrate ORCID

Review, Promotion & Tenure Module 

In AY 22-23, the Provost's Office implemented the following modules currently used for the Promotion, Tenure and Reappointment Process.

  • Review, Promotion, Tenure: The RPT module supports the collection of review materials and approval workflows for promotion, tenure, and reappointment cases. The module consists of workflow templates that govern every type of academic review case at UConn.
  • Dossier: Dossier is an online platform for faculty to collect and curate scholarly materials in a single, secure file management module. Faculty may curate academic materials and evidence in Dossier throughout the year and easily integrate into their PTR case.

    Dossier Module Resources Expand

    Dossier is an online platform for users to collect and curate scholarly materials, request and receive confidential letters of recommendation, and prepare for upcoming evaluations. A Dossier account never expires and documents stored in it remain indefinitely. Dossier gives users the opportunity to collect and organize their materials before they need to be used.
