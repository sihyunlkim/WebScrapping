AI SUMMIT 2026

Save the date for May 29, 2026!

The 2026 AI Summit will be hosted by the University of Wyoming Libraries, the Wyoming State Archives, and the Wyoming State Library on Friday, May 29, 2026, at the UW Conference Center. The AI Summit is an opportunity for information professionals to share and explore innovative uses of AI in our field.

Call for Proposals: Due February 13, 2026 

We are looking for presenters, discussion leaders, and lightning-round presenters to share how you have been using AI, particularly in your work. The AI Summit features 50-minute sessions in various formats: presentations, demonstrations, hands-on and interactive activities, or roundtable discussions. There is also a lightning round in the afternoon that's perfect for a 10-minute short presentation on a great tool, tip, discovery, or other information-sharing.

The University of Wyoming has earned its Research Level 1 (R1) status from the Carnegie Classification of Institutions of Higher Education, placing Wyoming's only four-year university among the top research universities in the United States.

The University of Wyoming Libraries has been a Federal Depository Library since 1907. The collection comprises approximately 1.5 million print and microfiche items in Coe Library and Library Annex, as well as maps, aerial and infrared photographs, and geological publications housed in the Brinkerhoff Geology Library. The Federal Document Library Program is changing from physical to digital distribution of government information; UW Libraries provides access to online publications available from the Federal Depository Library Program through the library catalog.
