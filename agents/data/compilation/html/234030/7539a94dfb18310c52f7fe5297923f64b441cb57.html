Generative AI at VCU

Generative Artificial Intelligence (Generative AI or GenAI) refers to systems that can create new content, such as text, images, music, or other digital content in response to prompts provided by its user. GenAI models are trained on large datasets and use this knowledge to generate outputs that mimic or draw inspiration from their training examples. 

Some common tools in use at VCU are Google Gemini, Microsoft CoPilot, and Adobe Firefly. These systems can generate content, provide insights, summarize, and help streamline our tasks. For a full list of VCU approved tools available for use review our AI Tools and Technologies page. 

For information about AI courses and programs, visit the VCU AI Guidebook. 

Guidelines and Policies for the adoption and use of GenAI tools at VCU

It is crucial to exercise caution when adopting and using GenAI. The policy and guidelines below address adoption and usage considerations at VCU. 

Protect confidential data

Do not enter confidential (Category 1) or sensitive (Category 2) data into any AI tools unless they have been approved for use by VCU IT Governance. Information given to generative AI tools is not private. Remember, humans may review content put into public models. Do not put anything in you wouldn’t want someone to review or read or to be available to anyone on the internet.

Confidential and sensitive data includes non-public research data, finance and credit card information, HR and employee data, student names, IDs, email addresses, records, and grades. It also includes medical info protected by HIPAA. Refer to VCU’s Data Classification Standard for more information. Or, use VCU’s Data Classification Tool to classify data.

Adhere to current AI guidelines and policies

Adhere to current university guidelines and policies when using AI tools.

  • VCU's AI policy "Acceptable Use of Generative AI Technology" establishes requirements and guardrails for safe adoption and usage of Generative AI tools. 
  • The VCU Faculty Affairs' Syllabus Statements dictate that instructors will provide guidance for students on the appropriate use of AI for their course assignments. 
  • Gen AI and Teaching Guidelines - Faculty
  • Gen AI Guidelines for Students
  • It is prohibited to upload or use content that is copyrighted or infringes on the rights of any third party. Review the "Plagiarism and Copyright" section in the "Generative Artificial Intelligence (Gen AI) and Teaching & Learning Tool” provided by VCU Faculty Affairs.

As the landscape of AI technology evolves, the university will continue to update its guidance and policies to reflect our growing understanding of the effects of generative AI tools in academic settings.

Protect student data and work

All coursework submitted by students is protected under FERPA. Do not upload student work to Generative AI tools without written consent from the student.  

FOIA Considerations

If your chats and conversations with a Generative AI tool involve VCU-related business and are retained in the history, they can be subject to the Freedom of Information Act. This includes chats on personal devices and via personal Generative AI tool accounts. 

You are responsible for any content that you produce or publish.

AI-generated content can be wrong or misleading. Fabrications are possible and infringement of copyright laws may occur. Be aware of the unreliability of AI tools and possible bias. AI tools are excellent research assistants, but they can "hallucinate" and suggest false facts and sources that sound plausible.

Check the generated content against reliable sources. Do not trust AI data to give medical, legal, financial advice and other professional advice. 

Avoid using unauthorized third-party bots with Zoom and Google Meet.

Meetings may contain sensitive information that requires the guarantee of confidentiality and security. VCU has vetted and approved the use of two transcription tools in our Zoom and Google Meet platforms. 

The Zoom AI Companion feature allows for AI generated transcription and meeting summary features for use in Zoom meetings. VCU has vetted and approved this AI feature for use in meetings, including those that discuss sensitive or confidential information.

For Google Meet, it is recommended to use the built in transcription tool to capture any meeting notes. 

We recommend avoiding use of any other third-party bots in virtual meetings at this time. Third-party bots can scrape your calendar for info and keep a written account or recorded minutes of meetings. They may save meetings in unknown places and join meetings when you’re not there. 

Understand Terms of Service and Privacy Policies

Learn the terms of service and privacy policies for the public Generative AI system you want to use. Make sure you are comfortable with how these tools handle and store your data. Be mindful of any rights the platform may claim over the content you create using their system. Check policies governing data use and sharing on these platforms.

Be alert for AI-enabled phishing

Generative AI has made it easier for bad actors to create scams on a larger scale. Keep following security best practices. Send suspicious messages to infosec@vcu.edu.

Generative AI tools need a VCU IT Governance Review

Any technology that stores, processes, or sends VCU data must have the required privacy, security and accessibility protections and be reviewed. Only use third-party tools that have been vetted and approved by IT Governance.

Please review our AI Tools and Technologies list for university-wide approved generative AI tools for use. Other tools not found on this page that you would like to use will need a contract with the vendor and to go through the VCU IT Governance review and approval in order for them to be used with university data. For ChatGPT specific information, please review the What Needs to be Reviewed page.
