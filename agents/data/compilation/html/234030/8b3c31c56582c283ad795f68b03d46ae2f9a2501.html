Generative AI Tools at VCU Libraries

VCU Libraries: Databases and Publications with Generative AI Features

Some library databases have added generative AI features onto the search tools. Instead of the generative AI creating its response based only on training data, it uses the content pulled from search results from a database to generate responses that are similar to the search results and connects the AI-generated response to the content in the database that has a similar response. Some of these tools also automatically adapt the search you type into a different set of search terms that the database actually uses. 
VCU Libraries currently has the following generative AI available on its tools: 

  • Britannica Online
    Encyclopaedia Britannica and related materials site has an "Ask Britannica" option that gives an AI-generated response when there's something in the Encyclopaedia about the topic and links to the relevant Encyclopaedia entries and other Britannica content.
  • Chronicle of Higher Education
    For users who create an account "Ask Chron" is available. It generates responses, with a list of related articles at the bottom of the page but does not link specific statements within the response to which related articles justify those statements.
  • Dow Jones Factiva
    A brief "Smart Summary" appears at the top of the results set from a search. The bottom of the summary includes a few related sources but, the summary does not link specific parts of the summary to specific sources. The summary changes if the date range or other aspects of the search are changed.
  • HeinOnline
    Within the "Law Journal Library" database inside HeinOnline, there is an option to toggle between the matching text from a search result and an AI-generated summary of the relationship of the journal article to the search.
  • LinkedIn Learning
    Provided by VCU Technology Services, LinkedIn Learning includes AI-generated role plays, content overviews, and "coaching"
  • Mintel

    The Mintel Leap AI assistant generates responses to natural language queries that connect to the relevant sections from Mintel's reports.

    Note: Users must authenticate with their VCU email address for access and confirm work is for educational purposes.

  • Morningstar Investment Center
    The red chat box in the lower right-hand corner opens a (non-private) AI chatbot. Chats can provide summarized information from Morningstar reports and analysis, with links to source information.
  • Oxford English Dictionary
    Oxford English Dictionary (OED) "AI Search Assistant" that appears in the top right corner uses pages with information ABOUT the dictionary and not FROM the dictionary itself. It typically suggests search terms or links to webpages with relevant information.
  • Oxford Academic
    Searches journals and books from Oxford University Press. After a search is conducted the option for an "AI Discovery Assistant" appears in the top right corner. The assistant provides summaries of the relevant content within each of the first few search results. Note: some of the results it presents are labelled "Get Access" and are not part of VCU Libraries' collections.
  • Pivot-RP (Formerly COS Pivot)
    Database that includes federal, foundation, private, and internal funding opportunities across all disciplines. The AI features can be toggled on or off and mostly involve expanding searches with related search terms and also may provide synopses of funding opportunities.
  • ScienceDirect
    "Reading Assistant" is offered with results from journals on ScienceDirect. It is limited to five uses per month and then prompts for payment. It allows users to enter questions about a document and receive responses with links that toggle to the relevant parts of the document, as well as to get AI-generated summaries.
  • Scifinder
    CAS database for chemical literature. SearchSense in SciFinder provides natural language searching and has options for AI summaries of results.
  • STAT!Ref Electronic Medical Library
    STAT!Ref cross-searchable resource of health care reference books, evidence alerts, and related materials. The STAT!Ref AI generates summaries based on materials included in VCU Libraries' subscription, with links to those sources. Maximum Simultaneous Users: 5
  • Statista
    Statista contains tables and graphs of statistical data. The "Research AI" option at the top of the generates text responses, with the information in Statista to justify the text linked from the footnotes at the bottom of the page.
  • UpToDate
    Licensed by the VCU Health System. Point-of-care collection of evidence-based recommendations for the diagnosis and management of medical conditions. Also includes drug information. Snippets of text appear at the top of the search results that were found, but not generated, using AI. AI-generated results are under "Expert AI" and require a registered account.
  • Washington Post
    Ask the Washington Post AI provides text responses with links to related content from the Washington Post but does not connect specific parts of the response specific news stories. The standard search box gives a summary at the top of the results, with links to specific sources.
    Note: Starting January 1, 2026, users must create an account with their VCU email address in order to access Washington Post content.
  • WRDS (Wharton Research Data Services)

    Access to financial, economic, and marketing data across multiple disciplines. Includes access to data from Compustat, CRSP, and others.

    Users must request an account using their VCU email address. Duo Mobile two-factor authentication is required.
