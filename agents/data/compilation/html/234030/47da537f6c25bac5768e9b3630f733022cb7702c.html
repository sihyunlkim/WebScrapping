AI Tools and Technologies

VCU Technology Services licenses a variety of generative AI tools that are vetted, secured and available to VCU faculty, staff and students to use for free. See our list of enterprise tools below for more information, including approved data designations. 

Microsoft Copilot

VCU has licensed Microsoft Copilot, a generative artificial intelligence chatbot for all faculty, staff, and students to use for free. Navigate to go.vcu.edu/copilot and login with your VCU email address and password. Copilot is built with the same security, privacy, and compliance standards as other Microsoft products. 

Our licensed access to Copilot is approved for Category 2 data and ensures that your prompts and Copilot’s responses are:

  • Not available to other customers
  • Not used to train or improve any third-party products or services (such as OpenAI models)
  • Not used to train or improve Microsoft AI models

Please use our Data Classification Tool to determine the sensitivity of your data and always minimize the data you provide GenAI tools. 

Important update: as of November 2024 the Copilot app integration and file upload features no longer work for all VCU accounts. Microsoft now requires OneDrive to be enabled for this feature and we do not currently have OneDrive available for all VCU accounts. 

Google Gemini

Google Gemini is a generative artificial intelligence chatbot for licensed all faculty, staff, and students to use for free. Navigate to gemini.google.com and login with your VCU email address and password. 

Our licensed access to Gemini is approved for Category 2 data and ensures that your prompts and Gemini’s responses are:

  • Not available to other customers
  • Not used to train or improve any third-party products or services (such as OpenAI models)
  • Not used to train or improve Google AI models

Please use our Data Classification Tool to determine the sensitivity of your data and always minimize the data you provide GenAI tools. 

Google Workspace with Gemini

It is now possible to enable Google Workspace with Gemini in your VCU-licensed Gemini account. This feature will provide Gemini access to your Google Workspace apps, like Drive and Gmail, allowing Gemini to assist you with analyzing, summarizing, and generating materials based on your Google Workspace data. 

While this feature provides a powerful collaborative tool, it also raises very serious security concerns. Hackers and other threats are constantly coming up with new ways to leverage AI in their attacks, utilizing strategies like prompt injection to gain access to your private data.

Before giving Gemini access to your Google Workspace, it's important that you consider and weigh the risks, as well as consider the kinds of sensitive data contained in your Workspace apps. 

To access Gemini and activate this feature, navigate to gemini.google.com and login with your VCU email address and password. Ask Gemini to access your Gmail or other Google Workspace Apps and a pop-up window will prompt you to approve Gemini's access to your Google Workspace. For more information on this feature visit Google's support page or contact emergingtech@vcu.edu with questions. 

Our licensed access ensures that your prompts and Gemini's responses are:

  • Not available to other customers
  • Not used to train or improve any third-party products or services (such as OpenAI models)
  • Not used to train or improve Google AI models
Adobe Firefly

Adobe Firefly is a generative AI tool that enhances creativity by generating high-quality images, text effects, and other visual content based on user prompts. New Adobe Firefly features such as generative fill are now integrated directly within Adobe apps such as Photoshop and Illustrator allowing creators to quickly and easily change backgrounds, generate and move objects, and expand images seamlessly. 

VCU students and instructional faculty have access to Adobe Firefly and other Creative Cloud products for free. VCU employees can request access to an Adobe Creative Cloud license to access Firefly and other creative tools. For more information on accessing Adobe at VCU: https://adobe.vcu.edu

Zoom AI Companion

The Zoom AI Companion feature allows for AI generated transcription and meeting summary features for use in Zoom meetings. VCU has vetted and approved this AI feature for use in meetings, including those that discuss sensitive or confidential information.

Meetings may contain sensitive information that requires the guarantee of confidentiality and security. VCU has vetted and approved the use of this transcription tool in our Zoom platform. We recommend avoiding use of any other third-party bots in virtual meetings at this time. Third-party bots can scrape your calendar for info and keep a written account or recorded minutes of meetings. They may save meetings in unknown places and join meetings when you’re not there. 

Google NotebookLM

NotebookLM is a Google Gemini-powered note-taking and research assistant offered as part of your VCU Google Workspace account. You can upload PDFs, Google Docs, web URLs, and YouTube videos into a "notebook." Once the sources are uploaded, NotebookLM becomes an expert on that content, allowing you to ask questions and receive answers with citations pointing back to the original text.

Key features include the ability to quickly summarize documents, generate different content formats such as briefing documents and study guides, and podcast-style audio overviews. NotebookLM can help users discover related study materials and visualize connections between ideas with a mind map feature. It aims to streamline research, enhance productivity, and improve information retention by providing AI-driven insights grounded in your own documents.

Our licensed access to NotebookLM is approved for Category 3 data and ensures that your prompts and NotebookLM's responses are:

  • Not available to other customers
  • Not used to train or improve any third-party products or services (such as OpenAI models)
  • Not used to train or improve Google AI models

Please use our Data Classification Tool to determine the sensitivity of your data and always minimize the data you provide GenAI tools. 

Medical campus users must request access by contacting emergingtech@vcu.edu.
