AI at Arizona

At the University of Arizona, artificial intelligence connects disciplines and people to deliver meaningful, ethical impact. Faculty, staff, and students advance real-world AI applications through research, teaching, and collaborative problem solving. The U of A offers campus-wide access to a growing suite of AI tools, platforms, and training. Explore the AI community at the U of A and discover campus-wide resources to support learning and innovation with responsible AI.

With innovation comes a responsibility to ensure ethical, transparent, and sustainable AI use. University leadership is reviewing the environmental and societal impacts of AI to align with long-term sustainability goals across campus and in the community. The Office of the Chief AI Officer and the AI Roadmap Sustainability Team are reviewing strategies for responsible AI use and the environmental footprint and incorporating AI sustainability into education, research, and operations.

Udall Center Fellowship

The Udall Center has partnered with the University of Arizona's BIO5 Institute, Arizona Institute for Resilience (AIR), College of Social and Behavioral Sciences (SBS), Office of Research and Partnerships (ORP), College of Agriculture, Life and Environmental Sciences (CALES), and Office of the Chief AI Officer (OCAIO) to offer specialized fellowship tracks for research with a public policy dimension.

Fellows receive dedicated time in Summer 2026, Fall 2026, or Spring 2027 to advance research projects that relate to or inform public policy in their field.

Deadline: February 16, 2026

AI Roadmap

The University of Arizona is developing an AI Roadmap to guide how artificial intelligence shapes education, research, and operations. This collaborative effort engages working teams of faculty, staff, and students to define goals, set priorities, and ensure our approach reflects the values of the University of Arizona community. Together, we are building a shared vision for the responsible and innovative use of AI across the university.

AI Insights

Participate in the AI Insights series, where enthusiasts share knowledge about artificial intelligence in a collaborative setting. AI Innovation Pilots

The AI Innovation pilots with the Arizona Institute for AI and Society (AI2S) is an internal opportunity designed to foster high-impact, interdisciplinary responsible AI projects by providing dedicated, hands-on support from our institute's leading experts in AI, Machine Learning, and Data Science. Our expert team is available to support intensive, short term pilot projects to offer a unique chance to rapidly prototype, design, and test novel AI concepts. We are seeking innovative teams from across the university looking to explore new frontiers in AI. This pilot is designed as a catalytic mechanism, leveraging our in-house expertise to build your project's capacity and to position your team to compete for major external funding opportunities. Share with us your project by submitting an AI Innovation Pilot Intake Form.

AI Research Communities of Excellence (AI RCoE)

AI RCoE is an initiative of the Arizona Institute for AI and Society (AI2S) that brings together faculty, researchers, and collaborators across disciplines to catalyze convergent research. It strategically connects AI and data experts with domain researchers to generate proposals, position researchers for external funding, and advance responsible AI applications while addressing societal challenges. Sign up to become an RCoE Member to receive regular notifications and invitations for upcoming RCoE activities. Members are expected to engage with at least one RCoE activity per semester. Member status is aimed at faculty, research staff, and post-doctoral hires. 

Key focus areas align with the strategic research initiatives of the Office of Research and Partnership: AI-Driven Health Care Innovation, Promise of Fusion Energy, Future of Sustainable Mining and Critical Minerals, and Space and National Security.
