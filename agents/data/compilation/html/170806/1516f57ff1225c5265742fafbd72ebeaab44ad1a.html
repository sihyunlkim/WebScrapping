Artificial Intelligence (AI)

This guide has been designed to introduce students, staff and others  to artificial intelligence (AI), Generative AI (Gen AI) and the ethical use of these tools.  This guide is a work in progress and will be updated as new resources are published. 

What is Artificial Intelligence (AI)?   Artificial intelligence is the ability for computers to imitate  human functions such as problem-solving, understanding language, and recognizing patterns. 

Types of AI :  AI is generally divided into 2 different types, Weak AI and Strong AI. 

Weak AI:  Weak AI or Artificial Narrow Intelligence (ANI) is the type of AI humans mostly use. Virtual assistants, like Siri, would fall into this group. These types of AI have a narrow focus. You can ask Siri what sound a dog makes and Siri will use a defined dataset (the Internet) to search for the answer and repeat it back to you. Chat GPT or any other chatbot (Google's Gemini) would fall into this category as well. 

Strong AI:  Strong AI or  Artificial General Intelligence has a goal of developing AI in which a machine's intellectual capability is equal to a human's, not relying on complex algorithms or standard datasets to answer a question, instead being able to think exactly like a human. It uses multiple domains of knowledge. Autonomous cars would be an example of strong AI. 

Examples of AI

Generative AI:  Generative AI is artificial intelligence that can create new content (videos, images, words ) based on a user's prompts.  Chat GPT is an example of generative AI. 

Virtual Assistants: Virtual assistants use AI to understand a human's voice command. Examples of virtual assistants are Apple's Siri and Amazon's Alexa.
