Resources Related to Generative AI for Faculty

Where can I access Generative AI?   Chat GPT -  requires sign up for a free account. You can also try Microsoft CoPilot

 

Since ChatGPT was released in 2022, there have been many articles, white papers, and opinions on AI in higher education. Below you will find articles/guides that we hope will give you some food for thought. 

  • Will ChatGPT Change How Professors Assess Learning? From the Chronicle of Higher Education
  • AI in the Classroom:   Butler University's guide on artificial intelligence in the classroom. 
  • Teaching and Generative AI: Pedagogical Possibilities and Productive Tensions
  • Leading Through Disruption: Higher Education Leaders Assess AI’s Impacts on Teaching and Learning: Learn about the changes taking place in higher education from a 2025 report by Elon University and AAC&U. 
  • AI for Education: Provide AI literacy training to 1 million educators and lead the responsible adoption of Generative AI in the education ecosystem, empowering teachers and ultimately improving student outcomes while preparing them for the future.
  • Artificial Intelligence and Generative AI for Media & Journalism: UNC University Libraries guide "provides an introduction to AI and Generative AI, focusing on their applications in academia. It emphasizes the importance of understanding these technologies and their potential impact on research, instruction, and study"

 

AI AND STUDENT ASSIGNMENTS

  • ChatGPT Assignments to Use in Your Classroom Today:  University of Central Florida's open access collection contains ideas for assignments that could be tailored depending on specific course content. A user-friendly collection that can get you started with AI in your teaching. 
  • Harvard AI Pedagogy Project’s Assignment Catalogue:  Harvard’s AI Pedagogy Project contains an "evolving collection of curated assignments that integrate AI tools, created by educators from around the world. "
  • AI Meets Education at Stanford : "AI Meets Education at Stanford (AIMES) is a VPUE effort to catalyze and support critical engagement with generative AI in Stanford teaching and learning contexts, coordinated by the Center for Teaching and Learning".

 

AI Tools for Research

  • Semantic Scholar
    A free, AI-powered research tool for scientific literature.
  • Generative AI Hub- MIDAS University of Michigan
    The Michigan Institute for Data Science (MIDAS) supports the ethical and responsible use of Generative Artificial Intelligence (Generative AI) to accelerate disciplinary and interdisciplinary research.
