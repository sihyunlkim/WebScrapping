AI Literacy

  • AI Literacy
    Understand how generative artificial intelligence works, considerations when using GenAI tools, and strategies for evaluating chatbot responses. This site from the University of Louisville University Libraries includes videos, handouts, teaching activities and sources for further reading.
  • Association of American Colleges and Universities Student Guide
    Elon University’s Imagining the Digital Future Center and the American Association of Colleges and Universities (AAC&U) have collaborated to create a two-publication series to guide students as they learn about and use AI. The first Student Guide to AI was published in August 2024 and the second guide was published in May 2025. Both publications are available for free download and are published under a Creative Commons license, allowing individuals and institutions to excerpt and adapt material from the guides with proper attribution.
  • Making the most of Artificial Intelligence
    A guide to using AI with children and young people.
  • Maryland Center for Media Literacy & Education
    Find several resources on AI literacy through the Maryland Center for Media Literacy & Education. Great guide for beginners and parents.
  • Pros and Cons of Using AI for Literature Searching
    Check out The University of Tennessee, Knoxville University Libraries guide on using AI tools for literature searching.
  • MODERN-DAY ORACLES or BULLSHIT MACHINES? How to thrive in a ChatGPT world
    Developed by University of Washington professors Carl T. Bergstrom and Jevin D. West, a 16 lesson course (each only 5 to 10 minutes) on what LLMs are, "how they work and how to thrive in a world where they are everywhere." "You will learn when these systems can save you a lot of time and effort. You will learn when they are likely to steer you wrong. And you will discover how to see through the hype to tell the difference."
