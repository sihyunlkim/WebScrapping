URSHAN STUDENT SUCCESS CENTER

The Urshan Student Success Center acts as a compass to help students navigate their academic careers. We facilitate the Urshan mission by supporting students at any level and ability in their course work, personal development, and career planning, thereby improving student retention and success.

URSHAN STUDENT SUCCESS CENTER

Academics

The USSC will offer services that will help you:


  • Succeed in difficult courses
  • Become a more confident and independent learner
  • Engage in more productive and effective study and learning behaviors
  • Improve your writing skills
  • Manage your time more effectively
  • Reduce your assessment anxiety
  • Explore and determine your major
  • Return to/maintain good academic standing
  • Plan and maintain a realistic path to successfully completing an Urshan degree

To schedule an appointment (academic accountability, academic advising, or Writing Center), click here.

For more information, contact us at urshansuccess@urshan.edu.

The Urshan Student Success Center acts as a compass to help students navigate their academic careers.

Academic advising is a dynamic relationship between advisor and student to support the students educational success. Advisors provide guidance on course selection, academic policies, and regulations.

Your faculty advisor will be your program director; however, if you wish to review your total program at any point during the semester, the USSC is happy to meet with you. Appointments for phone/video conference/face-to-face meetings can be made by contacting us at urshansuccess@urshan.edu.

ACADEMIC ADVISING

Academics

All on campus and distance learning UC students may take advantage of the Urshan Writing Center to sharpen skills in research, writing, and reasoning. Services are available to all students regardless of classification or degree program. Students work one-on-one with a writing tutor, who assists them with thesis, development, formatting, organization, and grammar. Students may schedule up to two, 30-minutes sessions per week.

WRITING HELPS

Academics

The SASO provides coaching and accountability to assist students with the planning and completion of your semester academic goals. You may request a one-time appointment with the SASO in order to organize your semester or you may request weekly appointments. Through our SASOs guidance you will gain a better understanding of your academic strengths and challenges and work towards establishing both immediate and long-term goals resulting in improved academic achievement. 

Students who are at provisional or probational status are required to meet with the SASO weekly.

For appointment availability, please contact us at urshansuccess@urshan.edu.

COUNSELING

Academics

Urshan seeks to provide pastoral counsel and care to each student through an onsite counselor. Any student needing professional counseling should contact the Dean of Student Services (deanofstudents@urshan.edu) for the referral email. Students who request a referral will have the cost of their initial consultation covered by UU. The student will then be responsible for the cost of any and all visits needed beyond the initial consultation. This service is available via Skype for our distance learners. More information is available from the Student Services Office.

DISABILITY RESOURCES

Academics

Urshan University is committed to the provision of reasonable accommodations for all students with learning and/or physical disabilities* as
legislated by the federal government.

Students with medical diagnoses that qualify them for accommodations must contact the USSC at extension 2104 or email urshansuccess@urshan.edu.

Once the director of the USSC has received medical documentation and a confidential consultation is completed with either a Student Academic Support Officer or the director, the student will then be responsible to provide the director with a list of his/her current professors and courses. Consulting with the SASO or director is required each semester.

*As defined in Section 504 of the Rehabilitation Act of 1973 and
with the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) of 1990.
