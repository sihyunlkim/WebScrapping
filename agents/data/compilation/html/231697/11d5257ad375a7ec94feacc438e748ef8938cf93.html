FASTFORWARD HYBRID COMPTIA A+

The CompTIA A+ Core Series requires candidates to pass two examinations: Core 1 (220-1101) and Core 2 (220- 1102). These exams focus on new content that underscores the technologies and skills required by IT professionals supporting a hybrid workforce:

• Software as a Service (SaaS) applications for remote work

• Remote diagnosis & repair of common software/hardware/ connectivity issues

• Evolving technologies (cloud apps, Internet of Things (IoT) device security, data management, scripting, etc.)

• Usage and maintenance of multiple operating systems

• Onsite repair options vs. delegation to remote specialists Additionally, you will learn and apply methods from two highly sought after areas in Cybersecurity and Cloud

Computing. This course will prepare you to take and pass the CompTIA A+ exam, boosting opportunities for your IT career. Prior knowledge of hardware and software is required prior to taking this course.

FASTFORWARD HYBRID COMPTIA SECURITY+

CompTIA Security+ is a vendor-neutral certification that validates the competency of security professionals working in the IT industry. The Security+ certification confirms a technician’s critical knowledge of communication security, infrastructure security, cryptography, operational security, and general security concepts. FASTFORWARD HYBRID CERTIFIED CLINICAL MEDICAL ASSISTANT (CCMA) PROGRAM

The Clinical Medical Assistant program is designed to prepare students to be professional Medical Assistants, as multi-skilled allied healthcare professionals that specialize in procedures commonly performed in the ambulatory healthcare setting. This class is 108 hours of classroom instruction and covers theory and in-class practice and an online portion. Students may work in medical offices, clinics, urgent care centers, and may work in general medicine or specialty practices. Students will be exposed to medical and administrative tasks. Students must score 70% or more on the classroom portion of the program to be eligible to sit for the NHA (National HealthCareer Association) exam.

FASTFORWARD HYBRID PHLEBOTOMY TECHNICIAN PROGRAM

The Phlebotomy Technician Program is designed to prepare students to be professional Phlebotomy Technicians dedicated to safely collecting venous and dermal blood specimens for laboratory testing. This class is 36 hours of classroom instruction and 40 hours of clinical training at a local medical center. Students must score 70% or more on the classroom portion of the program to be eligible to participate in clinical rotations and sit for the NHA (National Healthcareer Association) exam. Clinical rotations are scheduled by the CVCC clinical coordinator and students must be available to start clinicals immediately after classroom instruction is completed. During the two week clinical period, students are required to perform 30 successful, unaided venipuncture and 10 capillary or finger sticks. After successful classroom and clinical hours students will sit for the National HealthCareer Association exam (NHA) in Phlebotomy.

ADMISSION REQUIREMENTS:

• High school diploma or equivalent

• Criminal background check

• Drug Screen

FASTFORWARD HYBRID NURSE AIDE EDUCATION PROGRAM

Centra College and Central Virginia Community College collaboratively offer a 6-week Nurse Aide Education Program at the Main Campus site in Lynchburg, Virginia, and branch location at Bedford Memorial Hospital (BMH). Clinical experiences are provided at Centra in both acute care and long-term care facilities. Completion of the program qualifies a student to take the national Nurse Aide Assessment Program (NNAAP) Examination by Credentia. Successful completion of this examination leads to licensure as a Certified Nurse Aide (CNA). Licensure is required for employment as a certified nurse aide. The Nurse Aide Education Program is approved by the Virginia Board of Nursing. Nurse Aide Education Program participants will obtain licensure as a Certified Nurse Aide through completion of the NNAAP exam, will work within the scope of practices for a Nurse Aide in the state of Virginia within hospitals, long term care, facilities, or home settings, and will be able to accurately report changes in condition and responses to care plans to staff.

FASTFORWARD HYBRID EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN PROGRAM (EMT)

The course is designed to produce competent entrylevel EMT’s who can service the community with basic life support care via the EMS infrastructure. Students learn to assess injuries and illnesses and administer basic emergency medical care. Students learn best practices in transporting injured or sick persons to medical facilities. Basic Life Support (BLS) training is included in the course. This class will be a mix of classroom hours and online work. Employment opportunities are available for Ambulance, Fire and Rescue services, Hospitals, Government Departments, Industrial, and Humanitarian relief organizations. Students will demonstrate the skills necessary to achieve the State and National Registry EMT certification.

FASTFORWARD HYBRID ADVANCED EMERGENCY MEDICAL TECHNICIAN PROGRAM (AEMT)

The individual processes the basic knowledge and skills necessary to provide patient care and transportation. AEMT’s function as part of comprehensive EMS response, under medical oversight.
