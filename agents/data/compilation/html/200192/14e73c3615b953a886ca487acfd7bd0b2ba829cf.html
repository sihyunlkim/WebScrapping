Your Path Starts Here

Apply to Lake Region State College

Applying is an exciting step toward achieving your academic and career goals.

APPLY NOW
 
 

Complete Your Application

Create an Account and Complete the Application Form. You must submit your online application and a non-refundable $35 application fee. 


Determine Your Student Type:
  • First-Year Student: No college courses after high school.
  • Returning Student: Previously attended LRSC after high school (excluding early entry).
  • Early-Entry: High school junior/senior taking college courses.
  • Transfer Student: Completed college courses elsewhere after high school.
  • Collaborative Student: Enrolled at multiple NDUS institutions in one term. "Home institution" grants the degree; "provider institution" offers courses.
  • Continuing Student: LRSC graduate taking additional classes.
  • Non-Degree Student: Enrolled for personal enrichment, not a degree.

Submit ALL Official Transcripts

To be considered official, transcripts must be sent directly to LRSC in a sealed envelope from the issuing institution or through an electronic transcript exchange service.

  • High School Transcript or GED
    Not required if you have an AA or AS degree.
     
  • College/University transcripts
    Include transcripts for college credits earned during high school (excluding LRSC). Transcripts from institutions currently attended are required, with a final transcript due after grades are posted.
     
  • U.S. Transcripts
    Must be sent from the National Clearinghouse or Parchment.
     
  • International transcripts
    Secondary school or post-secondary school transcript evaluation completed by a NACES-approved organization.
    • Visit NACES for a list of approved organizations.
    • Request that the evaluation results be sent directly to Lake Region State College.

If these services are unavailable, request that an official transcript be sent to:

Provide Vaccination Records (On-Campus Students Only)

Students enrolled at Lake Region State College must provide documentation of immunity to the following:

  • Measles, Mumps, and Rubella (MMR): Two doses of the MMR vaccine at least 28 days apart for students born after January 1, 1957, or a blood test proving immunity. Students born before 1957 are exempt
     
  • Meningococcal (Meningitis): One dose of the Meningococcal vaccine given at age 16 or older is required for all students 21 and under. Note: The Meningococcal-B vaccine does not meet this requirement.
     
  • Tuberculosis (TB): Testing is required for international students from World Health Organization-identified high-risk countries and any student identified as high risk for TB exposure. Exemptions are listed in LRSC Policy 800.02.

Steps for the Journey of a Non-Traditional Student

Whether you are a transfer student, international, collaborative, or a currently serving or military veteran, Lake Region State College wants you to be aware of the steps involved in becoming an LRSC student. Below, you find additional information important to making decisions about your education. You can also contact the Office of Student Services for questions or more information.

Transfer Students

Transfer Credit Information

  • Remedial courses do not count for credit but may fulfill prerequisites.
  • Common numbered courses transfer as direct equivalents if offered by LRSC or as General Education credits; otherwise, they transfer as general education electives.
  • The NDUS General Education Requirement Transfer Agreement (GERTA) simplifies transfers.
  • Quarter system credits convert to semester hours.
  • Credit by exam, life experience, or from another institution requires documentation and is excluded from transfer evaluations.
  • Graduate-level courses are not transferable.
  • The final 15 semester hours must be completed at LRSC.
  • Transfer credits do not affect GPA at LRSC.

College Credit in High School (Early Entry)

High school juniors and seniors can earn college and high school credits by taking general education courses while still in high school.

Launch!

Launch! is a partnership between Lake Region State College and the University of North Dakota designed for students who are not yet eligible for admission to UND but meet LRSC's eligibility requirements. Students will enroll as full-time LRSC students while taking a limited number of prescribed UND courses. Classes will be held on the UND campus in Grand Forks, North Dakota. Students who complete 24 transferable credits and earn a 2.0 GPA may be considered for admission as degree-seeking transfer students to UND. Launch! Students may participate in student life, housing, student services, organizations, facilities, and activities, except Division I sports, available to every degree-seeking UND student. Courses are small, offering an opportunity for the instructor to assist students. Tutoring times are built into the schedule, and additional tutoring is available through the Student Success Center on the UND campus. Advisement is offered to each of the students and the Launch! Coordinator is located on the UND campus to offer assistance.

  1. Complete the Admissions Checklist above.
    • Launch! The student's admit type on the application is First-Year Student.
  2. Complete the UND Health Form.
  3. See Paying for College above to complete the following:
    • Complete your FAFSA
    • Complete your LRSC Scholarship application
    • See payment plan options if you do not qualify for financial aid

Collaborative

A collaborative student takes courses at multiple North Dakota University System (NDUS) institutions in a term. LRSC is your home institution, where the other NDUS school is your provider institution.

NDSU Collaborative Student - LRSC as Home Institution

Eligibility:
  • Fully admitted to LRSC with a 2.0+ GPA.
  • No enrollment holds.
  • Enroll in at least one LRSC course.
  • Do not exceed 20 semester hours without approval.
  • Meet provider institution prerequisites.
  • Graduate-level and correspondence courses are not eligible.
LRSC Responsibilities:
  • Combine provider institution credits with LRSC credits for financial aid.
  • Add provider institution tuition and bookstore charges to your LRSC bill.
  • Add bookstore charges to your LRSC account.
  • Request transcripts and transfer provider credits to LRSC.
Collaborative Registration Process:
  1. Complete the Collaborative Request in Campus Connection under the eForm Tile. (How-to guide: Collaborative eForm Process)
  2. LRSC reviews eligibility and submits the request to the provider institution.
  3. The provider institution registers you and emails your schedule. You are not registered until you receive this email.
  4. A hold is placed on your account after registration, preventing changes. Submit an updated request form for drops/adds.
  5. LRSC drop/add and refund deadlines apply. Submit requests before the first day of classes for timely processing.

Submit requests before the first day of classes to ensure timely processing.

NDUS Collaborative Student - LRSC as Provider Institution

Eligibility:
  • Must be degree-seeking with a 2.0+ GPA.
  • Enroll in at least one course at your home institution.
  • Submit a collaborative request form to your home institution.
  • Course approval is required from your home institution.
LRSC Responsibilities:
  • Enroll the student and place a "Collaborative Student Hold" (restricts schedule changes but has no negative impact) on their account.
  • Drops/Adds require an updated request form submitted to the home institution.
