Programs

Degree and Diploma Programs

Boatbuilding 12-month aos degree

The Boatbuilding AOS Degree program integrates traditional woodworking skills with modern techniques through hands-on training in various boat projects and subjects like lofting, drafting, planking, deck and house construction, and the use of composite materials.
Learn More

Marine Systems 9-month diploma

The Marine Systems Diploma program offers a hands-on curriculum based on competency and industry standards, catering to individuals of all skill levels and backgrounds in topics such as marine electrical and electronics, diesel engines, outboard engines, plumbing, corrosion and more.
Learn More

The School’s AOS Degree program and diploma program follow an intense instructional schedule. Classes are held 9am – 5pm on Mondays, 8am – 5pm on Tuesdays – Fridays.

Marine Systems Intensives Certificate Courses

The Northwest School of Wooden Boatbuilding offers non-credit continuing education intensives in marine systems topics for industry professionals. These 5-day courses are held throughout the year. Individuals who would most benefit from the dense material and fast-paced learning environment would have experience working professionally in a marine-based setting or as an experienced recreational boater. These courses are not suitable for beginners and are not intended to be taken as a substitute for or to have the same educational approach as the 9-month Marine Systems Diploma program.

Marine Systems Intensive Topics

  • Marine Electrical Systems
  • Marine Corrosion
  • Marine Diesel Engines

About Us

Founded in 1981, the school offers a full-time 12 month Associate of Occupational Studies (AOS) degree in Boatbuilding, a 9-month Diploma program in Marine Systems, and 5-day Intensive courses in Marine Systems topics.
