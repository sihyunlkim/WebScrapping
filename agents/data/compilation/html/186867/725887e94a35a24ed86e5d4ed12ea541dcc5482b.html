AI Research at the Library

The Library subscribes to a wide variety of journals, ebooks and databases that include information and research about AI.

 

O'Reilly Learning

A good first stop is O'Reilly's topic page on Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning. There you'll find courses, ebooks, audiobooks, videos and articles to help you learn more about and how to use AI, machine learning, GPT, deep learning, natural language processing and other subtopics.

  • O'Reilly This link opens in a new window
    The O'Reilly Learning database includes the Safari e-book collection as well as videos, case studies, and tutorials on topics like career development, data science, software development, and programming. Sources include the "For Dummies" guides, O'Reilly guides and case study videos, and others. -> To log in, select (or type) Stevens Institute of Technology. After you do this, you will get a prompt to enter your email address.

Conference Proceedings & Journal Articles

  • ACM Digital Library This link opens in a new window
    The ACM Digital Library provides access to computer science journals, newsletters, and conference proceedings and contains the full text of every article that has been published by the Association for Computing Machinery since its founding in the 1950s.
  • AIS eLibrary This link opens in a new window
    The AIS eLibrary is a repository of information systems research from the Association for Information Systems. The eLibrary contains scholarly journals, conference proceesings and content from other AIS projects (special interest groups, chapters, etc.).
  • arXiv This link opens in a new window
    arXiv (pronounced "archive") is an open access database of article preprints in physics, mathematics, computer science, quantitative biology, quantitative finance, statistics, electrical engineering and systems science, and economics. arXiv is maintained by Cornell University.
  • IEEE Xplore This link opens in a new window
    IEEE Xplore is a full-text scholarly and technical database from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers, covering electrical engineering, electronics, and computer science. Its content includes journals, conference proceedings, magazines, newsletters, courses, and IEEE standards, with some content dating back to 1872.

Handbooks & Guidance

  • AccessEngineering This link opens in a new window
    AccessEngineering from McGraw-Hill provides full-text textbooks and reference books for all of the subfields of engineering. It also provides access to a data visualization tool, Schaum’s Outlines study guides, instructional videos, calculators, interactive tables and charts, and curriculum maps.
  • Knovel Engineering & Scientific Online References This link opens in a new window
    Knovel, an Elsevier database, provides handbooks, manuals, data sets, and calculators for engineering and science.
