Program Policies Title: Exam & Testing Procedures for all Courses
Effective Date: August 2008
Revision Date: January 2025

All exams are on campus

Students must also comply with the following:

  1. No electronic devices are allowed in the testing environment with the exception of your computer, if applicable.
    1. Electronic devices include but are not limited to:
      1. Phones, smart watches, earbuds, tablets, secondary computers, etc.
    2. Sit in an upright position.
    3. Do not read questions aloud.
    4. No eyes straying from the computer screen. 
      1. To rest eyes:  close your eyes or look straight up at the ceiling.
    5. No coverage of webcam/camera during testing.
    6. No form of communication with any student for any reason during the exam.

For asynchronous courses only:

All students are required to perform a complete 360 degrees survey of the room prior to starting all tests via any online proctoring platform. This means, you must use your camera to visibly show your entire room, including the floor, walls, chair and desk (all of your workspace). Electronic devices ARE NOT allowed in the testing environment. These include but are not limited to: smart watches, earbuds, phones, tablets, secondary computers. All students must have a conducive testing environment and sit on a chair with only your computer and a blank erasable white board on your desk, if needed. Students are NOT permitted to have scratch paper and a pen or pencil. Once the test has begun, students are not allowed to leave the computer or move to a new environment until the test has been fully completed.

Students who take tests through a cloud-based proctor service off campus must also comply with the following:

  1. Sit in an upright position.
    1. Students are NOT permitted to take exams on a couch or bed.
  2. No external distractions such as other people in the room, TV turned on, etc.
  3. No notebooks, textbooks, or any supplemental material within or around your testing area.
  4. Do not read the questions out loud.
  5. No usage of electronic devices for any reason, such as smart watches, earbuds, phones, tablets, secondary computers, etc.
    1. Phones are not to be used as calculators. A calculator will be provided through the cloud-based proctor service, if needed.
  6. No eyes straying from the computer screen.
    1. To rest eyes: close your eyes or look straight up at the ceiling.
  7. No coverage of the Webcam/Camera during testing.
  8. No external communication, such as texting, phone calls, talking, etc. during the quiz or exam.

Deviation from any of the above requirements will result in a written incident report and an automatic â€œ0â€ on the test and/or an automatic failure of that course.  Remember, you are responsible for knowing and following the Collegeâ€™s testing procedures and the Academic Integrity Policy.

The College reserves the right to ask students to retake any exam that the College believes in good faith might have been compromised in any way, even if there is no suggestion that the student is at fault.
