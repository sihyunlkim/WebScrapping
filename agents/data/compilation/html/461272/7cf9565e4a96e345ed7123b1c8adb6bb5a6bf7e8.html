How to Choose the Right Professional for Dermaplaning

When it comes to effective skin rejuvenation, few treatments are as accessible and results-driven as dermaplaning. 

This manual exfoliation technique gently removes the top layer of dead skin cells and fine vellus hair, also known as peach fuzz, to reveal smoother, more radiant skin. 

But the treatment is only as good as the hands performing it. Choosing the right professional matters—not just for better results, but for your skin’s health and safety.

Let’s Know First What is Dermaplaning

Dermaplaning is a form of manual exfoliation that uses a sterile, surgical-grade blade. The latter gently scrapes away the top layer of dead skin cells along with fine facial hair. This is commonly known as peach fuzz. 

The dermaplaning process helps reveal fresher, smoother skin underneath. Plus, it can improve the appearance of dullness, uneven texture, and minor blemishes. Because it clears the surface of the skin, dermaplaning also enhances the absorption of skincare products. It even creates a flawless canvas for makeup application.

As a treatment under the umbrella of non-invasive facial procedures, Dermaplaning requires no downtime and is suitable for most skin types.

Why Choosing the Right Professional Matters

Although dermaplaning is considered one of the safer non-invasive facial procedures, it still involves the use of a sterile, surgical-grade blade gliding over your skin. This means improper technique, lack of sanitation, or inexperience can lead to:

  • Nicks or cuts
  • Skin irritation
  • Infection
  • Incomplete results

Working with someone experienced ensures the procedure is both effective and safe, especially if you have skin concerns like acne scars, dry patches, or sensitivity. The right provider also knows when Dermaplaning is or isn’t appropriate for certain skin types.

Here’s How to Select Your Dermaplaning Provider

Check for Licensing and Credentials

First and foremost, the provider should be a licensed esthetician or skincare specialist. In Utah, SB 112 now authorizes master-level estheticians to perform services such as eyelash and eyebrow technology and dermaplaning, expanding their scope of practice. This ensures that professionals offering professional skincare treatments are operating under updated and legally recognized guidelines.

Evaluate Experience and Skin Knowledge

Dermaplaning isn’t one-size-fits-all. A provider who understands how the skin behaves will tailor the session based on your needs. That means understanding:

  • Your skin type (oily, dry, combination, sensitive)
  • Existing conditions (eczema, acne, rosacea)
  • Medications or topical products that could affect your skin’s response

Professionals with real-world experience can adjust pressure, angle, and post-treatment recommendations accordingly. Their goal is not only peach fuzz removal but also improving tone and texture safely.

Review Before-and-After Photos

Any reputable provider should have examples of their work. Whether through a portfolio, online reviews, or client testimonials, you should see a visual record of:

  • Improved texture
  • Evened tone
  • Diminished appearance of fine lines
  • Enhanced glow

Look for results on patients with similar skin tones and concerns. This provides realistic expectations and insight into the provider’s consistency.

Ask About Sanitation Practices

A clean treatment area is non-negotiable. Your skin will be exfoliated and exposed. This means cleanliness directly impacts healing and outcome. Ask about:

  • Use of disposable blades or sterilization process
  • Handwashing and glove usage
  • Sanitization of treatment surfaces between patients

Proper infection control measures protect you from bacteria and breakouts.

What Should the Consultation Include?

The right professional will offer a thorough consultation before any blade touches your face. During this time, they should:

  • Discuss your skincare goals
  • Examine your skin under proper lighting
  • Review your skincare routine
  • Note any contraindications (recent chemical peels, sunburn, active acne, etc.)

They should also explain the process step-by-step and suggest how Dermaplaning could fit into a broader skincare plan or be combined with other professional skincare treatments.

Red Flags to Watch For

Trust your gut. A few things that should raise concern include:

  • Vague answers to your questions
  • No consultation or skin analysis
  • Use of non-sterile tools
  • Rushed or dismissive attitude

Even if the price seems right, your skin deserves someone who takes the time to get it right.

Compare Treatment Settings

Not all providers work in the same environment. Some are based in medical spas, others in salons or solo practices. Choose a setting that is professional, comfortable, and equipped with proper tools and lighting.

Some treatment rooms may feel more clinical, while others resemble a relaxing spa. Either is acceptable, but cleanliness and credentials are more important than ambiance. Make sure the provider follows proper hygiene protocols and uses sterile equipment for every procedure. Your safety and results depend on both skill and standards.

Understand What to Expect After Dermaplaning

Recovery is usually minimal, but your provider should walk you through what to expect:

  • Slight redness for a few hours
  • Extra sensitivity to the sun (wear SPF!)
  • Makeup will go on smoother
  • Skin may absorb products better

They should also advise when to return for maintenance and what skincare products to use or avoid.

Key Questions to Ask Before Booking

Here is a short list to help you feel more prepared:

  1. What training and certifications do you have in Dermaplaning?
  2. How do you assess if someone is a good candidate?
  3. What are your pre- and post-treatment instructions?
  4. What is your process for cleaning and sanitizing equipment?
  5. Can I see some client results from past treatments?

These questions help clarify the provider’s expertise and your comfort level.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is dermaplaning safe for all skin types?

Yes, dermaplaning is generally safe for most skin types, but those with active acne or certain skin conditions should consult a professional first.

How often should I get dermaplaning done?

Most professionals recommend scheduling dermaplaning every 4 to 6 weeks for optimal results.

Does dermaplaning make hair grow back thicker?

No, peach fuzz removal through dermaplaning does not change the color or thickness of the hair as it regrows.

Can I wear makeup after a dermaplaning treatment?

Yes, you can wear makeup after dermaplaning, and it often goes on smoother due to the exfoliated surface.
