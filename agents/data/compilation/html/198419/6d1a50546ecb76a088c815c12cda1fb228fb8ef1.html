AI at Duke Initiative Strengthens Leadership and Expands Faculty Engagement

in Learning, Research, Teaching, Work with AI

October 15, 2025

The AI at Duke steering committee continues to gain momentum as it enters a critical phase of strategic planning and university-wide engagement. Launched by Provost Alec Gallimore in May, the initiative aims to identify Duke’s strategic priorities around AI and develop critical infrastructure and communities of practice to drive Duke forward as a leader in the evolving field of artificial intelligence.

To guide this effort, the steering committee—supported by four pillar advisory committees—has welcomed three new leaders to its ranks:

  • Tracy Futhey, vice president and chief information officer and member of the steering committee, will take on a new role as co-chair. Futhey brings extensive expertise in cyber-infrastructure and –security and a strong track record in IT leadership and management.
  • Ricardo Henao, associate professor of biostatistics & bioinformatics and chair of the sustainability in AI advisory committee, has also been appointed co-chair. His addition strengthens ties with the School of Medicine and reinforces Duke’s commitment to sustainable AI practices.
  • Nicoleta Economou-Zavlanos, director of the Duke Health AI Evaluation & Governance Program, joins the steering committee and will serve as liaison for the trustworthy & responsible AI advisory committee.

Joseph Salem, Rita DiGiallonardo Holloway university librarian and vice provost for library affairs, will continue to serve in his role as co-chair. The new appointments restore the steering committee’s original three co-chair structure and bring deep institutional knowledge, interdisciplinary expertise, and renewed energy to the initiative.

Upcoming Faculty Engagement Opportunities

To ensure broad faculty input, the steering committee will launch a faculty-wide survey in the next two weeks. This survey will help identify perspectives and challenges related to AI across Duke’s schools and departments.

In addition, the initiative will host two faculty forums on November 6 from noon to 1:30pm and November 11 from 5 to 6:30pm to gather feedback and foster dialogue about Duke’s strategic direction around AI. These forums will provide space for faculty to share perspectives, raise concerns, and help shape the final recommendations of the committee.

All Duke faculty will receive an email on Monday, October 20 with details about the survey and forums.

Looking Ahead

The AI at Duke initiative is on track to deliver a comprehensive set of recommendations to the Provost by the end of the year. These will be informed by committee reports, faculty input, and a large-scale inventory of AI-related activity across campus—including data from peer institution benchmarking and Scholars@Duke. To support this latter effort, all faculty working with a focus on AI are encouraged to update Scholars@Duke with current information.

The Provost’s AI initiative brings together Duke experts from across all disciplines who are advancing AI research and applications, addressing the most pressing ethical challenges posed by AI, and shaping the future of AI in the classroom and in our lives. To learn more, visit ai.duke.edu.
