Show-Me AI training and resources

The University of Missouri is piloting a new AI walled garden tool as part of MU Provost Martens’ priority of embracing AI in higher education. Learn more about Show-Me AI.

Show-Me AI: Features

Show-Me AI has a lot of robust capabilities. These guides will get you started.

Show-Me AI: Creating an assistant

AI assistants can be used for student tutoring, assignment creation, writing assistance and more.

Show-Me AI: Creating prompt templates

Prompt templates allow you to create a reusable, structured set of instructions designed to guide you toward consistent, reliable results.

Show-Me AI: Creating custom instructions

Custom instructions are a dynamic add-on to prompt templates that enable you to more fully control the tone, style or content of the AI output.

Show-Me AI: Uploading files

Access to class handouts and other materials gives your AI assistant more context for its responses.

Show-Me AI: Model selection

The ability to choose an AI model is a powerful feature of Show-Me AI. This guide describes the strengths and weaknesses of each option.

Show-Me AI: Sharing

From conversations to prompt templates and even assistants. Sharing to others is a huge part of what makes Show-Me AI great. Here’s how.

Sharing in Show-Me AI

Learn how to share conversations, folders and assistants with other Show-Me AI users.

Show-Me AI: Accessing shared items

Find and access conversions, folders and assistants that have been shared with you in Show-Me AI.

Show-Me AI: Sharing assistants with your class

Follow this guide to learn how to share your AI assistants with students in your class.
