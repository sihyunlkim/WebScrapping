Reimagining PedAIgogy

The Spring 2026 Reimagining PedAIgogy series schedule is being unveiled!

Session recordings and resources from 2024-2025 are available below to revisit, explore at your own pace, or catch up on anything you missed.

Have questions or are you interested in sharing what you are learning with AI as part of this series?

Contact Lauren Kelley atlaurenmk@udel.edu

What you can expect from this series:

  • Thoughtfully explore ways to integrate AI tools into your teaching toolset
  • Reimagine ways students might learn alongside AI
  • Explore AI’s impact on academic research

Next Session

Part 1: Three-Way Partnerships: AI as Research Partner

Lauren Kelley, PhD
Maria Barefoot

February 25, 2026, 12-1:15 p.m.

Explore how AI tools can enhance research capabilities for both faculty and students. This hands-on session introduces specialized platformsincluding Perplexity, Elicit, Consensus, and university enterprise AI toolsthat streamline literature reviews, improve search strategies, and support evidence synthesis. Discover where AI assistance is most valuable in the research process and how to guide students in becoming discerning AI users.

Note: This session will involve the use of enterprise AI tools provided by our institution.

Prerequisite: Participants will need a smart device to explore AI tools during the session.

AI for Teaching & Learning Seminars

In addition to the Reimagining PedAIgogy Workshop Series, there is an AI Teaching & Learning Seminar Series hosted by the AI Teaching & Learning Working Group. This series is hosted collaboratively with the Library, Museums, and Press and strives to provide a space for UD faculty and staff to grapple with how AI is changing the nature of teaching and learning in higher education. This series provides more opportunities to dive more deeply into the central questions and concerns that arise as faculty have experimented with these tools in their teaching through panel discussions and research.

Learn more about this series at Artificial Intelligence for Teaching and Learning at the University of Delaware.
