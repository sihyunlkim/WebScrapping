ACCJC Spring 2026 Election - Nomination and Application Instructions

Date: February 6, 2010

To: Chancellors, Superintendents, Presidents, and Accreditation Liaison Officers of ACCJC Member Institutions; Other Interested Parties

From: Mac Powell, President MP

Subject: ACCJC Spring 2026 Election - Nomination and Application Instructions

On behalf of the Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC), the ACCJC Nominating Committee is seeking nominations and applications from individuals who wish to be considered for election as Commissioners to ACCJC.

The spring 2026 vacancies are as follows:

Category 1 Commissioners

  • Two Academic Members (two incumbents)
  • One Administrative Member
  • One Chief Instructional Officer Expertise Member
  • One Higher Education Finance Expertise Member (incumbent)
  • Two Public Members

Category 2 Commissioners

  • One California Community Colleges Chancellor's Office (CCCCO) Nominee (incumbent)
  • One Pacific Postsecondary Education Council (PPEC) Nominee (incumbent)
  • One WASC Senior College and University Commission (WSCUC) Nominee (incumbent)

Terms of Service: Commissioners are elected for three-year terms and are limited to two (2) three-year terms unless elected as an officer for a term that extends beyond a sixth year.

Commission Composition: A summary of the Commissioner categories, types, and special definitions is available on the ACCJC website. Please review this document carefully before making a nomination.

Nomination and Application Information:

The process for nominating and electing Commissioners is described in the ACCJC Bylaws (Article 4). Nominations may be made in a letter that names the nominee, includes their contact information, and describes the rationale for the nomination. The nomination letter may serve as one of the nominee's required letters of recommendation (see below). Nomination letters should be sent to Alexandra Spring at communications@accjc.org.

Whether recommended by others or self-nominated, all nominees will be asked to complete an online application form (see below). Within that form, nominees will submit the following:

  • a. A letter of application stating the basis for interest in service on the Commission
  • b. A resume
  • c. Two letters of recommendation

Before completing the online application, nominees will be asked to review Commissioner expectations described in the application, as well as the Policy on Conflict of Interest for Commissioners, Evaluation Team Members, Consultants, Administrative Staff, and Other Commission Representatives, the Policy on Professional and Ethical Responsibilities of Commission Members, and the ACCJC Bylaws for information about serving as a Commissioner. Nominees should note that if elected to the Commission, they may need to limit their roles with certain other organizations to avoid conflicts of interest.

All nomination letters, Commissioner Application Forms, and supporting documents must be submitted no later than 5:00 p.m. (PST) on Thursday, March 5, 2026, to receive consideration.


CLICK HERE TO ACCESS THE COMMISSIONER APPLICATION FORM


Election Next Steps:

The ACCJC Nominating Committee will meet in March to nominate a slate of candidates from among the applicants. After a slate is identified and announced to the field, member college CEOs will have the opportunity to nominate additional at-large candidates for the ballot. At-large candidates with at least 10 endorsements from CEOs and who meet all requirements of the ACCJC Bylaws will be added to the ballot in competition with the slate candidate(s) of the same position. Ballots will be emailed to each institutional CEO in April and election results will be announced at the Open Session of the June 2026 Commission meeting and posted to the ACCJC website. Elected Commissioners will begin their three-year term on July 1, 2026.

New Commissioner Orientation:

The New Commissioner Orientation is scheduled for Tuesday, June 2, 2026, from 2:00 - 5:00 p.m. with dinner to follow. As part of our "Commissioners Rising" orientation program, we also invite and encourage newly elected Commissioners to attend the Commission meeting as observers (June 3). All costs of attending would be covered by ACCJC. We ask that applicants hold the dates on their calendars in the event they are elected to serve on the Commission.

Please feel free to direct questions to me or Alexandra Spring, Communications Director, at communications@accjc.org.

Thank you in advance for your interest and participation in this important process!

December 16, 2025

Dear ACCJC Member Faculty, Staff, Board Members, and Students,

I am writing to share an update following today's meeting of the National Advisory Committee on Institutional Quality and Integrity (NACIQI), the bipartisan advisory body appointed by Congress and the Administration to make recommendations to the U.S. Department of Education regarding the recognition of accrediting agencies.

The meeting opened with the introduction of President Trump's six new members, and the election of one of those new members, Jay Greene, to serve as Chair of NACIQI. Dr. Greene is a Senior Research Fellow for the Center for Education Policy at the Heritage Foundation and will now lead the Committee's work.

Following Dr. Greene's election, NACIQI heard remarks from Under Secretary of Education Nicholas Kent regarding the Administration's perspective on accreditation, including its perceived strengths, shortcomings, and the broader expectations for how higher education and quality assurance must evolve.

The Department of Education shared the text of Under Secretary Kent's remarks as part of his presentation, and I am including that material with this message. I strongly encourage you to review the remarks to better understand the Administration's stated position on the future direction of accreditation and federal oversight.

ACCJC will continue to work closely with our fellow accrediting agencies, our policy advisors at Penn Hill Group, and key federal stakeholders to advocate on behalf of our member institutions and-most importantly-our students. We have a scheduled meeting with Under Secretary Kent at the Department of Education on January 20, and we will continue to keep our community informed as these conversations progress.

Thank you for your continued commitment to student learning, student outcomes, and continuous improvement. The Commission remains firmly aligned with the vital role of two-year institutions in providing high-quality, affordable, and workforce-aligned educational opportunities that support social mobility and economic opportunity for millions of students and their families.

We appreciate your engagement and partnership as this important national conversation continues.

Sincerely,

Mac Powell, MBA, PhD
President
Accrediting Commission for Community and Junior Colleges (ACCJC)

    Hello,

    In March of this year, we invited institutions to participate in ACCJC's Workforce Partnership Survey to gather institutional efforts in the attached preliminary draft paper celebrating and supporting workforce partnerships at ACCJC member institutions.

    This paper will be shared with higher education professionals, government officials, and the public as a tool to share how colleges like yours connect students to high-demand careers, build pathways into local industries, and support economic mobility. At this critical time, we believe it is important to share innovative practices and inform national policy conversations about the value of higher education.

    In addition to the paper, we will be providing readers with an accompanying list and brief on all workforce partnerships at our member institutions. This component is still in development as we've provided one last opportunity for those who have not participated to share a partnership brief. A final draft will be distributed to our members and used for ACCJC's national advocacy efforts in late July. Whether your institution is profiled within this paper, or included in the supplemental list, we thank you for sharing your story and bringing attention to your institution's efforts in expanding educational and employment opportunities for students.

    Sincerely,

    ACCJC

    August 8, 2017

    • Invitation for Member Comments on Proposed Change of Standards
    • From ACCJC President Dr. Richard Winn:
    • I am pleased to report that, on Monday, August 7, papers were filed in federal court confirming a settlement agreement in the legal actions filed by the California Federation of Teachers (CFT) and related litigants against ACCJC. This brings to a conclusion a lengthy period of tension between the two agencies. The settlement agreement acknowledges a number of changes in ACCJC operations, some of which were brought into focus by the lawsuit. The Commission has routinely made changes and will continue to make changes through its continuous self-improvement program that incorporates suggestions from ACCJC member colleges and educational leaders. The settlement agreement also notes that, in keeping with federal policy, ACCJC continues to preserve its autonomy in setting its policies and making accreditation decisions. The agreement also reflects a move toward resetting the relationships among the leadership of the agencies that anticipates collaboration toward shared goals in institutional quality improvement and increased student achievement.
