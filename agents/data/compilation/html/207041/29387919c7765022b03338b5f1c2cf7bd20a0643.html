The I-DREAM Project at East Central University

Innovation & Development through Resources, Education, Access, and Mentorship (I-DREAM) Center of Excellence in Educator Preparation

The Augustus F. Hawkins Centers of Excellence Program is a federally authorized initiative under the Higher Education Act of 1965 supporting excellence in teacher preparation. Through this funding, East Central University’s College of Education & Psychology facilitates the Innovation & Development through Resources, Education, Access, and Mentorship (I-DREAM) Center of Excellence in Educator Preparation.

The I-DREAM Project is a five-year, $3.1 million federally supported initiative designed to strengthen Oklahoma’s teacher workforce by increasing participation and success among students who may face barriers to teacher preparation access. The project emphasizes place-based strategies and Indigenous-grounded pedagogy through the Family Education Model, an approach proven effective across tribal-college contexts for promoting student persistence and community connection.

I-DREAM supports students whose experiences and perspectives reflect the communities served by Oklahoma’s schools, including those with First American legal status, bilingual students, first-generation college students or individuals from rural backgrounds, whose preparation and participation are vital to the state’s educator workforce.

I-DREAM provides financial, academic, and holistic supports, as well as high-impact professional experiences, to prepare students for successful, sustainable careers in teaching and educational leadership.

  • $3000 scholarship per semester
  • $1000 scholarship subsidy for students with dependents per semester
  • Stipends for students who choose to join a language immersion program
  • Additional tuition assistance toward I-DREAM required indigenous language coursework

    • Spanish
    • American Sign Language
  • Summer Curriculum Development Camp Internship including on campus room and board
  • Mentorship program
  • Travel & professional development opportunities
  • Laptop loan program
  • Learning materials stipend
  • Annual Networking Banquet
  • Assistance with teacher certification & exam fees
  • Student Teaching Fellowship - $2000 during student teaching semester
  • Employment Incentive - $1000 stipend upon employment with a hard-to-staff Oklahoma public school
  • Writing Center expansion to include graduate-level composition tutoring
  • Subsidies for high school students attending ECU concurrently

Faculty/Curriculum

  • Culturally sensitive advising training for faculty advisors across campus
  • Culturally sensitive curriculum updates in core education courses

Campus Life

  • Student Family Resource Contact & NASA sponsor – to ease the minds of indigenous families sending their kids off to college
