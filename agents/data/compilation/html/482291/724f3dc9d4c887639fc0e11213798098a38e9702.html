Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) about UEI College

What is UEI College known for? Expand

UEI College specializes in providing short-term vocational and technical career training for students who seek entry-level positions in the industries of healthcare, business, and multiple skilled trades such as HVAC, automotive, electrical, welding, and criminal justice. UEI is a career college with multiple campuses in California, Arizona, Georgia, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas and Washington.

What programs are taught at UEI? Expand

UEI College offers diploma programs in the fields of healthcare (Medical Assistant, Dental Assistant, Medical Billing and Insurance Coding, and Medical Office Specialist), business (Business Office Administration), and skilled trades (HVAC, Automotive Technician, Criminal Justice, Electrician Technician, and Welding). Most of these career diploma programs can be completed in as little as 10 months and include both hands-on and real-world training and experience.

Is UEI College accredited? Expand

Yes, the UEI College Chula Vista, Garden Grove, Huntington Park, Oceanside, Ontario, Stockton, Reseda and West Covina campuses in California, the Phoenix and Mesa campuses in Arizona, the Morrow campus in Georgia, and the Dallas trade school in Texas are all accredited by the Accrediting Council for Continuing Education and Training (ACCET). The UEI College campuses in Bakersfield, Gardena, Fresno, Riverside, and Sacramento in California, the campuses in Albuquerque, NM, Las Vegas, NV, Stone Mountain, GA, and Tacoma, WA are accredited by the Accrediting Commission of Career Schools and Colleges (ACCSC).

Accreditation is a recognition that the college and/or institution maintains a high level of educational standards, as determined by an independent accrediting agency.

How much does UEI College cost? Expand

All career training and diploma programs at UEI College can be completed in as few as 10 months, allowing us to keep tuition rates lower than many two- and four-year college options. Most students at UEI College qualify for financial aid, federal loans and grants, and/or scholarships, which can help make the cost of earning career-specific training through UEI programs more affordable. Our financial aid team can help you learn more about your financial aid options, which are available for those who qualify.

How do I apply to UEI College? Expand

The application process at UEI College is simple and fully supported by an admissions team trained to guide applicants through every step in the process. Interested students may call admissions, fill out an online form, or simply drop in to your local campus to meet an admissions representative and learn more about UEI’s career training programs. From touring the campus and choosing a program to applying for school and exploring financial aid options, our admission team is there to make the college admissions process as seamless, simple and stress-free as possible.

Can I visit a UEI College campus near me? Expand

Yes, please visit us any time (during business hours) at one of our UEI College campuses in California, Arizona, Georgia, Nevada, New Mexico, Texas and Washington. Stop in to meet one of our admissions representatives, learn about the programs offered, take a tour of the campus, ask any questions you may have, and maybe even see a class in session. You may even meet with someone in on our finance team to discuss possible financial assistance options as this may factor into your decision to attend. Our doors are open; stepping through them could be the first step in achieving career success.

Who can I talk to for more information about UEI College? Expand

UEI College’s faculty and staff prides itself on being accessible and available for all students, prospective students, and graduates. If you have any questions about UEI College, our programs, locations, admissions, financial assistance, please don’t hesitate to reach out! You may fill out our Request for More Information form, contact us through our main line at (877) 463-4472, or call or visit a campus near you. Our team will work closely with you to provide the guidance and support you need as you explore your educational and career training options.

Does UEI offer financial assistance? Expand

The UEI student finance team is available to help all students learn about the financial aid options available to them so tuition costs don’t get in the way of their educational opportunities and a brighter future. Through one-on-one finance support, most UEI students qualify for some type of financial assistance, including federal grants, federal student loans, state-specific grants and loans, alternative loans, Federal Work-Study (FWS) programs, and VA benefits. Our faculty also ensures students are aware of scholarship opportunities available through UEI and outside organizations. Financial aid is available for those who qualify. Contact your local campus to get more information on applying for financial aid and eligible programs.

Does UEI College offer financial aid options for military veterans? Expand

Yes, additional financial aid assistance is available for military veterans, active duty members, reservists, and their dependents, helping them retrain for in-demand careers in the civilian workforce.
