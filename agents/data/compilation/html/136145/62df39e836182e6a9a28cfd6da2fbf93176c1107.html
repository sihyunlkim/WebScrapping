OVERVIEW

The Learning Resources Department consists of the Marshall W. Hamilton Library and Academic Success Center (ASC) & Tutor Lab, all in Building 4 (the Library). The Office of Accessibility Services (OAS) in Building 7 is also an extension of Learning Resources.

Learning Resources is committed to serving NFC students and faculty in-person, remotely, and online alike. Resources offered at the Madison campus are available at the NFC Live Oak and Perry Locations as well. Community patrons are welcome, but cannot access all of the services or resources designated for NFC students. Read more in our Library Guidelines.

MISSION STATEMENT

The Marshall W. Hamilton Library actively supports the curriculum and mission of North Florida College by providing access to quality learning resources and instructional services.  The Library promotes the use of electronic resources and seeks to provide its users with the competencies necessary to pursue their goals and to become productive members of the community.  The Marshall W. Hamilton Library serves both on campus and remote access users.  The Library enriches not only the college community but also NFCâ€™s entire six-county district.

HISTORY

The Library was named for Dr. Marshall W. Hamilton, the first president of the College, then North Florida Junior College (NFJC). In 1959, the Library became one of the first permanent buildings on campus (breaking ground on Jan. 8, opening to students on Nov. 23, and having a formal opening on Dec. 6) and in 1998 was expanded. The Academic Success Center (ASC) & Tutor Lab were relocated to the Library in 2015, and the Office of Accessibility Services (OAS, est. 2025), previously the Disability Resource Center (DRC), joined Learning Resources in 2022.
