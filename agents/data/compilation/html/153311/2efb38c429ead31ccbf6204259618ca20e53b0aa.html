Gaming and simulation are changing the way we play, learn, and solve problems. Whether it’s designing immersive game worlds, developing cutting-edge training simulations, or exploring extended reality (XR), this program gives you the skills to make it happen. Project-focused learning lets you develop a portfolio that showcases your talents and sets you apart in this fast-growing field. 

What You Learn in Game and Simulation Development

Work with industry-standard tools and techniques, from game engine development to 3D modeling and animation. Study the principals behind engaging, interactive applications. Use software to build characters, environments, and assets. Design and texture immersive worlds. Develop applications for augmented and mixed reality platforms. How deeply you dive into these subjects depends on your goals: do you want to earn a certificate or a diploma? 

What Can I Do With This Diploma?  

Be an application developer, 3D artist, animator. Create games, movies, and visual effects. Manage projects. Work in architecture and product visualization. Lead multimedia design projects. Just like any form of technology, this field will continue to evolve, and so will your opportunities.

REACH YOUR CAREER GOALS

Phi Theta Kappa

Phi Theta Kappa (PTK) is the International Honor Society for two-year colleges. Complete 12 credit hours with 3.5 GPA, and your local chapter will send you an invite to join. Each of our colleges has a chapter which means you’ll be supported by your peers. PTK offers transfer scholarships, intellectual enrichment, and personal development.
