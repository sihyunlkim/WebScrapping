Welcome to the EICC Fall 2025 Start Up Week! As we gear up for an exciting new academic year, we invite all team members to join us on campus for a week filled with engaging professional development.  These events are designed to help you reconnect with colleagues, refresh strategies and prepare for the upcoming semester.  Check out the detailed schedule below to plan your participation and create an enriching experience.  Let us kick off the Fall 2025 semester with vibrant enthusiasm and a renewed sense of purpose.

Exploring Innovation Through Sabbatical: A Presentation on Transformative Academic Projects

Join us for an engaging session that highlights the impactful work made possible through the EICC Sabbatical Program. This presentation will showcase a recent sabbatical project that exemplifies innovation, professional growth, and institutional advancement. Attendees will gain insight into the application process, the scope of sabbatical-supported work, and the lasting value such projects bring to both faculty and the broader academic community. Whether you are considering a sabbatical or simply curious about the creative endeavors of your colleagues, this session promises to inspire and inform.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/99100132411 
Presenters: Jeremy Pickard and Catherine Peterson

Unlocking Success: Empowering Students with Career Services

Career Services is not just a department—it is a catalyst for student success. Come explore practical ways faculty and staff can integrate career resources into everyday learning environments and interactions. Learn about the resources available so you can easily share them with students.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/94915239825
Presenter: Shawna Shields

Honorlock Facilitation & Online Proctoring

Orientation to the basics of Honorlock, as well as examples of how to effectively utilize it as an online instructor. After the initial presentation, we will discuss measures to ensure academic integrity in our online courses.  

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/8969858742  
Presenter: Marie Ripslinger-Atwater 

Open Education Resources 

Join us for a session on Open Educational Resources (OER). We will begin with a brief introduction to OER, highlighting how these freely accessible, openly licensed materials can enhance teaching and learning. Learn about the EICC OER Workgroup and their efforts to support educators in integrating OER into their classrooms. We will also highlight new resources from the Open Education Network and the Open Textbook Library, offering valuable tools for educators seeking high-quality, customizable content. The session will conclude with updates from the State of Iowa OER Action Team, sharing the latest developments in promoting OER across the state. 

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/5581645651
Presenters: Deb Lowman and Gabe Knight

AI in Photoshop: Creative Power and Classroom Potential

Discover how Adobe Photoshop’s AI tools are reshaping digital creativity and how they can be brought into the classroom. This session will include examples of Photoshop’s generative AI features like Generative Fill and Generative Expand, plus an overview of two new course modules for ART 186-Digital Photography. One on the Legal and Ethical use of AI in Photography, and an Introduction to Photoshop AI Tools.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/97627174375
Presenter: Patrick Cheak

Maximizing Impact: Teaching in the 8-Week Format

In this session, a faculty panel will share their experiences designing and delivering accelerated courses. Learn how they have adapted instructional strategies, maintained academic rigor, and supported student success-- while meeting course learning outcomes. Gain practical insights and proven strategies for thriving in the 8-week model. 8-week courses- they are not just for summer anymore!

Zoom link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/93476553919
Presenters: Julie Howar and Alli Vandermyde

All About our EICC Libraries

Learn about everything and anything related to our EICC Libraries including databases, our collections, subscriptions available, book clubs and other events that our Librarians host.

Zoom Link:  https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/5581645651
Presenter: EICC Librarians

Retention in Focus: Key Indicators and Classroom Impact

This session explores key retention and student success indicators—including term-to-term retention, DFW rates, course completion, and insights from Dropout Detective. Together, we will examine how these metrics intersect to tell a broader story about student persistence and success at EICC.

Through data snapshots and guided reflection, we will uncover patterns, raise questions, and create space for dialogue around what is working—and what could work better. Faculty will have the opportunity to share strategies, surface challenges, and consider how early alerts, instructional approaches, and support systems can improve student outcomes.

Whether you're looking to deepen your understanding of the data or contribute to campus-wide retention efforts, this session offers insight, inspiration, and practical takeaways.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/93724491985
Presenter: Erin Snyder and Jeremy Pickard

eLearning

Join the eLearning team for updates regarding all things eLearning! Hear about some upcoming accessibility guidelines, updates on processes and procedures, refresh yourself on some tips and tricks, and bring questions!

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/6513137023
Presenter: Tianna Perkins, Brandon Lange, and Michelle Rasmussen

A "What’s in my Canvas shell?" Episode

The new EICC OSCQR rubric highlights two big areas for improvement for Canvas shells: Accessibility and Engagement. Learn about how to check if your Canvas shell is accessible and how to fix any issues with picture and video descriptions. Also learn about ways to engage online students through discussions.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/8969858742 
Presenter: Ashley Hartwig

Exploring FY Enrollment Trends: Understanding the Story Behind the Numbers

Join us for an interactive session diving into the latest fiscal year enrollment trends at EICC. This session offers an opportunity to engage with institutional data in new ways—disaggregating information by campus, modality, and student population to uncover meaningful patterns and shifts. Together, we’ll explore changes in delivery formats, enrollment behavior, and student demographics, discussing what these trends might mean for instruction, program planning, and student support.

We'll also set aside time for group reflection and conversation, providing space for faculty to share insights, ask questions, and consider how the data connects to their own work and experiences. Whether your data-curious or a seasoned trend-watcher, this session will help bring the numbers to life—and guide us in shaping the future together.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/93724491985
Presenter: Erin Snyder and Jeremy Pickard

Multidisciplinary – A Healthcare Simulation Overview

Learn more about our multidisciplinary healthcare simulation, a project created to improve teamwork and communication among various healthcare students. Find out how it was created, how it worked and what challenges we faced while looking forward to correcting the next one.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/9901023433
Presenter: Carrie Jackson

Brainfuse Tutoring at EICC

Join this session to refresh yourself on the Brainfuse tutoring option for EICC students. We will review the tool and the pathway for students to access tutoring on-demand. We will also talk about referrals for tutoring, why they are important, and share strategies for increasing usage and awareness for students of this incredible tool.

Zoom Link:  https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/6513137023
Presenter: Michelle Rasmussen

Information Literacy Sessions

Discover what information literacy is and how your campus library can help your students apply research basics, such as: keyword searching, Boolean operators, nested search terms, as well as critical evaluation of sources and writing citations. Campus library employees Courtney Pritchard and Erin Steckel will discuss their approaches to information literacy; how they have adapted information literacy in the past to various departments, student assignments, or modalities; how to book information literacy sessions at each campus; and the electronic resources available for research to EICC students, faculty, and staff. 

Zoom Link:   https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/5581645651
Presenters: EICC Librarians

Brainfuse for Nursing

The training teaches nursing students how to navigate the platform to get academic support, improve their writing, prepare for crucial exams, and collaborate with others in a flexible, on-demand environment.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/9901023433
Presenter: Carrie Jackson

Inbox Makeover: Organize, Prioritize and Simplify

Drowning in emails? Learn practical strategies to organize your inbox, reduce clutter, and regain control of your day. This session covers filters, folders, batching, and simple tools to streamline your email workflow and lower stress. Take back your inbox with techniques you can implement right away.

Zoom Link:  https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/96064458634
Presenter: Alli Vandermyde

Disability Services - Current Realities

From students disclosing a disability to faculty receiving a Letter of Accommodations from a student. What you need to know about Disability Services and how you can support the needs of our diverse learners no matter what role you play at EICC.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/98333273299
Presenter: Kerry Dalbey

Outcomes Matter- Understanding Student Success Beyond the Classroom

What happens after students leave our classrooms? This session takes a closer look at key outcome measures, including graduation rates, the state’s Outcomes Dashboard (placement rates and wage levels), and Regent University transfer data. Together, we will explore what we know about our students’ success—whether they are entering the workforce, continuing their education, or both.

We will review insights by program, campus, and student population, offering space to reflect on how academic experiences influence long-term outcomes. Faculty will be invited to consider how teaching, advising, and curriculum decisions play a role in student success beyond completion.

This session offers both a big-picture view and actionable ideas—connecting institutional goals with classroom-level impact. Join us to engage with the data, ask questions, and help shape the future of student success at EICC.

Zoom Link: https://eicc-edu.zoom.us/j/99641900021
Presenter: Erin Snyder and Jeremy Pickard
