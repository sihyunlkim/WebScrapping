Community Organizations Step Up in Winter Storm Recovery

Weeks after January’s winter storm slammed into northern Louisiana, the Mississippi Delta and northern Mississippi, hundreds of people are still without power, maneuvering through fallen trees and downed power lines. In Oxford, the community has come together to take care of those needs — not just while waiting for other responders, but also fill persistent gaps.

Artemis Mission Shows How US Space Strategy Has Changed

The strategy going into NASA's Artemis II mission looks very different from that of the Apollo program in the 1970s. The United States is no longer competing against a single rival in a largely symbolic race, explains Michelle Hanlon, director of the UM Center for Air and Space Law.

Residents Grieve for Storm-Ravaged Trees

In communities across the South, the most visible, and lasting, damage from the ice storm is to the trees. It will take months for debris to be cleared and longer to replace thousands of trees that were lost. At the University of Mississippi, the chancellor has pledged to quickly replace trees that had to be removed.

Colleges See Major Racial Shifts in Student Enrollment

The Supreme Court ruling in 2023 banning race-conscious college admissions led to declines in Black and Latino admissions at highly selective universities. At many other schools, the opposite occurred, according to a new analysis. The University of Mississippi, for example, saw a 50 percent increase in Black freshman enrollment.
