          Generative AI Policy for Authors | Publication Ethics

          AI Policy | May 2025

          The University of Texas Press has established the following policy regarding generative artificial intelligence (AI) for all of our publications.

          As outlined in our author contracts, authors guarantee that their work is original, that they are the sole owners of their work, and that their work in no way violates existing copyright. Therefore, the Press will not accept any work that is substantially written or generated by an AI or Large Language Model (LLM) tool. We define “substantially” as consisting of 5 percent or more of the total word count in any given project. Any AI-generated images will require Press approval. Authors are legally liable for the use of AI or LLMs in the work they submit. Authors are required to disclose to their editor their use of AI or LLMs in their work when they agree to submit their manuscript for peer review. Authors must also include a statement at the beginning of their work describing the nature of any such use, detailing whether AI tools were used to generate the text of the submitted work, or to create any images, graphs, tables, or any other parts of the work. Each use must be cited as would be done with any third-party source.

          Individual journals published by the University of Texas Press may have their own specific guidelines for the use of generative AI and LLMs.

          Publication Ethics

          Our journals program considers our responsibility to work with authors and editors to adhere to the ethical guidelines put forth by the Committee on Publication Ethics (COPE), viewable on the COPE website: http://publicationethics.org/. Each journal may have differing policies. For additional information please see the journal’s page on this website.
