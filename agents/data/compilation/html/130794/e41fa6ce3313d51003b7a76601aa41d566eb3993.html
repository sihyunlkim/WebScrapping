Yale’s AI Tools and Resources

Get started with a range of AI tools. Whether you are a student, faculty member, or staff member, you are invited to explore and experiment with these offerings. The following tables provide information on available tools, how to access them, and the data types they can support.

Yale Provided Tools

These tools are provided to all Yale community members and are an excellent entry point for exploring generative AI.

Tool Description Availability Data Classification Level
Clarity Platform The Clarity Platform provides access to AI chatbots similar to OpenAI’s ChatGPT or Microsoft Copilot Chat but housed within Yale’s secure infrastructure, and provides access to large language models from Anthropic, Google and OpenAI. The featured Clarity AI chatbots are generative AI tools that can help you write emails and reports, write and check code,  describe and analyze uploaded images, and summarize uploaded documents. 

Available to: staff, faculty, and students

Pricing: Free

How to Access: Clarity Platform Chat

 High, with some exceptions such as ePHI. Refer to Clarity Platform Security for more information
Copilot Chat Copilot Chat, formerly known as Copilot with data protection, is a secure AI chatbot using GPT-5 and DALL·E 3. It does not use your conversations to train any AI model or share any data with OpenAI, ensuring your information remains private. Functionality is split into a Work and a Web tab. High risk data should only be used in the Work search. You can use Copilot Chat for writing emails, reports, and other text, generate images based on natural language descriptions, or upload documents and images for extracting information and answering questions.

Available to: staff, faculty, and students (18 years of age or above)

Pricing: Free

How to Access: Copilot Chat (log in with your Yale email address) Students should log in at  Copilot Chat for Students 

 Moderate in Web tab, High in Work tab
Zoom AI Companion Zoom AI Companion is an AI feature within the Zoom app that enhances meetings by transcribing meetings, assisting with organizing meetings and sharing files, generating summarized meeting notes, answering questions about a meeting, and providing action items.

Available to: staff and faculty

Pricing: Free (with Zoom account)

How to Access: Zoom AI Companion is enabled by default for everyone with a Zoom account 

 High
Google Gemini and NotebookLM Google Gemini for Education is an AI assistant designed to enhance productivity and creativity. It can be used for tasks such as drafting emails, summarizing documents, and generating creative content. By leveraging its capabilities, users can save time and effort while ensuring clear and professional communication. NotebookLM is also part of the Google for Education offering. NotebookLM is a research and note-taking tool that helps analyze, summarize, and interact with uploaded documents using the Gemini language models.

Available to: staff, faculty and  students (18 years of age or above)

Pricing: Free

How to Access: Google Gemini and NotebookLM (login with your Yale Google Workspace (EliApps) account)

 Moderate
Power BI Copilot Power BI Copilot is an AI-enhanced feature integrated into the Power BI Service. It utilizes generative AI technology to assist users in extracting and sharing insights from data more effectively. It also has developer capabilities listed in the developer tools section below.

Available to: staff and faculty

Pricing: Included in Fabric Power BI premium license

How to Access: Access to Power BI Copilot

 High
Microsoft 365 Copilot M365 Copilot is an AI assistant integrated into Microsoft 365 apps like Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Outlook, and Teams, powered by GPT-4 from OpenAI. You can use it to generate draft content in Word, Outlook and Teams, use AI to analyze data in Excel, and create presentations in PowerPoint, as well as other uses. 

Available to: staff and faculty (18 years of age or above)

Pricing: $30 per user per month

How to Access: Request access to M365 Copilot

 High
Microsoft 365 Copilot Studio Microsoft 365 Copilot Studio is an AI powered platform that enables users to create and share custom agents. Access to Copilot Studio is included with a M365 Copilot license.

Available to: staff and faculty (18 years of age or above)

Pricing: $30 per user per month

How to Access: Request access to M365 Copilot

 High, with some exceptions such as ePHI.
Teams Premium Microsoft Teams Premium includes AI-powered features designed to enhance your experience within Microsoft Teams, especially regarding meetings. It integrates directly within Teams, providing assistance with various tasks before, during, and after meetings. 

Available to: staff and faculty

Pricing: $1.88 per user per month

How to Access: Request access to Teams Premium

 High

Developer Tools

This list of tools highlights more advanced options that are available to support building new Yale tools and experiences. For additional details about research support, please visit Yale Center for Research Computing.

Tool Description Availability Data Classification Level
GitHub Copilot GitHub Copilot is an AI-powered coding assistant designed to help developers write and understand code more efficiently. It integrates seamlessly with popular coding environments, such as Visual Studio Code and JetBrains, providing support right where you code.

Availability: staff and faculty 

Pricing: $19 per month

How to Access: Request access to GitHub Copilot

 Moderate
Power BI Copilot Power BI Copilot is an AI-enhanced feature integrated into both Power BI Desktop and Power BI Service. It utilizes generative AI technology to assist developers in understanding their data models and DAX calculations, along with DAX and report creation.

Available to: staff and faculty

Pricing: Included in Fabric Power BI premium license

How to Access: Access to Power BI Copilot

 High
Azure AI Azure AI provides access to advanced AI models from Microsoft, OpenAI, Mistral, Meta, Stability AI, Core42, Nixtla and more, all hosted in Microsoft’s Azure cloud. Developers can use Azure services to build applications that utilize these models.

Availability: staff and faculty 

Pricing: Based on usage

How to Access: AI request review process

 SPA required per app
AWS Q Business Amazon Q Business allows developers to easily build AI-powered assistants with access to enterprise data. AWS Q automatically selects the best language model for each request. AWS developers who want to have more control over their applications, including choice of AI models, will want to use AWS Bedrock instead.

Availability: staff and faculty 

Pricing: Based on usage

How to Access: AI request review process

 SPA required per app
AWS Bedrock Amazon Bedrock is a fully managed service providing access to various foundational AI models from providers like AI21 Labs, Anthropic, Cohere, Meta, Mistral AI, Stability AI, and Amazon. Bedrock is suitable for developing AI applications, offering flexibility in model selection and robust security features, similar to Azure AI.

Availability: staff and faculty 

Pricing: Based on usage

How to Access: AI request review process

 SPA required per app

Popular No Cost Tools

A set of popular tools that have the power to further enhance productivity and creativity. This list is for informational purposes only as these tools are not endorsed by Yale. They are for personal use only and are not provided by the university. Use when handling low-risk, unsecured data for experimentation and collaboration.

Tool Description Availability Data Classification Level
OpenAI ChatGPT ChatGPT is an AI chatbot developed by OpenAI, which is credited with starting the AI boom. The free version gives users access to the GPT-4o large language model (with limitations). ChatGPT tends to push the envelope on advanced features such as real-time voice mode on the mobile app. By default ChatGPT will capture user data to include in future training of AI models. Users should change their settings to opt out of model training.

Pricing: Free and paid plans

How to Access: ChatGPT.com

 Low
Google Gemini Gemini is an AI chatbot developed by Google, in the same class as ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot. The free version of Gemini can create images based on a text prompt, unlike the free version of ChatGPT. It is well-integrated with Google Search and other Google products.

Pricing: Free and paid plans

How to Access: Gemini

 Low
Anthropic Claude Claude is an AI chatbot developed by Anthropic. It is similar to ChatGPT, but is a little less versatile. For example it cannot generate images and does not have web search capabilities. The Claude large language models excel at summarizing and reasoning on large amounts of text and images. The Claude 3.5 Sonnet model is particularly good at coding tasks.

Pricing: Free and paid plans

How to Access: Claude.ai

 Low
Meta.ai Meta.ai is an AI chatbot from Meta that utilizes the powerful open source Llama models.

Pricing: Free (some features require Facebook or Instagram account)

How to Access: Meta.ai

 Low
Perplexity AI Perplexity AI is an AI-powered search engine. Unlike traditional search engines that present a list of links, Perplexity delivers synthesized information in a natural language format, complete with citations and follow-up suggestions for more refined searches.

Pricing: Free and paid plans

How to Access: Perplexity.ai

 Low
Consensus Consensus is an academic search engine powered by AI. Unlike more generic AI search tools like Perplexity AI, Consensus searches peer-reviewed academic literature.

Pricing: Free and paid plans

How to Access: Consensus

 Low
OpenEvidence OpenEvidence is an AI-powered search engine developed by Harvard and MIT focused on medicine. It provides evidence-based summaries, direct links to research articles and clinical guidelines, and provides standard output formats for common clinical workflows. Use of any Yale personally identifiable data or protect health information is prohibited. 

Pricing: Free

How to Access:
OpenEvidence  

 Low
Doximity GPT Doximity GPT is an AI chatbot built on top of ChatGPT that focuses on clinical administrative workflows. It focuses on provider correspondence, such as chart notes, patient education materials, clinical references, letters of patient support, and insurance appeals. Use of any Yale personally identifiable data or protect health information is prohibited. 

Pricing: Free (for verified U.S. clinicians and medical students)

How to Access:
Doximity GPT

 Low
