“AI won’t take your job. It’s someone using AI who will take your job.” - Richard Baldwin, Professor of International Economics, International Institute for Management Development

Everyone is talking about how AI is reshaping the workforce, from business to healthcare, the arts, and beyond. As we move into this AI-driven world, students need more than just skills—they need to know how to use AI safely, ethically, and efficiently. This session introduces the essentials of generative AI, helping you tap into its power for your studies while respecting the boundaries that protect your academic integrity and privacy. Walk away with the skills to navigate this new landscape confidently, laying the groundwork for a lifetime of responsible and informed AI use.

This workshop adapts content created by our colleagues from Elon University and the American Association of Colleges and Universities. For more information see the Student Guide to Artificial Intelligence.

All sessions are free to GW students, faculty, staff, and alumni. GW has an institutional commitment to ensuring that all of our programs and events are accessible for all individuals. If you require any accommodations to participate in this event, please contact libraryevents@gwu.edu at least 72 business hours (3 business days) prior to the event.

In-person attendance of this workshop is open to anyone whose GWorld allows them to tap into Gelman Library. If you do not have access to Gelman, please attend online.
