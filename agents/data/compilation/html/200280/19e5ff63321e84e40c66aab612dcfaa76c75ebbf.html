AI and Human Innovation Showcase seeking project proposals

January 20, 2026

The University of North Dakota’s AI and Human Innovation Initiative is excited to announce its second annual AI and Human Innovation Showcase to be held at UND on March 20, 2026.

Proposals are sought for projects featuring new and developing models for how AI can be used to partner with human thought and endeavors in classroom activities, research, creative activities and community projects.

Showcase Focus:

Organizers are particularly interested in projects that focus on the partnership of AI and human innovation to enhance or initiate:

  • Human connection and/or the articulation of the human experience
  • Social impact or community change
  • Creative endeavors
  • Interdisciplinary problem-solving
  • Improved understanding of the human-AI interface

Eligible Projects:

We welcome submissions from:

  • Teaching/pedagogical initiatives
  • Research/scholarly activities projects
  • Creative endeavors
  • Community-related or outreach projects or programs
  • “Town and gown” collaborations
  • Interdisciplinary or cross-institutional projects

Projects can be at any stage of development from conceptual to fully implemented. Please indicate if AI is used to create the proposal or any part of the presentation. We seek to showcase a balance of disciplines and project types, and welcome submissions from faculty, staff, community members and students. Presenters must attend the showcase and present in person.

Presentation Formats:

Proposals may indicate preference for one of two formats:

  • Digital Exhibit which includes but is not limited to demonstrations, poster presentations, and interactive displays
  • AI in Action Lightning Round: Brief, engaging presentations, capped at five minutes*

*Only eight submissions will be selected for the Lightning Round and presentations will be strictly limited to 5 minutes, with speakers who exceed that time being muted.

Submission Guidelines:

Please submit a proposal that includes:

1. Project title

2. Presenter name(s) and affiliations(s)

3. Project abstract (max. 75 words)

4. Project description (max. 250 words)

5. Explanation of how the project addresses the showcase focus

6. Preferred presentation format (poster or lightening round)

Important Dates:

  • Submission deadline: Feb. 13, 2026
  • Notification of acceptance: Feb. 27, 2026
  • Showcase: March 20, 2026

Submission Process: Please submit your proposal online by Feb. 13, 2026.

Note: For those planning to attend, Early Bird Registration by March 2 will receive a free lunch – registration is free.

For questions or additional information, please contact Emily Cherry Oliver (emily.oliver@UND.edu)
