Workshops

The E. Kent & Barbara Culller Robinson Writing Center offers workshops throughout the semester. Below, you can find more information about what they offer.

Email 101: Unspoken Etiquette Among Professionals
This workshop reviews the basic components of a professionally crafted email and introduces students to some strategies for more effectively communicating with their professors and/or prospective employers via email.

Resume 101: Getting to the Top of the Pile
This workshop is designed to provide students with an overview of the basic components of resumes and to introduce them to four visual design principles.

Resume 102: Job Descriptions to Make Your Resume Stand Out
This workshop teaches students three guidelines for improving their work experience descriptions on their resumes. These descriptions should be: 1) Action-Oriented, 2) Results-Driven, and 3) Specific and Concise.

Cover Letter Bootcamp: How to Get Started and Get Noticed
This workshop covers the basic components of a cover letter and introduces students to strategies for making their letters more persuasive.
