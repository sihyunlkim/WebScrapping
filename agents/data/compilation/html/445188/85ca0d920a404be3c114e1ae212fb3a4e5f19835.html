How I AI: Innovation Meets Education

Series Showcasing How We are Utilizing AI in Graduate Education

AI is everywhere powering research, making exams (maybe?), and even helping faculty perfect their risotto recipes. In this biweekly series, we sit down with UC Merced faculty to uncover how they are using AI in their research, teaching, and yes, even their hobbies. From groundbreaking discoveries to accidental AI-fueled mishaps, expect insightful, entertaining, and maybe even slightly existential conversations. Because lets be honest whos teaching who at this point?

Stay tuned for stories of innovation, adaptationand the occasional AI-generated chaos.

P.S.: All images on this page have been generated, as is appropriate, by ChatGPT.

How I AI:A Political Scientist Uses AI to Decode Democracy featuring Amreeta Das (PhD student, Political Science)

When Amrita started her PhD in Political Science, she didnt expect to be training neural networks. But her path from fieldwork in Indian elections to coding deep-learning models shows how AI is reshaping the study of politics itself.Amrita applies AI not tocreatecampaign material but tostudyit using deep learning to analyze visual patterns in political imagery and generative AI to automate coding tasks once done by research assistants.

How I AI:Modeling Life, One Line of Code at a Time featuring Suraj Sahu (PhD student, Physics)

When Suraj talks about his research, he makes you see physics not as the study of lifeless particles, but as a living, breathing language that explains how cells organize themselves, how blood vessels form, and ultimately, how life takes shape. I study the physics aspects of biological systems, he explains. Right now, Im modeling how vascular systems develop in embryos, how those first blood vessels know where to grow.

How I AI: Modeling Movement, Teaching with Chatbots featuring Ranjan Das (PhD student, ME)

When Ranjan Das began his PhD in mechanical engineering at UC Merced, he found himself in new territory. His background was intribology and machine design drilling bits, wear-and-tear, the mechanics of contact. But his doctoral research would focus on something far more human:biomechanics and human gait analysis.
