Generative AI Primer

What is generative AI?

Generative AI (GAI) is a type of artificial intelligence that can generate text, images, videos, and other content in response to a user prompt, based on its training data.

This guide focuses on text-based AI generators. For information on image and video AI generators, see the Machines and Society guide.

Text-based generative AI: LLMs

Large language model (LLMs) are the foundation of GAI. LLMs are trained on vast amounts of text to understand existing content and generate original content. They function as chatbots, responding to user prompts by processing natural language in a conversational, human-like way. They can perform a variety of language-based tasks, like generating, summarizing, and translating text.

What are some of the LLMs out there?

  • ChatGPT: (OpenAI) Has free and fee-based options

  • Copilot: (Microsoft) Free

  • Gemini: (Google) Free to anyone with a Google account

  • Llama2: (Meta) Free for download

What types of things can ChatGPT and other LLMs do?

When asked what it is capable of doing, this is one way ChatGPT has answered:

  • Text generation: This refers to my ability to generate new text based on a given prompt or topic. I can use my understanding of language and my training data to produce sentences, paragraphs, or even longer pieces of text.
  • Text completion: This involves predicting and generating the most likely words or phrases to complete a given sentence or paragraph based on context.
  • Question-answering: I can answer questions by identifying the most relevant information from my training data and generating an appropriate response.
  • Summarization: I can summarize longer pieces of text into shorter summaries while retaining the main ideas and important details.
  • Text translation: I can translate text from one language to another, with varying levels of accuracy depending on the language pairs.
  • Conversational AI: This refers to my ability to engage in natural language conversations with humans, responding to prompts and questions in a conversational manner.
  • Sentiment analysis: I can analyze text to determine the overall sentiment or emotion expressed within it.
  • Named entity recognition: I can identify and extract named entities such as people, places, and organizations from text.
  • Part-of-speech tagging: I can identify the part of speech (noun, verb, adjective, etc.) of each word in a given sentence.

(ChatGPT, personal communication, March 14, 2023)

How do LLMs work, for example ChatGPT?

What is behind ChatGPT's mystique? This is one way that ChatGPT has responded to the question of how it works:

ChatGPT is a language model that utilizes a range of concepts, architectures, processes, and data inputs to perform its tasks. Here are some of the key elements that enable ChatGPT to work:

  1. Large language model: ChatGPT is a large language model that is designed to process and generate large amounts of natural language text. It is trained on massive amounts of text data, including books, articles, websites, the Common Crawl, Wikipedia, and other sources. These datasets provide the foundational knowledge that ChatGPT draws on when processing input text and generating responses.

  2. Artificial Neural Networks (ANNs): ANNs are mathematical models that simulate the way that the human brain processes information. They are used extensively in ChatGPT to process input text and generate responses.

  3. Natural Language Processing (NLP): This is the use of computational techniques to analyze and understand human language. ChatGPT uses NLP to process input text and generate responses.

  4. Transformer architecture: The transformer architecture is a type of neural network that is used in GPT models. It enables ChatGPT to process input text and generate responses in a way that is both efficient and effective.

  5. Generative Pre-trained Transformer (GPT): ChatGPT is based on GPT, a type of deep learning model specifically designed for natural language processing. GPT is trained on vast amounts of text data to learn the patterns and structures of language, enabling it to generate human-like responses to input prompts.

  6. Transfer learning: ChatGPT is trained on a vast corpus of text data that covers a wide range of topics and styles. Transfer learning enables ChatGPT to transfer knowledge learned in one domain to another related domain, allowing it to generate responses to a diverse range of input prompts.

  7. Reinforcement learning: ChatGPT uses reinforcement learning to improve its performance over time. This involves training it on a dataset of prompts and human-generated responses, and then adjusting its behavior based on feedback from humans.

  8. Attention mechanism: The attention mechanism is a component of the transformer architecture that allows ChatGPT to focus on specific parts of the input text when generating a response. This helps it to generate more accurate and relevant responses.

Overall, ChatGPT utilizes a complex combination of technologies and processes to analyze and understand human language, enabling it to generate responses that are similar to those produced by humans.

(ChatGPT, personal communication, March 14, 2023)

Example 2

User question: What is the most efficient cardio-vascular exercise?

ChatGPT answer: There is no single "most efficient" cardio-vascular exercise as different exercises offer different benefits and are suited for different individuals and fitness goals. However, some cardio exercises are generally considered more effective than others for improving cardiovascular fitness and burning calories.

Here are a few examples of popular cardio exercises that are generally considered effective:

  1. Running: Running is a great way to get your heart rate up and improve your cardiovascular fitness. It also burns a significant number of calories per minute and can be done indoors or outdoors.
  2. Cycling: Cycling is a low-impact exercise that can be done indoors or outdoors. It's a great way to work your legs and get your heart rate up, and can be adjusted to suit different fitness levels.
  3. Swimming: Swimming is a low-impact exercise that works your whole body and can improve cardiovascular fitness. It's also a great option for people with joint problems or injuries.
  4. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT): HIIT workouts involve short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest. These workouts can be very effective for improving cardiovascular fitness and burning calories in a short amount of time.

Ultimately, the most efficient cardio exercise for you will depend on your individual fitness level, goals, and preferences. It's important to choose an exercise that you enjoy and can stick to in the long term, as consistency is key to seeing results.

(ChatGPT, personal communication, March 13, 2023)
