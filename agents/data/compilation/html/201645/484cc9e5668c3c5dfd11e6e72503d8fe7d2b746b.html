UCITE AI in Teaching & Learning Workshops

New workshop dates have been added for Fall 2025!

Authors and fellow higher education colleagues José Antonio Bowen and C. Edward Watson advocated: “It is the job of educators to help students become better thinkers. Our new job is to help them become even better thinkers with AI” (2024, p. 77). Whether you think AI could play a supporting role in your students’ learning, or not... UCITE is excited to consider this process with you.


UCITE will offer three interactive workshops this semester, focused on Teaching and Learning with AI. These workshops are part of a larger effort by the Provost’s Office to integrate artificial intelligence with student learning. Visit the Provost’s new AI in Education website to learn more about campus-wide AI initiatives and resources.

 

Explore the Possibilities of AI to Enhance Your Teaching!

Are you ready to take your AI literacy to the next level and apply it directly to your teaching? The UCITE AI in Teaching & Learning Workshop Series offers hands-on, interactive sessions designed to help you leverage AI for course design, student engagement, and feedback.

These 90-minute, hybrid workshops provide practical, ready-to-use strategies for faculty who already have basic familiarity with AI tools. UCITE recommends that interested faculty complete the [U]Tech Teaching and Learning Technologies Canvas course, Getting Started with Generative AI in Education, or have some experience with LLMs and basic prompting tools before attending.

Attend one, two, or all three workshops -- each session is topically independent, so you can choose what fits your needs!

What You'll Do:

  • Experiment with AI tools in real time
  • Learn structured prompting ideas to get individualized results
  • Apply AI-generated ideas directly to one or more of your courses
  • Discuss practical and ethical applications of AI in education

What You'll Take Away:

  • AI-driven strategies for course design, assessment, and student engagement
  • Ready-to-use AI-enhanced learning activities
  • Ideas for guiding students in responsible AI use
  • Confidence in leveraging AI as a thought partner in teaching and learning

Bonus for In-Person Participants! Faculty who attend in-person sessions be eligible to receive a free copy of Teaching with AI: A practical guide to a new era of human learning by José Antonio Bowen and C. Edward Watson (2024).

 

Choose Your Workshops!

Workshop 1: Thinking with AI | Course Design and Planning
Discover how AI can help you brainstorm, structure, and refine course context. You'll use AI to generate learning outcomes, course themes, and dynamic syllabi that align with your goals.
Friday, September 12, 2025, 2:30 PM to 4:00 PM
Register for Workshop 1 on Campus Groups!
 

Workshop 2: Teaching with AI | Lesson Planning and Student Engagement
Explore AI's role in creating engaging learning activities, discussions, and assessments. Learn how to customize AI-generated content to fit your teaching style and student needs. Consider ways to AI-proof assignments when you want to ensure student cognitive independence.
Friday, September 26, 2025, 10:30 AM to 12:00 PM
Register for Workshop 2 on Campus Groups! 

 

Workshop 3: Learning with AI | Analyze Student Course Feedback, Personalize Student Learning, and Consider AI-Resistant Learning Spaces
See how AI can support your understanding of student learning through analysis of student course feedback. Creative activities to help students personalize their study practices. Consider situations in which you may want AI-Resistant learning spaces. Walk away with strategies to help students harness AI responsibly in their work.
Friday, October 10, 2025, 10:30 AM to 12:00 PM
Register for Workshop 3 on Campus Groups!
